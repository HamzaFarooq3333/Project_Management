#### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will consist of the following phases and activities, aligned with PRINCE2 principles:

I. **Project Initiation:**
   i. Define the project's scope, objectives, and deliverables [Ref 3, Ref 12].
   ii. Identify and analyze stakeholders, considering their interests and potential influence [Ref 1, Ref 7].
   iii. Establish project governance and communication channels.
   iv. Develop a project initiation document (PID), summarizing the project's purpose, scope, risks, and approach, including the defined life cycle [Ref 8, Ref 13].
   v. Conduct a feasibility study and obtain project authorization.

II. **Project Planning:**
   i. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
   ii. Define activities, dependencies, and estimate durations to create a project schedule.
   iii. Develop a budget and resource allocation plan.
   iv. Identify and assess project risks, developing mitigation strategies [Ref 9, Ref 10].
   v. Establish quality standards and control measures.
   vi. Develop a communication plan to manage stakeholder expectations [Ref 3].
   vii. Refine the PID based on the planning stage outcome.

III. **Project Execution:**
   i. Manage and coordinate project activities according to the project plan.
   ii. Monitor progress against the schedule, budget, and quality standards.
   iii. Manage project risks and issues, implementing mitigation plans.
   iv. Procure necessary resources and manage contracts (if applicable) [Ref 3].
   v. Manage changes through a formal change control process.
   vi. Keep the stakeholders informed about the project progress.

IV. **Project Monitoring and Controlling:**
   i. Regularly track and report on project performance, including progress, costs, and risks [Ref 1, Ref 3].
   ii. Analyze variances from the plan and take corrective actions.
   iii. Manage project issues and escalate as necessary.
   iv. Ensure the project adheres to quality standards and delivers its planned deliverables [Ref 8].
   v. Control all phases of the project [Ref 7].

V. **Project Closure:**
   i. Formally close out the project, ensuring all deliverables are accepted.
   ii. Obtain final stakeholder sign-off.
   iii. Document lessons learned for future projects.
   iv. Archive project documentation.
   v. Handover the project's deliverables.

#### Decision Gates

Important decision points during the project:

i.  **Project Initiation Approval:** Before proceeding to planning, the project sponsor must approve the Project Initiation Document (PID) and authorize the project.
ii. **Plan Approval:** The Project Manager presents the project plan to the Sponsor, Project Board, and Key Stakeholders for approval.
iii.  **Change Requests Approval:** Any changes to scope, schedule, or budget must be submitted as change requests and approved by the Change Authority [Ref 3].
iv.  **Stage Gate Reviews:** Periodic reviews to ensure project is on track in terms of deliverables and goals.
v.  **Project Closure Approval:** The Project Sponsor and key stakeholders must approve the project's completion, deliverables, and final report.
