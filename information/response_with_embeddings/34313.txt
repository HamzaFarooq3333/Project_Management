### Process Recommendation: Large-Scale IT Marketing Project (ISO Standards)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a framework for managing a large-scale Information Technology marketing project, adhering to ISO project management standards, and intended for projects exceeding 18 months. The framework leverages guidance from ISO 21502 and ISO 21500, and insights from the PMBOK Guide and PRINCE2, emphasizing adaptability and alignment with organizational context. The project will be structured into phases with defined activities, roles, and decision gates, incorporating stakeholder engagement, risk management, and change control. The methodology will be tailored to the specific project needs, considering its size, complexity, and the IT industry [Ref 2]. Consistent monitoring and control mechanisms will be implemented to ensure project success, focusing on value delivery and adapting to evolving project requirements [Ref 3, Ref 12].

-----

### Phases and Activities:

The project will be organized into the following phases, with key activities:

**I. Initiation:**

i. **Project Definition and Scope:** Define the project objectives, scope, and deliverables [Ref 1]. Conduct a detailed stakeholder analysis to identify key stakeholders and their expectations [Ref 5, Ref 7].
ii. **Feasibility Study:** Assess the project's feasibility, considering technical, financial, and market factors.
iii. **Project Charter Development:** Create a project charter that outlines the project's purpose, objectives, scope, and key stakeholders. Obtain sponsor approval [Ref 7].
iv. **Establish Communication Plan:** Develop a plan for ongoing and routine communication to all stakeholders [Ref 1].

**II. Planning:**

i. **Detailed Scope Definition:** Break down the project scope into smaller, manageable work packages using a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 5].
ii. **Schedule Development:** Create a detailed project schedule with realistic timelines and resource allocation [Ref 1, Ref 4].
iii. **Budget Development:** Develop a comprehensive project budget, considering all costs associated with resources, materials, and services [Ref 1].
iv. **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks and develop mitigation strategies to minimize their impact [Ref 14]. The organizations risk attitude will be considered.
v. **Resource Planning:** Define the resources required for the project, including personnel, equipment, and software.
vi. **Communication Plan Finalization:** Finalize the communication plan, specifying the frequency, format, and channels for project communication [Ref 1].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** If applicable, define the procurement strategy for acquiring goods and services from external vendors [Ref 1].

**III. Execution:**

i. **Task Execution:** Execute the tasks defined in the project schedule, following established procedures and quality standards.
ii. **Team Management:** Manage the project team, providing guidance, support, and motivation [Ref 13].
iii. **Stakeholder Engagement:** Maintain regular communication with stakeholders, providing updates on project progress and addressing their concerns [Ref 3, Ref 12].
iv. **Quality Assurance:** Implement quality assurance measures to ensure that project deliverables meet the required standards [Ref 12].
v. **Change Management:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget using a formal change control process [Ref 1, Ref 4].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling:**

i. **Performance Monitoring:** Track project performance against the project plan, identifying variances and taking corrective actions.
ii. **Risk Monitoring:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as needed [Ref 14]. Identify and address new risks that emerge during the project.
iii. **Change Control:** Manage change requests, assessing their impact and approving or rejecting them based on established criteria [Ref 1].
iv. **Communication Management:** Ensure that project communication is timely, accurate, and effective.
v. **Regular Reporting:** Provide regular reports to stakeholders on project progress, risks, and issues.

**V. Closure:**

i. **Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
ii. **Project Documentation:** Complete all project documentation, including project plans, reports, and lessons learned.
iii. **Final Reporting:** Prepare and distribute a final project report summarizing the project's outcomes, achievements, and lessons learned.
iv. **Project Closure Meeting:** Conduct a project closure meeting to celebrate the project's success and identify areas for improvement.
v. **Archiving:** Archive all project documents and data for future reference.

### Roles:

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall guidance and support for the project and secures necessary resources [Ref 7].
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project [Ref 13].
iii. **Project Team Members:** Contribute to the project by completing assigned tasks.
iv. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project's outcomes [Ref 5, Ref 7].

### Decision Gates:

i. **Phase Completion:** At the end of each phase (Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring & Controlling), a review will be conducted to assess progress and determine whether to proceed to the next phase. This will involve evaluating deliverables, addressing risks, and making necessary adjustments to the project plan.
ii. **Major Deliverable Acceptance:** Before final acceptance, each major deliverable must be reviewed and approved by stakeholders.
iii. **Change Request Approval:** All change requests that impact the project scope, schedule, or budget must be reviewed and approved by the project sponsor and key stakeholders [Ref 1].
iv. **Go/No-Go Decisions:** After each major milestone and/or significant external changes, make a determination on whether to proceed with the project based on the current conditions.
