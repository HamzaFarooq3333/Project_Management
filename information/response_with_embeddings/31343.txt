#### II. Phases and Activities

The following phases are recommended. Each phase includes key activities derived from best practices in project management, including PMBOK and ISO standards.

##### I. Initiation Phase

i. **Project Charter Development:** Define the project scope, objectives, stakeholders, high-level requirements, and project constraints [Ref 2, Ref 4, Ref 7].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify all stakeholders (persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in the project) and analyze their needs and expectations [Ref 3, Ref 7].
iii. **Risk Assessment (Initial):** Conduct an initial risk assessment to identify potential threats [Ref 9].
iv. **High-Level Planning:** Develop a preliminary schedule and budget.
v. **Project Governance Establishment:** Define project governance principles, policies, and procedures [Ref 13].

##### II. Planning Phase

i. **Detailed Requirements Gathering:** Document detailed software requirements based on stakeholder input.
ii. **Scope Definition and Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Create a detailed scope statement and decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
iii. **Schedule Development:** Develop a comprehensive project schedule, including task dependencies, resource allocation, and timelines [Ref 2].
iv. **Budgeting and Cost Management:** Develop a detailed budget and establish cost control procedures [Ref 2].
v. **Resource Planning:** Identify and secure necessary resources, including personnel, equipment, and software licenses [Ref 2].
vi. **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan to ensure effective information flow among stakeholders [Ref 2].
vii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a detailed risk management plan to identify, analyze, and mitigate potential risks [Ref 10].
viii. **Procurement Planning:** Develop a plan for procuring necessary goods and services [Ref 2].
ix. **Quality Management Planning:** Establish quality standards and procedures to ensure project deliverables meet requirements.

##### III. Execution Phase

i. **Software Development:** Execute the software development activities according to the planned schedule.
ii. **Resource Management:** Manage and allocate project resources effectively.
iii. **Change Management:** Implement change management processes to handle scope changes, ensuring proper documentation and approvals [Ref 2].
iv. **Communication Management:** Regularly communicate project status, issues, and risks to stakeholders.
v. **Risk Response Implementation:** Implement risk responses as outlined in the risk management plan.
vi. **Quality Assurance:** Conduct quality assurance activities throughout the development lifecycle.

##### IV. Monitoring and Controlling Phase

i. **Performance Monitoring:** Track project performance against the baseline plan (schedule, budget, and scope).
ii. **Progress Reporting:** Generate regular progress reports for stakeholders.
iii. **Issue and Risk Management:** Monitor issues and risks, implement corrective actions, and update the risk register.
iv. **Change Control:** Manage change requests, assess their impact, and obtain approvals.
v. **Earned Value Management (EVM):** Utilize EVM techniques to measure project performance and forecast future outcomes.
vi. **Measurement:** Continuously measure and analyze the project's performance [Ref 3].

##### V. Closing Phase

i. **Project Deliverables Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
ii. **Final Reporting:** Prepare a final project report summarizing project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations.
iii. **Contract Closure:** Close out any procurement contracts.
iv. **Knowledge Transfer:** Transfer project knowledge and documentation to the relevant stakeholders.
v. **Project Closure Activities:** Close the project officially, ensuring all administrative tasks are completed.

#### III. Roles

The following roles are essential for project success:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions [Ref 7].
*   **Project Manager:** Leads the project team and is responsible for managing the project to achieve its objectives [Ref 14].
*   **Business Analyst:** Gathers and documents business requirements.
*   **Software Developers:** Develop the software based on the requirements.
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Team:** Tests the software to ensure it meets quality standards.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with interests in the project [Ref 3, Ref 7].
*   **Steering Committee:** Provides guidance and oversight to the project, making key decisions.
