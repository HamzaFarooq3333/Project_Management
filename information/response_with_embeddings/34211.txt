### Process Recommendation: Medium-Sized IT Marketing Project (PMBOK)

#### Process Summary

This process recommendation outlines a project management framework for a medium-sized (6-18 months) marketing project within the Information Technology industry, aligning with the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK). The framework emphasizes stakeholder engagement, iterative planning, risk management, and continuous measurement [Ref 2]. We will utilize a structured approach that balances the predictive nature of PMBOK with the adaptability required in a marketing context [Ref 3]. The process focuses on delivering value through a collaborative team environment and diligent stewardship [Ref 12]. The project will be broken down into phases, each with defined activities, roles, and decision gates to ensure effective control and achievement of project objectives [Ref 6].

-----

#### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation:**
   i. **Define Project Scope & Objectives:** Clearly define the project's objectives, deliverables, and success criteria. This involves understanding the "what" and "why" of the project and its alignment with the overall business strategy [Ref 1].
   ii. **Identify Stakeholders:** Identify all individuals, groups, or organizations that may affect or be affected by the project [Ref 7]. Develop a stakeholder register to track their interests, influence, and communication requirements.
   iii. **Develop Project Charter:** Create a project charter that formally authorizes the project and documents the initial high-level project information [Ref 4]. This charter should include the project's purpose, objectives, key stakeholders, and high-level budget and schedule.
   iv. **Assemble Project Team:** Assemble a project team with the necessary skills and expertise to execute the project. Define clear roles and responsibilities for each team member [Ref 1].

II. **Planning:**
   i. **Develop Project Management Plan:** Create a comprehensive project management plan that outlines how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled. This plan should include sub-plans for scope, schedule, cost, communication, risk, and quality management [Ref 1].
   ii. **Define Scope Baseline:** Develop a detailed scope statement, work breakdown structure (WBS), and WBS dictionary to define the project's scope. The WBS breaks down the project deliverables into smaller, more manageable work packages [Ref 5].
   iii. **Develop Schedule Baseline:** Create a project schedule using a critical path method (CPM) or other scheduling technique. This schedule should identify the project's critical path, milestones, and dependencies.
   iv. **Develop Cost Baseline:** Develop a project budget that estimates the cost of all project activities. This budget should include direct costs, indirect costs, and contingency reserves.
   v. **Develop Risk Management Plan:** Identify potential project risks and develop mitigation strategies to minimize their impact. The risk management plan should include risk identification, analysis, response planning, and monitoring [Ref 15].
   vi. **Develop Communication Plan:** Establish a communication plan that outlines how project information will be communicated to stakeholders. This plan should define the frequency, format, and distribution methods for project communications.
   vii. **Develop Procurement Plan:** If the project requires external resources, develop a procurement plan to manage the acquisition of goods and services.

III. **Execution:**
   i. **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the project activities according to the project management plan. This involves coordinating team members, managing resources, and addressing issues as they arise.
   ii. **Implement Quality Assurance:** Implement quality assurance processes to ensure that the project deliverables meet the defined quality standards [Ref 12].
   iii. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Engage with stakeholders throughout the project to ensure their continued support and satisfaction [Ref 12].
   iv. **Manage Communications:** Disseminate project information to stakeholders according to the communication plan.

IV. **Monitoring & Controlling:**
   i. **Monitor Project Performance:** Track project performance against the project management plan baselines. This involves monitoring schedule, cost, and scope performance.
   ii. **Control Changes:** Implement change control processes to manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget. This includes evaluating change requests, approving changes, and updating the project management plan.
   iii. **Manage Risks:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as needed. Identify and respond to new risks that emerge during the project.
   iv. **Report Performance:** Provide regular project status reports to stakeholders.

V. **Closure:**
   i. **Close Project:** Formally close the project by obtaining acceptance of the final deliverables from the customer or sponsor.
   ii. **Conduct Post-Project Review:** Conduct a post-project review to identify lessons learned and best practices.
   iii. **Archive Project Documentation:** Archive all project documentation for future reference.
   iv. **Release Resources:** Release project team members and other resources.

#### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support for the project.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project.
*   **Marketing Team Lead:** Leads the marketing team and ensures the project delivers the marketing objectives.
*   **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):** Provides expertise in specific areas, such as content creation, social media, or SEO.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project's outcome.

#### Decision Gates

i. **Project Charter Approval:** After the initiation phase, the Project Sponsor must approve the project charter [Ref 4]. This decision gate ensures that the project is aligned with the organization's strategic goals and that adequate resources are committed.
ii. **Project Management Plan Approval:** After the planning phase, the Project Sponsor and key stakeholders must approve the project management plan. This decision gate ensures that the project plan is comprehensive and realistic.
iii. **Milestone Reviews:** At key project milestones, conduct reviews to assess project progress and identify any issues or risks. These reviews provide an opportunity to make course corrections and ensure that the project is on track.
iv. **Go/No-Go Decisions for Major Activities:** Before commencing major activities, such as a significant marketing campaign launch, a go/no-go decision should be made based on current market conditions and project performance.
