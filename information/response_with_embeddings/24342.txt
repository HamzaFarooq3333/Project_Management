#### 2. Phases and Activities

The project will be managed in distinct phases, with each phase having defined activities and deliverables.

**I. Project Initiation:**

   i. **Define the Project Scope:** Clearly define the project objectives, deliverables, and scope, ensuring alignment with organizational strategy and stakeholder needs [Ref 2]. This includes a clear understanding of the project's context, including factors like the finance industry and organizational maturity [Ref 14].
   ii. **Develop the Project Management Approach:** Select and tailor a suitable PRINCE2 approach. Define the project's lifecycle and choose appropriate management stages [Ref 9].
   iii. **Identify and Analyze Stakeholders:** Identify all stakeholders and assess their interests and influence. Develop a communication plan to manage stakeholder expectations [Ref 3, Ref 6].
   iv. **Create the Project Initiation Document (PID):** Compile all necessary project information into the PID. This document should include the project's justification, scope, objectives, risks, and proposed management approach, providing a baseline for the project [Ref 5].
   v. **Conduct Risk Assessment:** Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities) and develop initial risk mitigation plans [Ref 4, Ref 8, Ref 19].

**II. Project Planning:**

   i. **Detailed Scope Definition:** Break down the defined scope into manageable components using a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
   ii. **Activity Definition and Sequencing:** Define activities, estimate activity durations, and sequence activities to create a project schedule [Ref 2].
   iii. **Resource Planning:** Identify and allocate resources required for each activity.
   iv. **Budget Development:** Create a detailed project budget, considering all costs, including resources and procurement [Ref 2].
   v. **Communication Plan Development:** Refine and finalize the communication plan [Ref 2].
   vi. **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards and processes.
   vii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a comprehensive risk management plan, refining risk responses [Ref 11].
   viii. **Procurement Planning:** If applicable, develop a procurement plan [Ref 2].

**III. Project Execution and Monitoring & Controlling:**

   i. **Manage and Execute Activities:** Undertake planned activities, manage the project team, and deliver the project deliverables.
   ii. **Monitor Progress:** Track project progress against the baseline schedule and budget. Use performance measurement tools [Ref 2].
   iii. **Manage Risks:** Implement risk mitigation plans and monitor identified risks.
   iv. **Manage Issues:** Address and resolve project issues promptly and effectively.
   v. **Manage Change Requests:** Use a formal change management process to control scope changes [Ref 2].
   vi. **Quality Control:** Conduct quality assurance activities to ensure deliverables meet quality standards.
   vii. **Stakeholder Engagement:** Communicate with stakeholders and manage their expectations.

**IV. Project Closure:**

   i. **Project Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the relevant stakeholders.
   ii. **Final Project Review:** Conduct a final project review to evaluate project performance, identify lessons learned, and document them for future projects.
   iii. **Contract Closure:** Finalize any outstanding contracts and complete procurement processes.
   iv. **Document Archiving:** Archive project documentation, including the PID, schedule, budget, and all project reports.
   v. **Resource Release:** Release project resources and reassign staff as needed.

#### 3. Roles

   i. **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable project success [Ref 6]. Provides overall project direction and ensures the project aligns with business objectives.
   ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for day-to-day management of the project, including planning, executing, controlling, and closing the project.
   iii. **Project Team:** Individuals contributing to the project tasks and activities.
   iv. **Senior User:** Represents the interests of the users who will use the project's products or services.
   v. **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those designing, developing, facilitating, procuring, or implementing the products or services delivered by the project.
   vi. **Project Assurance:** Provides independent oversight and assurance to the Project Board on the project's progress and adherence to the PRINCE2 methodology.

#### 5. Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial points within the project lifecycle where decisions are made regarding project continuation.

   i.  **Project Initiation Phase Gate:** Before moving to planning, review the PID and the project’s justification, risks, and proposed management approach. The Project Board must approve the PID before the project proceeds [Ref 5].
   ii. **Planning Phase Gate:** Review the completed Project Plan (including scope, schedule, budget, risk management plan, and communication plan). The Project Board must approve the plan before execution commences [Ref 2].
   iii. **Stage End Gate:** At the end of each stage, assess progress against the stage plan and budget. The Project Board decides whether to authorize the next stage.
   iv. **Project Closure Phase Gate:** Conduct a final review of the project deliverables. The Project Board must formally accept the project and approve its closure [Ref 9].
