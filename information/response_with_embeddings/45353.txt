### 2. Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be structured into distinct phases, each with specific objectives, deliverables, and decision gates, promoting controlled progression and accountability.

#### I. Project Initiation Phase

This phase establishes the foundational elements of the project, ensuring alignment with organizational strategy and obtaining formal authorization.
   i.   **Define Project Objectives and Scope at a High Level**: Clearly articulate the "temporary endeavour to achieve one or more defined objectives" [Ref 6, Ref 12] for the education infrastructure.
   ii.  **Identify and Engage Key Stakeholders**: Identify all "person, group or organization that has interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of a project" [Ref 6, Ref 4]. Develop an initial stakeholder engagement plan, focusing on effective engagement [Ref 7].
   iii. **Appoint Project Sponsor**: Identify the "person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success" [Ref 6].
   iv.  **Formulate Project Charter**: Develop the document that formally authorizes the project, outlining its purpose, objectives, high-level requirements, and assigned Project Manager. This also establishes project governance principles [Ref 10].
   v.   **Perform Feasibility Studies and Business Case Development**: Assess the viability and justification for the infrastructure project, ensuring it focuses on value [Ref 7].

#### II. Project Planning Phase

This phase involves detailed elaboration of the project scope, schedule, budget, and resources, creating a comprehensive Project Management Plan (PMP). PMBOK explicitly details planning elements [Ref 3].
   i.   **Develop Project Management Plan (PMP)**: A comprehensive plan that integrates all subsidiary plans.
   ii.  **Define Product and Project Scope**: Clearly define "Product scope" and "Project scope," including deliverables [Ref 3, Ref 11]. Break down the defined scope using a "work breakdown structure decomposition" into progressively lower levels [Ref 4].
   iii. **Estimate, Schedule, and Budget**: Develop detailed "Estimating," "Scheduling," and "Budget" plans for all project activities and resources [Ref 3].
   iv.  **Resource Planning and Team Composition**: Define "Team composition" and roles, ensuring a collaborative project team environment [Ref 3, Ref 7].
   v.   **Risk Management Planning**: Identify potential threats [Ref 4, Ref 9] and opportunities. Develop strategies to "Optimize risk responses" [Ref 7, Ref 15], ensuring responses are appropriate and cost-effective [Ref 15].
   vi.  **Communication Management Planning**: Create a "Communication plan" outlining how information will be shared among stakeholders [Ref 3].
   vii. **Procurement Management Planning**: Develop "Procurement" strategies for acquiring necessary goods and services for the infrastructure [Ref 3].
   viii. **Quality Management Planning**: Define quality standards and processes, emphasizing building "quality into processes and deliverables" [Ref 7].
   ix.  **Change Management Planning**: Establish a "Change management" process to address modifications to the project plan [Ref 3].
   x.   **Measurement Planning**: Define key performance indicators and metrics for "Measurement" throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 1, Ref 3].

#### III. Project Execution Phase

This phase involves carrying out the planned work to achieve project objectives and produce deliverables. It embodies "Project Work" and "Delivery" [Ref 1].
   i.   **Direct and Manage Project Work**: Perform the activities defined in the PMP, guiding the project team and managing technical and organizational interfaces.
   ii.  **Acquire and Develop Project Team**: Mobilize and develop the project team, fostering a collaborative environment [Ref 7].
   iii. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement**: Continually "effectively engage with stakeholders" [Ref 7], managing expectations and fostering support.
   iv.  **Implement Procurement Activities**: Execute procurement contracts and manage supplier relationships [Ref 3].
   v.   **Perform Quality Assurance**: Implement planned quality activities to ensure processes and deliverables meet standards [Ref 7].
   vi.  **Construct and Deliver Infrastructure Components**: Oversee the physical construction and integration of education infrastructure components, leading to tangible deliverables [Ref 11].

#### IV. Project Monitoring & Controlling Phase

This phase runs concurrently with execution, tracking progress, measuring performance, and taking corrective actions to manage uncertainty [Ref 1].
   i.   **Monitor Project Work**: Track actual progress against the PMP, analyzing performance data and identifying variances. Utilize defined "Measurement" approaches [Ref 1, Ref 3].
   ii.  **Control Scope, Schedule, and Cost**: Manage changes to scope, schedule, and budget, ensuring approved changes are implemented and baseline integrity is maintained. Apply the "Change management" plan [Ref 3].
   iii. **Manage Risks and Issues**: Continuously identify, analyze, and respond to new and existing risks, including "threat" occurrences [Ref 4, Ref 9]. Implement "optimize risk responses" [Ref 7, Ref 15] and address emerging issues [Ref 11].
   iv.  **Control Quality**: Monitor specific project results to determine if they comply with quality standards.
   v.   **Monitor Stakeholder Engagement**: Assess the effectiveness of stakeholder engagement strategies and adjust as needed [Ref 7].
   vi.  **Manage Communication**: Ensure timely and appropriate collection, creation, distribution, storage, retrieval, and disposition of project information.
   vii. **Perform Integrated Change Control**: Review all change requests, approve changes, and manage changes to deliverables, organizational process assets, and the project management plan.

#### V. Project Closure Phase

This phase formally concludes all activities across all process groups to formally complete the project or phase.
   i.   **Close Project or Phase**: Finalize all activities, obtain formal acceptance of the final product, service, or result.
   ii.  **Conduct Post-Mortem Analysis/Lessons Learned**: Document successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
   iii. **Release Project Team**: Formally release project resources.
   iv.  **Archive Project Documentation**: Organize and store all project records for future reference and compliance.
   v.   **Final Financial Closure**: Close all financial accounts and contracts related to the project.

### 3. Roles

Key project roles are crucial for defining responsibilities and ensuring accountability throughout the project lifecycle:

i.   **Project Sponsor**: "Person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success" [Ref 6]. Provides high-level direction and support.
ii.  **Project Manager**: The individual responsible for coordinated activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 6]. Oversees all aspects of the project.
iii. **Project Governance Board/Steering Committee**: Provides strategic guidance, approves major decisions, and ensures alignment with organizational objectives. ISO 21502 defines "project governance principles, policies and procedures by which a project is authorized and directed" [Ref 10].
iv.  **Core Project Team**: Individuals who perform the work to produce the project's deliverables. They contribute to creating a collaborative project team environment [Ref 7].
v.   **Key Stakeholders**: "Person, group or organization that has interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of a project" [Ref 6]. This includes end-users (students, faculty), regulatory bodies, community representatives, and contractors. Effective engagement with stakeholders is vital [Ref 7].
vi.  **Functional Managers**: Provide resources and expertise from their respective departments.
vii. **Quality Assurance/Control Lead**: Ensures adherence to quality standards and processes.

### 5. Decision Gates

Decision gates are formal points at the end of each project phase where a go/no-go decision is made, based on a review of performance, deliverables, and updated plans. These gates reinforce project governance [Ref 10] and ensure the project aligns with its defined objectives [Ref 12].

i.   **Gate 1: Project Authorization (End of Initiation Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To formally authorize the project to proceed to detailed planning.
    *   **Review**: Project Charter, initial business case, high-level scope, and identified key stakeholders.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor and Project Governance Board [Ref 10] approve the Project Charter and allocate initial budget for planning.

ii.  **Gate 2: Plan Approval (End of Planning Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To ensure all planning is complete, comprehensive, and acceptable before execution.
    *   **Review**: Approved Project Management Plan (PMP), including detailed scope, schedule, budget, risk management plan [Ref 3], and procurement strategy.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor and Project Governance Board [Ref 10] approve the PMP, allowing the project to move into execution.

iii. **Gate 3: Mid-Project Review/Phase Transition (Mid-Execution Phase, if applicable for large projects)**
    *   **Purpose**: To assess significant progress, re-evaluate project viability, and make necessary adjustments for subsequent execution stages.
    *   **Review**: Current performance against baselines, earned value analysis, updated risk register [Ref 15], stakeholder satisfaction, and upcoming major deliverables.
    *   **Decision**: Project Governance Board [Ref 10] decides on continued investment and course corrections.

iv.  **Gate 4: Pre-Closure Review (End of Execution/Start of Closure Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To verify that all planned work is completed to specifications and the project objectives are met.
    *   **Review**: Verification of all deliverables, final quality reports, resolution of all open issues [Ref 11], and acceptance by key stakeholders [Ref 7].
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor and Project Manager confirm readiness for formal closure.

v.   **Gate 5: Project Final Acceptance & Closure (End of Closure Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To formally close the project and acknowledge the successful delivery of objectives.
    *   **Review**: Final project report, lessons learned, formal sign-off from all relevant stakeholders, and financial closure.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor and Project Governance Board [Ref 10] grant final project closure.
