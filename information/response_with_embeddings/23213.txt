### Process Recommendation: IT Research Project

This process recommendation outlines a structured approach for managing a medium-scale information technology research project, adhering to ISO standards and drawing upon the PMBOK and PRINCE2 methodologies where relevant. The project, expected to last between 6-18 months, requires a comprehensive framework to ensure successful delivery of research objectives [Ref 2]. This framework emphasizes the application of methods, tools, techniques, and competencies to meet project objectives, while recognizing and addressing potential challenges [Ref 3]. The project will leverage key PM domains like Planning, Stakeholder Management, and Delivery.

#### I. Project Initiation and Planning

This phase defines the project's scope, objectives, and initial plan. This directly aligns with the planning aspect highlighted in the PMBOK [Ref 1, Ref 4].

i.  **Initiation:**
    *   Define project goals and objectives. [Ref 6] states that projects are "temporary endeavors to achieve one or more defined objectives."
    *   Identify key stakeholders and their interests [Ref 5, Ref 6].
    *   Develop a Project Charter outlining the project's purpose, scope, and high-level deliverables.
    *   Secure sponsor approval and commitment [Ref 6].
    *   Develop a high-level communication plan [Ref 1].

ii. **Planning:**
    *   Define the project scope in detail. The work breakdown structure (WBS) will be used to decompose the scope [Ref 5].
    *   Create a detailed project schedule, estimating task durations and dependencies. Consider the project life cycle [Ref 10].
    *   Develop a budget, including resource allocation and cost estimates [Ref 1].
    *   Identify and assess potential risks and develop a risk management plan. This includes defining mitigation strategies for threats [Ref 5, Ref 13].
    *   Develop a detailed communication plan [Ref 1].
    *   Determine team composition and roles [Ref 1].
    *   Define procurement needs, if any [Ref 1].
    *   Establish change management processes [Ref 1].
    *   Create a quality management plan.

-----

#### II. Execution and Monitoring & Controlling

This phase involves executing the planned activities, monitoring progress, and controlling deviations from the plan. Project Work is a key domain in PMBOK [Ref 4].

i.  **Execution:**
    *   Execute the tasks defined in the project plan.
    *   Manage and coordinate project team members.
    *   Procure necessary resources.
    *   Conduct regular project meetings to track progress and address issues.
    *   Manage stakeholders expectations.

ii.  **Monitoring and Controlling:**
    *   Track project progress against the schedule, budget, and scope. This covers Measurement [Ref 1, Ref 4].
    *   Monitor risks and implement mitigation plans.
    *   Manage issues and changes, using the change management process.
    *   Control project costs.
    *   Manage communication and stakeholder relationships.
    *   Regularly update the project plan as needed.

-----

#### III. Project Delivery & Closure

This phase focuses on the successful delivery of project deliverables and formal project closure.

i.  **Deliverables:**
    *   Ensure all deliverables meet the defined quality standards [Ref 11].
    *   Obtain stakeholder acceptance of the deliverables.

ii.  **Project Closure:**
    *   Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the sponsor and stakeholders.
    *   Conduct a project post-implementation review to capture lessons learned. This ensures the project context is considered [Ref 7].
    *   Close out contracts, if any [Ref 1].
    *   Archive project documentation.
    *   Formally close the project.

#### IV. Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for securing resources, executive decisions, and overall project success [Ref 6].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, and closing the project, managing resources, and motivating the team [Ref 14].
*   **Project Team:** Individuals assigned to perform the project tasks.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or organizations who have an interest in the project. [Ref 5, Ref 6].
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Team/Individual**: Responsible for checking quality standards are met for project deliverables.

#### VI. Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points where project decisions are made, often requiring stakeholder or sponsor approval.

*   **Project Charter Approval:** (At the end of Initiation)  This is where the project gets officially authorized, ensuring resources and support are in place [Ref 6].
*   **Project Plan Approval:** (At the end of Planning) Sign-off on the detailed project plan, including schedule, budget, and risk management plan.
*   **Deliverable Acceptance:** (Throughout Execution and Delivery)  Formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders ensures quality and alignment with objectives [Ref 11].
*   **Project Closure Approval:** (At the end of Closure) Formal approval of project closure, signifying completion and acceptance of all deliverables.
