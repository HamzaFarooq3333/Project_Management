### Process Recommendation for Small Healthcare Construction Project (PRINCE2)

**1. Process Summary**

This process recommendation outlines a project management approach for a small healthcare construction project (under 6 months), leveraging the PRINCE2 methodology and aligned with PMBOK [Ref 1] and ISO 21502 [Ref 3] standards. The process prioritizes stakeholder engagement [Ref 11], a structured planning phase [Ref 2], risk management [Ref 10], and continuous monitoring and control to ensure successful delivery of the project objectives within the healthcare context. Healthcare projects require specific attention to regulatory compliance and stakeholder (patients, medical staff) needs, which will be integrated into the overall process. The project will utilize a tailored PRINCE2 approach [Ref 7], selecting and adapting processes to fit the project's size and complexity. This plan accounts for typical challenges that can occur such as ambiguity regarding responsibilities [Ref 5] and unrealistic expectations [Ref 5].

-----

**2. Phases and Activities**

The project will be structured into the following PRINCE2 phases:

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU)**:
    i. Define the project mandate and objectives.
    ii. Identify key stakeholders [Ref 1].
    iii. Develop a project brief outlining the project's scope, objectives, and constraints.
    iv. Assess project risks and opportunities [Ref 10].
    v. Create the Project Management Team, and define the Project Board.
    vi. Prepare the Initiation Stage Plan.

II. **Initiating a Project (IP)**:
    i. Develop the Project Initiation Document (PID). This includes detailed plans for project scope [Ref 2], schedule, budget, quality, communication, and risk management.
    ii. Refine the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
    iii. Establish project controls, including change management processes [Ref 2].
    iv. Agree on roles and responsibilities.
    v. Finalize risk responses, making them appropriate for the significance of the risks, cost effective and agreed to by stakeholders [Ref 10].

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS)**:
    i. Authorizing work packages.
    ii. Monitoring progress against the stage plan.
    iii. Reviewing the status of risks and issues.
    iv. Taking corrective actions to address deviations from the plan.
    v. Escalating issues to the Project Board as necessary.
    vi. Ensuring risks are addressed continually [Ref 10].

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP)**:
    i. Executing work packages and creating deliverables [Ref 8].
    ii. Ensuring quality standards are met.
    iii. Obtaining acceptance of deliverables from stakeholders.

V. **Managing Stage Boundary (SB)**:
    i. Reviewing stage performance.
    ii. Updating the Project Plan.
    iii. Creating the next Stage Plan, including updated risk assessments and resource forecasts.
    iv. Reporting stage performance to the Project Board.

VI. **Closing a Project (CP)**:
    i. Verify that all project objectives have been met.
    ii. Obtain formal acceptance of the final deliverables.
    iii. Disseminate project results and lessons learned.
    iv. Archive project documentation.
    v. Perform a post-project review.

**3. Roles**

i. **Executive:** Represents the interests of the project sponsor and ensures the project aligns with the organizational strategy [Ref 13].
ii. **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's outputs, in this case, likely medical staff and patients.
iii. **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those providing the resources and expertise to deliver the project.
iv. **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project, ensuring it's delivered on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards [Ref 11].
v. **Project Assurance:** Provides independent oversight to ensure the project is being managed effectively and that risks are being appropriately managed.
vi. **Team Manager:** Leads and manages the project team responsible for delivering specific work packages [Ref 4].

**5. Decision Gates**

i. **End of Starting Up a Project (SU):** The Project Board reviews and approves the Project Brief and the Initiation Stage Plan, authorizing the initiation stage.
ii. **End of Initiating a Project (IP):** The Project Board reviews and approves the Project Initiation Document (PID), including the project plan, risk management plan, and other key documents, authorizing the project to proceed.
iii. **End of Each Stage (SB):** The Project Board reviews the stage performance and the next stage plan, deciding whether to continue, modify, or terminate the project. This includes reviewing stage performance [Ref 10].
iv. **Project Closure (CP):** The Project Board reviews the final deliverables and project performance, formally closing the project and authorizing the dissemination of results.
