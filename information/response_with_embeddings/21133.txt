### Phases and Activities

#### I. Project Initiation
This phase establishes the project's purpose, objectives, and initial scope, ensuring foundational alignment before significant investment.

i.  **Define Project Purpose and Objectives:** Clearly articulate what the project aims to achieve, in line with organizational goals.
ii. **Identify Key Stakeholders:** Systematically identify all persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, or be affected by, the project [Ref 4, Ref 11]. This includes end-users, regulatory bodies, and internal management.
iii. **Establish Project Governance:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project will be authorized and directed [Ref 12, Ref 13]. This includes roles, responsibilities, and decision-making authorities.
iv. **Develop Project Charter:** Document the project's purpose, objectives, scope, high-level requirements, and assigned Project Manager, and obtain authorization from the Project Sponsor [Ref 11].
v. **High-Level Risk Identification:** Conduct an initial assessment of potential threats and opportunities to the project's objectives [Ref 8, Ref 9, Ref 15].

#### II. Planning
This phase elaborates on the project charter, detailing how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed, ensuring all aspects are thoroughly considered.

i.  **Scope Definition (Product & Project):** Clearly define both the product scope (features of the software) and the project scope (work required to deliver the product). This involves decomposing the defined scope into a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4] and defining work packages.
ii. **Estimating and Scheduling:** Develop detailed estimates for effort, cost, and duration, leading to a comprehensive project schedule [Ref 2]. Given the small scale, this should be pragmatic but robust.
iii. **Resource Planning:** Determine required team composition, identify necessary resources, and plan for their acquisition and allocation [Ref 2].
iv. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop strategies for identifying, assessing, and controlling uncertainties that could affect project objectives [Ref 8, Ref 15]. This includes planning for appropriate, cost-effective, and realistic risk responses [Ref 8].
v. **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards and activities specific to healthcare software development, embedding quality into processes and deliverables from the outset [Ref 6]. This ensures the software meets functional, security, and regulatory requirements.
vi. **Communication Planning:** Establish how information will be shared among stakeholders and the project team [Ref 2].
vii. **Change Management Planning:** Define the process for collecting, assessing, and controlling changes to the project's baselines (scope, schedule, cost) [Ref 2, Ref 15].
viii. **Procurement Planning (if applicable):** If external vendors or software components are required, plan for their acquisition and management [Ref 2].
ix. **Measurement Planning:** Define metrics and mechanisms to monitor and compare actual achievements against planned objectives [Ref 3, Ref 15].

#### III. Execution & Control
This phase involves carrying out the planned work, monitoring progress, managing changes, and ensuring the project stays on track to deliver its objectives. Given the software development context, an iterative or adaptive approach within this phase is recommended [Ref 1].

i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the activities defined in the project plan, performing the actual project work to create deliverables [Ref 3].
ii. **Team Management and Collaboration:** Create a collaborative project team environment [Ref 6], ensuring effective engagement and motivation.
iii. **Stakeholder Engagement:** Continuously engage with stakeholders, managing expectations and ensuring their involvement throughout the project [Ref 3, Ref 6].
iv. **Develop and Build Software:** Conduct software design, coding, integration, and testing activities, delivering unique and verifiable elements [Ref 13].
v. **Monitor and Control Progress:** Regularly track actual achievements against the project plan, reporting on performance and forecasting future progress [Ref 15].
vi. **Manage Risks and Issues:** Continuously identify, analyze, and respond to individual and overall project risks [Ref 8, Ref 15]. Address issues that arise during the project lifecycle promptly [Ref 15].
vii. **Perform Quality Assurance:** Implement planned quality activities and processes to ensure deliverables meet defined standards [Ref 6].
viii. **Manage Changes:** Process change requests according to the defined change management plan, ensuring baselines are updated as required [Ref 2, Ref 15].
ix. **Report Performance:** Communicate project status, progress, and forecasts to relevant stakeholders as per the communication plan.

#### IV. Project Closure
This final phase formally completes the project, ensuring all deliverables are accepted, administrative tasks are finalized, and lessons learned are documented.

i.  **Final Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the final software product from the customer and relevant stakeholders [Ref 3].
ii. **Administrative Closure:** Complete all contractual obligations, release project resources, and archive project documentation.
iii. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a post-project review to capture lessons learned, identifying successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
iv. **Final Project Report:** Document the project's performance against objectives and key metrics.

### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor (S):** Person responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions to enable project success [Ref 11]. Provides high-level direction and removes impediments.
*   **Project Manager (PM):** Responsible for the application of methods, tools, techniques, and competencies to enable the project to meet its objectives [Ref 5]. Overall coordination and management of the project lifecycle [Ref 7].
*   **Development Lead (DL):** Leads the technical development team, overseeing design, coding, and technical implementation.
*   **Healthcare Subject Matter Expert (SME):** Provides expertise on clinical workflows, regulatory requirements (e.g., HIPAA, GDPR for data privacy), and specific healthcare domain knowledge.
*   **Quality Assurance / Tester (QA):** Responsible for ensuring the quality of deliverables through testing and quality assurance processes.
*   **Development Team Member (DTM):** Responsible for executing specific development tasks as assigned.
*   **Key Stakeholders (KS):** Individuals or groups affected by or affecting the project, including end-users, IT operations, and regulatory contacts [Ref 4, Ref 11].

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project lifecycle where formal reviews occur, and a decision is made to proceed to the next phase, re-plan, or terminate the project.

i.  **Project Authorization Gate (End of Initiation):**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm the project's viability and strategic alignment.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go to Planning Phase. The Project Sponsor provides formal authorization based on the Project Charter [Ref 11]. Project governance principles ensure proper authorization and direction [Ref 12].
    *   **Output:** Signed Project Charter.

ii. **Baseline Approval Gate (End of Planning):**
    *   **Purpose:** To approve the detailed project plan (scope, schedule, budget, resources, risks, quality) before execution begins.
    *   **Decision:** Proceed with Execution or revise the plan. Key stakeholders, including the Project Sponsor, review and approve the comprehensive plan elements [Ref 2].
    *   **Output:** Approved Project Management Plan.

iii. **Iteration/Phase Completion Review (During Execution & Control):**
    *   **Purpose:** For an iterative software development approach, these regular reviews assess progress, delivered increments, and address issues or changes [Ref 15].
    *   **Decision:** Continue to next iteration, re-plan current iteration, or escalate. These occur at regular intervals, based on the measurement plan [Ref 3, Ref 15].
    *   **Output:** Accepted iteration increment, updated backlog/plan.

iv. **Final Acceptance & Closure Gate (End of Closure):**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm that all project objectives have been met, deliverables are accepted, and the project can be formally closed.
    *   **Decision:** Project closure. Formal acceptance by the customer and Project Sponsor, confirming that objectives are achieved and deliverables produced [Ref 3, Ref 13].
    *   **Output:** Project Closure Report, Formal Acceptance Sign-off.
