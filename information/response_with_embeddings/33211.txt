### Process Recommendation: Medium-Sized IT Research Project (PMBOK)

This process recommendation outlines a structured approach for managing a medium-sized information technology research project, expected to last between 6-18 months, and is aligned with the PMBOK methodology. This recommendation incorporates key principles from PMBOK, ISO 21500/21502, and PRINCE2 standards to ensure project success.

#### Process Summary

This process recommendation focuses on the application of project management methods, tools, techniques, and competencies to enable the project to meet its objectives [Ref 3]. The project will follow a predictive approach, but with opportunities for incremental delivery and adaptation as research progresses. Key areas include stakeholder management, team management, a well-defined development approach and life cycle, meticulous planning, effective execution of project work, timely delivery of results, robust measurement of progress, and proactive management of uncertainty [Ref 4]. This recommendation offers guidance on project management practices that are important for the project's successful delivery [Ref 12].

#### Phases and Activities

The project will be divided into the following phases:

I. **Initiation:**
  i. Define project objectives, scope, and deliverables [Ref 5].
  ii. Identify key stakeholders and their expectations [Ref 6].
  iii. Develop a project charter and secure approval from the sponsor [Ref 6].
  iv. Establish the project governance structure and communication channels.

II. **Planning:**
  i. Develop a detailed project management plan [Ref 1]. This includes:
   a. Defining the product scope and project scope [Ref 1].
   b. Estimating resources, timelines, and costs [Ref 1].
   c. Creating a project schedule [Ref 1].
   d. Defining the team composition [Ref 1].
   e. Developing a communication plan [Ref 1].
   f. Establishing procurement processes [Ref 1].
   g. Creating a change management plan [Ref 1].
   h. Defining measurement criteria and KPIs [Ref 1].
  ii. Conduct a risk assessment and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 5].
  iii. Define the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 5].

III. **Execution:**
  i. Execute the project plan according to the defined schedule and budget.
  ii. Manage the project team and resources effectively.
  iii. Implement the communication plan to keep stakeholders informed [Ref 1].
  iv. Manage project risks and issues proactively [Ref 11].
  v. Track project progress against the baseline plan using key performance indicators (KPIs) [Ref 1].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:**
  i. Monitor project performance against the project management plan.
  ii. Control scope, schedule, and cost variances.
  iii. Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget using the change management process [Ref 1].
  iv. Regularly report project status to stakeholders.
  v. Address issues and risks promptly.

V. **Closure:**
  i. Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
  ii. Document lessons learned.
  iii. Archive project documents and records.
  iv. Release project resources.
  v. Conduct a final project review and evaluation.

#### Roles

Key roles within the project include:

*   **Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, resources, and support for the project [Ref 6].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing the project.
*   **Research Lead:** Responsible for the technical aspects of the research project, including methodology and results.
*   **Team Members:** Contribute to the project by completing assigned tasks and activities.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project or its outcomes [Ref 6].

#### Decision Gates

The following are key decision gates throughout the project lifecycle:

I. **Project Initiation Approval:** Approval of the project charter by the sponsor to formally initiate the project.
II. **Project Management Plan Approval:** Approval of the detailed project management plan by key stakeholders before the execution phase begins [Ref 1].
III. **Phase Gate Review (Midpoint):** A review of project progress, risks, and issues at the midpoint of the project to determine whether to continue to the next phase.
IV. **Final Deliverable Acceptance:** Formal acceptance of the project deliverables by the stakeholders at the end of the project.
