### Project Management Process Recommendation: Large-Scale Education Research Project (PMBOK Aligned)

**Process Summary:** This process outlines the project management approach for a large-scale education research project, adhering to PMBOK principles, and guided by ISO21502 for general project management guidance [Ref 1, Ref 7]. Due to the project's large size (>18 months), a phased approach with robust planning, stakeholder engagement [Ref 5], risk management [Ref 12] and change control processes [Ref 6] are crucial. The project should also embrace adaptability and resiliency [Ref 9]. This includes detailed planning, execution, monitoring and controlling, and closure phases. Effective communication and collaboration are paramount throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 2].

-----

### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation:**

   i. Define Project Objectives and Scope: Clearly articulate the research goals, objectives, and boundaries of the project. Conduct preliminary stakeholder analysis [Ref 5] to identify key individuals and groups affected by or influencing the project.
   ii. Develop Project Charter: Document the project's purpose, scope, objectives, deliverables, and key stakeholders. Obtain formal approval from the sponsor [Ref 5].
   iii. Identify Key Stakeholders: Identify and document all stakeholders and their interests [Ref 5].

II. **Planning:**

   i. Develop Project Management Plan: Create a comprehensive plan outlining how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled [Ref 6]. This includes defining scope, schedule, budget, resources, communication, risk management, and quality management plans.
   ii. Define Scope: Break down the project deliverables into smaller, manageable tasks using a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
   iii. Create Schedule: Develop a detailed project schedule with tasks, dependencies, milestones, and resource assignments.
   iv. Develop Budget: Estimate the costs associated with each project activity and develop a budget.
   v. Develop Risk Management Plan: Identify potential risks, assess their probability and impact, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 4, Ref 12].
   vi. Develop Communication Plan: Define how project information will be communicated to stakeholders, including frequency, methods, and responsibilities [Ref 6].

III. **Execution:**

   i. Execute Project Tasks: Carry out the activities defined in the project schedule.
   ii. Manage Project Team: Provide leadership, guidance, and support to the project team. Foster a collaborative and productive team environment [Ref 9].
   iii. Manage Communication: Implement the communication plan to ensure stakeholders are informed of project progress and issues.
   iv. Implement Risk Response Plans: Take action to mitigate risks as they arise [Ref 12].
   v. Manage Deliverables: Ensure that project deliverables are produced according to the defined quality standards [Ref 11].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:**

   i. Monitor Project Progress: Track project performance against the project management plan, schedule, and budget.
   ii. Control Scope: Manage changes to the project scope through a formal change control process [Ref 6].
   iii. Control Schedule: Monitor schedule performance and take corrective action as needed to keep the project on track.
   iv. Control Costs: Monitor budget performance and manage costs to stay within the approved budget.
   v. Manage Issues: Identify, document, and resolve project issues.
   vi. Report Performance: Regularly report project performance to stakeholders.

V. **Closure:**

   i. Obtain Project Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the sponsor and stakeholders.
   ii. Document Lessons Learned: Document the successes and challenges of the project to improve future projects.
   iii. Close Project: Finalize all project documentation, archive project records, and release project resources.

-----

### Roles

i. Project Sponsor: Provides overall direction and support for the project, secures funding, and approves key decisions [Ref 5].
ii. Project Manager: Leads and manages the project team, develops and executes the project management plan, and ensures the project is completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards [Ref 13, Ref 14].
iii. Research Team Members: Conduct research activities, analyze data, and produce research reports.
iv. Stakeholders: Provide input and feedback on the project and ensure it meets their needs [Ref 5].
