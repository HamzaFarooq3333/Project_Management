### Phases and Activities

The project will follow a phased approach, reflecting the PRINCE2 methodology, with specific activities for each phase.

I.  **Project Initiation Phase:** This phase establishes the foundation for the project.

    i.  **Define Project Objectives and Scope:** Clearly define the research objectives, scope, and deliverables, aligning them with stakeholder needs [Ref 3]. This includes creating a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
    ii. **Identify Stakeholders:** Identify all stakeholders involved in or affected by the project, including their interests and influence [Ref 7].
    iii. **Develop Project Management Approach:** This includes selecting a project life cycle that aligns with the project deliverables [Ref 2, Ref 8].
    iv.  **Develop Project Plan:** Create a comprehensive project plan including the project schedule, budget, risk management strategy, communication plan, and resource allocation [Ref 3].
    v.  **Approve Project Initiation Document (PID):** Obtain formal approval of the PID, which encompasses all of the above, from the Project Board.

II. **Project Planning and Configuration Phase:** This phase will focus on configuring the manual aspects of the project.

    i.  **Detailed Research Planning:** Break down the project into manageable work packages, assigning responsibilities and deadlines [Ref 4].
    ii.  **Resource Allocation:** Secure the necessary resources (personnel, tools, funding) and assign them to tasks [Ref 3].
    iii. **Configure Research Environment:** Setting up the manual research environment as required. This involves configuring tools and systems, identifying data sources.
    iv.  **Risk Assessment and Management:** Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities), assess their impact, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 3, Ref 10, Ref 19].
    v.  **Develop Communication Plan:** Define how project information will be communicated to stakeholders [Ref 3].
    vi. **Quality Planning:** Identify quality standards and determine how quality will be ensured throughout the project lifecycle.

III. **Project Execution Phase:** This is where the research and configuration work is performed.

    i.  **Execute Research Activities:** Carry out the research tasks as defined in the project plan [Ref 2].
    ii. **Manage and Monitor Configuration:** This focuses on the manual configuration to ensure the environment is performing as expected.
    iii.  **Manage Project Team:** Lead and manage the project team, providing guidance and support [Ref 11].
    iv.  **Monitor Progress:** Track project progress against the plan, using Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to measure performance [Ref 2].
    v.  **Manage Issues and Changes:** Implement the change management process to handle any deviations from the plan, issues, or changes that arise [Ref 3].
    vi.  **Communication and Reporting:** Regularly communicate project status and any issues to stakeholders according to the communication plan [Ref 3].

IV. **Project Delivery and Measurement Phase:** This phase focuses on quality and results.

    i. **Deliver Project Outputs:** Deliver the final research outputs.
    ii.  **Testing and Validation:** Validate research outputs to ensure they meet quality requirements.
    iii.  **Performance Measurement:** Measure and assess the project's performance, including time, cost, and scope against the established baseline [Ref 2].
    iv. **Finalize Deliverables:** Prepare final research reports, presentations, and any other deliverables.
    v. **Obtain Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the final deliverables from the Project Board.

V. **Project Closure Phase:** This phase closes down the project.

    i. **Project Closure Activities:** Conduct lessons learned sessions to identify what went well and what could be improved in future projects.
    ii.  **Document Lessons Learned:** Document project outcomes, including achievements, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
    iii.  **Finalize Financial Closure:** Settle all outstanding financial matters.
    iv.  **Archive Project Documentation:** Archive all project documentation.
    v.  **Project Board Sign-off:** Obtain formal sign-off from the Project Board to confirm project closure.

### Roles

Key project roles and responsibilities are:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 7].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project, ensuring the project meets its objectives [Ref 7].
*   **Project Team:** Responsible for carrying out the project tasks.
*   **Project Board:** Provides direction and approval, including the Project Sponsor, Senior User, and Senior Supplier [Ref 5, Ref 9].
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by or having an interest in the project's outcome [Ref 7].
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Team/Role:** Ensures that the project deliverables meet the required quality standards.

### Decision Gates

Key decision gates will ensure project governance and control. The Project Board, fulfilling governance responsibility, will make the final decision.

1.  **Project Initiation Document (PID) Approval:** Formal approval to proceed with the project, which is a key decision point [Ref 7, Ref 9].
2.  **Project Plan Approval:** Ensure the plan meets requirements.
3.  **Phase End Reviews:** Reviews at the end of each phase to assess performance, review deliverables, and approve the next phase. [Ref 9]
4.  **Change Requests Approval/Rejection:** Decision on requested changes, their impact, and associated cost/schedule implications.
5.  **Final Deliverables Acceptance:** Formal acceptance of deliverables, indicating successful completion and meeting project objectives [Ref 8].
6.  **Project Closure Approval:** Formal approval to close the project [Ref 9].
