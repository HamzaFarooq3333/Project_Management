### Process Summary

This process recommendation outlines a comprehensive project management approach for a large-scale construction project exceeding 18 months, adhering to ISO standards. The project management approach emphasizes adherence to ISO standards, stakeholder engagement, and a focus on delivering value while adapting to changes [Ref 11]. It integrates key principles from both PMBOK and PRINCE2, emphasizing a structured yet flexible approach to managing the project lifecycle, from initiation through closure [Ref 6, Ref 8, Ref 13]. This plan incorporates rigorous planning, monitoring, and control mechanisms to mitigate risks and ensure project objectives are met within budget and schedule.

### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle is divided into distinct phases, each with specific activities aligned with ISO and PMBOK guidelines.

#### I. Project Initiation & Definition

i.  **Stakeholder Identification & Analysis:** Identify all stakeholders and assess their interests and influence [Ref 1, Ref 8]. Develop a stakeholder management plan.
ii.  **Project Charter Development:** Define project objectives, scope, deliverables, high-level requirements, and success criteria. Secure project sponsor approval [Ref 8].
iii.  **Feasibility Study:** Evaluate project viability, including technical, economic, and operational feasibility.
iv.  **High-Level Risk Assessment:** Identify and assess initial project risks [Ref 14].
v.  **Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) Creation:** Decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 3].
vi. **Organizational strategy alignment:** Ensuring project is in line with organizational goals, vision, and values [Ref 15].

#### II. Planning

i.  **Scope Management:** Develop a detailed scope statement, including product and project scope [Ref 2].
ii.  **Schedule Development:** Create a detailed project schedule, including activities, dependencies, and critical path analysis.
iii.  **Budgeting:** Develop a detailed budget based on cost estimates for all project activities.
iv.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and acquire necessary resources (labor, equipment, materials).
v.  **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards, processes, and metrics.
vi.  **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan to manage stakeholder communication [Ref 2].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** Identify procurement needs and develop procurement strategies [Ref 2].
viii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a detailed risk management plan, including risk identification, analysis, response planning, and monitoring.

#### III. Execution

i.  **Work Package Execution:** Carry out activities as defined in the WBS and project plan.
ii.  **Team Management:** Manage the project team, ensuring effective communication, collaboration, and performance.
iii.  **Procurement Management:** Manage contracts and vendor performance.
iv.  **Quality Assurance:** Implement quality control processes to ensure deliverables meet quality standards.
v.  **Change Management:** Implement the change management process to control and manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget [Ref 2].
vi. **Risk Response Implementation:** Execute risk response plans to mitigate identified risks.

#### IV. Monitoring & Controlling

i.  **Performance Measurement:** Track project performance against the project plan, including schedule, budget, and scope.
ii.  **Progress Reporting:** Regularly report on project progress to stakeholders, highlighting variances and corrective actions.
iii.  **Issue Management:** Identify, document, and resolve project issues.
iv.  **Change Control:** Manage and control changes to the project baseline.
v.  **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor and control project risks, updating the risk register as needed.

#### V. Project Closure

i.  **Project Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables from the client/stakeholders.
ii.  **Contract Closure:** Close out all contracts and procurement activities.
iii.  **Final Reporting:** Prepare a final project report, summarizing project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations.
iv.  **Project Documentation Archiving:** Archive all project documentation for future reference.
v.  **Team Dissolution:** Release project team members and reassign resources.
vi.  **Post-Project Review:** Conduct a post-project review to identify lessons learned and improve future project performance.

### Roles

Key project roles and their responsibilities:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides resources and executive decisions [Ref 8].
*   **Project Manager:** Leads and manages the project, responsible for all aspects of the project lifecycle.
*   **Construction Manager:** Oversees day-to-day construction activities.
*   **Quality Manager:** Ensures that all processes and deliverables meet defined quality standards.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals, groups, or organizations that have interests in the project [Ref 8].
*   **Team Members:** Carry out project activities.

### Decision Gates

These are critical points where decisions are made to proceed to the next phase.

i.  **Project Charter Approval:** At the end of Phase I, the Project Charter must be approved by the Project Sponsor [Ref 8].
ii.  **Project Plan Approval:** At the end of Phase II, the detailed project plan (including scope, schedule, budget, and risk management plan) must be approved by the Project Sponsor.
iii.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** At the end of Phase V, deliverables are formally accepted by stakeholders. This signals successful completion of the project, including its objectives [Ref 7].
iv.  **Change Request Approvals:** Throughout the project, all change requests must be approved by a Change Control Board (or similar body) to ensure changes are properly assessed and authorized.
