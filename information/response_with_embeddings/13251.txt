### Process Summary

This recommendation outlines a project management process tailored for a medium-scale education research project, leveraging the PMBOK framework. The project's manual configuration nature and 6-18 month duration necessitate a structured, phased approach to manage scope, schedule, budget, and resources effectively. This process incorporates key PMBOK principles such as stakeholder engagement, team collaboration, and risk management [Ref 8]. The project will follow a structured approach encompassing initiation, planning, execution, monitoring and controlling, and closure, aligning with the project life cycle described in ISO21502 [Ref 9]. It will also focus on delivering a unique and verifiable deliverable [Ref 12] that meets the defined objectives. The aim is to ensure project success by addressing typical challenges of ambiguity, unclear responsibilities, and changing requirements [Ref 3].

### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation**

*   **Activities:**
    i.  Define project objectives, scope, and deliverables. This aligns with defining the project scope [Ref 6].
    ii.  Identify stakeholders and their expectations [Ref 4].
    iii. Develop a Project Charter outlining the project's purpose, objectives, and high-level requirements.
    iv.  Identify the project's development approach and life cycle [Ref 2].
    v.  Conduct initial risk assessment.

II.  **Planning**

*   **Activities:**
    i.  Develop a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope [Ref 5].
    ii.  Define project activities, estimate durations, and sequence tasks.
    iii. Create a detailed schedule and resource allocation plan.
    iv.  Develop a budget and cost management plan. This incorporates the project budget [Ref 6].
    v.  Develop a communication plan [Ref 6].
    vi.  Develop a procurement plan if external resources or services are needed [Ref 6].
    vii.  Develop a risk management plan to identify, analyze, and plan for potential threats [Ref 14].
    viii. Develop a change management plan.

III. **Execution**

*   **Activities:**
    i.  Execute the project plan, managing tasks, resources, and deliverables.
    ii.  Manage project team and resources, creating a collaborative environment [Ref 8].
    iii. Procure necessary resources or services as needed [Ref 6].
    iv.  Conduct regular project status meetings and reporting.
    v.  Manage and address project issues.
    vi.  Implement the approved change requests through the change management process [Ref 6].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**

*   **Activities:**
    i.  Monitor project progress against the baseline schedule and budget.
    ii.  Track and manage project risks and issues.
    iii.  Control changes through a defined change management process [Ref 6].
    iv.  Monitor the quality of project deliverables.
    v.  Communicate project status to stakeholders [Ref 6].
    vi. Measure project performance against key metrics.

V. **Closure**

*   **Activities:**
    i.  Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
    ii.  Conduct a post-project review to document lessons learned.
    iii.  Close out contracts and procurements.
    iv.  Archive project documents and records.
    v.  Release project resources and team members.

### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions [Ref 4].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for overall project planning, execution, and control.
*   **Project Team:** Individuals responsible for completing project tasks.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or organizations with an interest in the project [Ref 4].

### Decision Gates

i.  **Project Charter Approval:** (Initiation phase) - Formal authorization to begin the project. The project is directed to accomplish agreed objectives [Ref 9].
ii.  **Project Plan Approval:** (Planning phase) - Approval of the detailed project plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and risk management plans.
iii.  **Change Request Approval:** (Execution, Monitoring & Controlling phases) - Approval of changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget through a defined process [Ref 6].
iv.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** (Closure phase) - Formal acceptance of the project deliverables by the stakeholders [Ref 12].
