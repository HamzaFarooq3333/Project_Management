#### 2. Phases and Activities

The project will follow these phases, incorporating principles from relevant standards [Ref 9]:

I.  **Initiation:** This phase defines the project's purpose, objectives, and scope.
    i.  Define project objectives, aligned with business goals and stakeholder needs [Ref 6].
    ii.  Conduct a thorough stakeholder analysis, identifying key stakeholders and their interests [Ref 3, Ref 6].
    iii.  Develop a high-level project scope statement.
    iv.  Establish a project charter, formally authorizing the project and assigning the project manager.
    v.  Identify high-level risks and constraints.

II. **Planning:** Detailed planning is crucial for a project of this scale and in the finance industry.
    i.  Develop a detailed Project Scope including scope management plan [Ref 2].
    ii.  Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope [Ref 4].
    iii.  Develop a detailed schedule, including activity definition, sequencing, and duration estimation [Ref 2].
    iv.  Create a detailed budget and resource allocation plan [Ref 2].
    v.  Develop a communication management plan, defining communication frequency, methods, and stakeholders [Ref 2].
    vi.  Develop a risk management plan, identifying, analyzing, and planning for risk responses [Ref 8, Ref 11].
    vii. Develop a procurement plan (if applicable) [Ref 2].
    viii. Develop a change management plan [Ref 2].

III. **Execution:** This is the phase of executing planned activities.
    i.  Manage the project team and resources, ensuring effective teamwork and collaboration [Ref 11].
    ii.  Execute planned marketing campaigns and initiatives.
    iii.  Manage vendor relationships (if applicable).
    iv.  Implement the communication management plan.
    v.  Monitor project progress and performance against the baseline plans.
    vi.  Track and manage risks, implementing risk response plans.
    vii. Apply quality assurance measures to all deliverables [Ref 11].

IV.  **Monitoring and Controlling:** Continuous monitoring and control are essential to keep the project on track.
    i.  Track project performance against the baseline plans (schedule, budget, scope).
    ii.  Monitor and control project scope, ensuring changes are managed through a formal change management process [Ref 2].
    iii.  Manage issues and risks, implementing mitigation strategies.
    iv.  Track and report progress to stakeholders.
    v.  Conduct regular project performance reviews.
    vi.  Manage project communication, and update the communications plan.
    vii. Implement earned value management (EVM) to measure project performance.

V. **Closure:** Finalizing the project and ensuring all deliverables are complete.
    i.  Formally close out contracts and vendor agreements (if applicable).
    ii.  Obtain formal project acceptance from stakeholders.
    iii.  Conduct a post-project review/lessons learned session.
    iv.  Document and archive all project documentation.
    v.  Release project resources.

#### 3. Roles

Key roles are vital to ensure project success.

*   **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for providing resources, executive decisions, and overall project success [Ref 6].
*   **Project Manager:** Leads and manages the project, responsible for planning, execution, monitoring, and closing the project [Ref 6].
*   **Project Team:** Executes the project tasks and deliverables.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by the project [Ref 3, Ref 6]. They could be internal (e.g., Marketing, Legal, Compliance) or external (e.g., Customers, Regulatory bodies) to the organization.
*   **Change Control Board:** Reviews and approves/rejects change requests.

#### 4. RACI Matrix

| Phase                 | Initiation        | Planning          | Execution         | Monitoring & Controlling | Closure           |
|-----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **Project Sponsor**      | A                 | I                 | I                 | I                        | A, C              |
| **Project Manager**      | R                 | R                 | R                 | R                        | R                 |
| **Project Team**       | C                 | A, C              | A                 | A, C                     | C, I              |
| **Stakeholders**       | C                 | C                 | C                 | C                        | I                 |
| **Change Control Board** | -                 | A                 | A                 | A                        | -                 |

*   **R** = Responsible:  The person or role who does the work.
*   **A** = Accountable:  The person or role who is ultimately accountable for the correct and thorough completion of the deliverable or task. Only one "A" per task.
*   **C** = Consulted:  The person or role who is consulted before a decision is made or an activity performed.
*   **I** = Informed:  The person or role who is kept informed of progress.

#### 5. Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial checkpoints to validate project progress and make crucial decisions:

*   **Initiation Phase Approval:** The Project Sponsor approves the Project Charter, signifying project authorization and alignment with organizational goals.
*   **Planning Phase Approval:** Sign-off on the detailed project plan (scope, schedule, budget, etc.) by the Project Sponsor and relevant stakeholders before execution to ensure alignment and feasibility [Ref 2].
*   **Execution Phase Review:** Regular reviews of project performance against the plan, with decision points for scope changes, budget adjustments, or schedule revisions [Ref 3].
*   **Change Requests Approval:** Formal approval or rejection of change requests by the Change Control Board, ensuring changes are managed and controlled [Ref 2].
*   **Project Closure Approval:** Formal acceptance of all deliverables by the Project Sponsor and stakeholders upon project completion [Ref 5].
