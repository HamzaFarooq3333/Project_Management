### Process Recommendation: Small-Scale IT Marketing Project (ISO Standard)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a project management framework for a small-scale information technology marketing project, adhering to ISO Standards and aiming for completion within six months. It incorporates best practices from PMBOK and ISO 21502/21500, focusing on a streamlined approach suitable for smaller projects [Ref 3]. The framework emphasizes clear stakeholder engagement, iterative planning, risk management, and continuous monitoring to ensure project success [Ref 2, Ref 10]. Tailoring principles are applied to adapt the framework to the specific project context and organizational maturity [Ref 8, Ref 9, Ref 10].

-----

### Phases and Activities:

I. **Initiation:**

i.  **Project Definition & Scope:** Define project objectives, deliverables, and scope [Ref 1]. Develop a preliminary project scope statement.
ii. **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify all stakeholders who may be affected by or can affect the project [Ref 7].
iii. **Feasibility Study:** Assess the project's feasibility, considering technical, economic, and operational aspects [Ref 15].
iv. **Project Sponsor Assignment:** Assign a project sponsor who will be responsible for providing resources and making executive decisions [Ref 7].

II. **Planning:**

i.  **Detailed Scope Planning:** Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 5].
ii. **Schedule Development:** Develop a realistic project schedule with defined activities, durations, and dependencies [Ref 1].
iii. **Budgeting:** Establish a detailed project budget, including resource allocation and contingency reserves [Ref 1].
iv. **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks (both threats and opportunities), assess their impact and probability, and develop risk response plans [Ref 14].
v.  **Communication Planning:** Define communication channels, frequency, and responsibilities to ensure effective stakeholder engagement [Ref 1].
vi. **Resource Planning:** Plan for the necessary resources, including team members, equipment, and tools.
vii. **Procurement Planning:** If applicable, plan for procurement activities, including vendor selection and contract management [Ref 1].

III. **Execution:**

i.  **Team Kick-off:** Conduct a project kick-off meeting to communicate project goals, roles, and responsibilities to the team.
ii. **Deliverable Creation:** Execute project tasks according to the project schedule and WBS.
iii. **Quality Assurance:** Implement quality assurance processes to ensure that deliverables meet the required standards.
iv. **Communication:** Regularly communicate project status, issues, and risks to stakeholders [Ref 1].
v.  **Change Management:** Implement a change management process to handle any changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 1].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:**

i.  **Progress Tracking:** Monitor project progress against the project schedule and budget.
ii. **Performance Measurement:** Measure project performance using key performance indicators (KPIs).
iii. **Risk Monitoring:** Continuously monitor identified risks and implement risk response plans as needed [Ref 14].
iv. **Issue Resolution:** Address any issues that arise during the project execution phase.
v.  **Change Control:** Manage changes to the project baseline through a formal change control process [Ref 1].

V. **Closure:**

i.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from the stakeholders.
ii. **Project Documentation:** Archive all project documentation, including project plans, reports, and lessons learned.
iii. **Team Release:** Release project team members.
iv. **Final Report:** Prepare and distribute a final project report summarizing project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations.
v.  **Project Closure Meeting:** Conduct a project closure meeting to celebrate project success and identify areas for improvement.

### Roles:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, resources, and support for the project [Ref 7].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing the project [Ref 12].
*   **Marketing Team Members:** Responsible for executing marketing tasks and delivering project deliverables.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by or can affect the project [Ref 7].

### Decision Gates:

I. **End of Initiation Phase:** Approval to proceed to the Planning Phase based on the feasibility study and defined scope.
II. **End of Planning Phase:** Approval of the project plan (scope, schedule, budget) before moving to Execution.
III. **Mid-Project Review:** Assessment of project progress and performance at the midpoint of the project. Decide whether to continue, modify, or terminate the project.
IV. **Closure Approval:** Formal sign-off on deliverables and project closure from stakeholders.
