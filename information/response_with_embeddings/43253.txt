### Phases and Activities

The project will proceed through distinct phases, each with specific objectives and activities, ensuring governance and control throughout the project life cycle [Ref 9].

#### I. Initiation Phase
This phase establishes the project's foundation and formal authorization.
   i.  **Define Project Objective and Scope:** Articulate the core purpose and high-level boundaries of the research project, aligning with organizational goals. A project is a "temporary endeavour to achieve one or more defined objectives" [Ref 4].
   ii. **Identify and Analyze Stakeholders:** Proactively identify all individuals, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, or be affected by the project [Ref 4]. This includes university administration, faculty, students, funding bodies, and research participants. Effective engagement with stakeholders is crucial [Ref 8].
   iii. **Develop Project Charter:** Formalize the project's existence, objectives, high-level scope, deliverables [Ref 12], assumptions, constraints, and initial budget. This document names the Project Manager and authorizes them to apply organizational resources to project activities.
   iv. **Appoint Sponsor and Project Manager:** The sponsor is responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 4]. The Project Manager is responsible for guiding the project team and ensuring successful delivery [Ref 11].
   v.  **Establish Project Governance:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project will be authorized and directed to accomplish its agreed objectives [Ref 9].

#### II. Planning Phase
This phase develops the comprehensive plan for how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled.
   i.  **Develop Detailed Project Scope:** Decompose the project's defined scope using a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) into progressively lower levels, creating manageable work packages [Ref 5]. This includes both product scope (the research outcome) and project scope (the work to deliver it) [Ref 6].
   ii. **Define Research Methodology:** Specify the research design, data collection methods, analysis techniques, and ethical considerations appropriate for the education context.
   iii. **Create Schedule:** Develop a detailed project timeline, identifying activities, durations, dependencies, and milestones [Ref 6].
   iv. **Estimate and Allocate Resources:** Determine the human, material, and financial resources required for each activity, including team composition [Ref 6].
   v.  **Develop Budget:** Prepare a detailed cost estimate and allocate funds across project activities and phases [Ref 6].
   vi. **Identify and Plan for Risks:** Identify potential threats (risk occurrences with negative impact) and opportunities, and develop strategies for optimizing risk responses [Ref 5, Ref 8, Ref 14].
   vii. **Develop Communication Plan:** Define how, when, and by whom project information will be shared with various stakeholders [Ref 6].
   viii. **Develop Quality Management Plan:** Establish standards and processes to ensure that project deliverables meet specified quality requirements [Ref 8].
   ix. **Develop Change Management Plan:** Define the process for requesting, evaluating, and approving changes to the project baseline [Ref 6].
   x.  **Tailor Development Approach and Life Cycle:** Based on the research project's context, refine the chosen development approach (e.g., predictive with adaptive elements for specific research spirals) [Ref 1, Ref 2, Ref 8].

#### III. Execution Phase
This phase involves carrying out the project work according to the established plans.
   i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Perform the planned activities to create the project deliverables [Ref 2]. This includes conducting research, collecting data, and performing analysis.
   ii. **Acquire, Develop, and Manage Project Team:** Foster a collaborative team environment and provide the necessary support for the research team [Ref 8, Ref 11].
   iii. **Manage Communications:** Implement the communication plan, distributing information to stakeholders as planned [Ref 6].
   iv. **Conduct Procurements:** If external resources or services are required (e.g., specialized software, external review), manage the procurement process.
   v.  **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Continuously engage with stakeholders, managing their expectations and addressing their concerns [Ref 8].

#### IV. Monitoring & Control Phase
This phase runs concurrently with Execution, ensuring the project stays on track and within scope, budget, and schedule.
   i.  **Monitor and Control Project Work:** Track project performance against the plan using defined metrics and project performance domains [Ref 2, Ref 6].
   ii. **Perform Integrated Change Control:** Review all change requests, approve changes, and manage changes to deliverables, organizational process assets, and the project management plan [Ref 6].
   iii. **Control Scope:** Monitor the status of the project and product scope, managing changes to the scope baseline [Ref 6].
   iv. **Control Schedule:** Monitor project status to update project progress and manage changes to the schedule baseline [Ref 6].
   v.  **Control Costs:** Monitor the status of the project to update the project budget and manage changes to the cost baseline [Ref 6].
   vi. **Control Quality:** Monitor specific project results to determine whether they comply with relevant quality standards [Ref 8].
   vii. **Control Resources:** Ensure that assigned resources are available and utilized efficiently.
   viii. **Monitor Risks:** Continuously identify new risks, monitor existing risks, implement risk responses, and evaluate their effectiveness [Ref 8, Ref 12, Ref 14].
   ix. **Manage Issues:** Identify, track, and resolve any events that arise during the project that require management action [Ref 12].

#### V. Closure Phase
This final phase formally completes the project, ensuring all administrative aspects are finalized.
   i.  **Close Project or Phase:** Formalize the completion of all project activities and formalize the achievement of project objectives [Ref 4].
   ii. **Obtain Final Acceptance of Deliverables:** Secure formal acceptance from the sponsor and relevant stakeholders for all final research outputs and reports [Ref 12].
   iii. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document project successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
   iv. **Release Project Team:** Formally release project team members and reassign resources.
   v.  **Archive Project Documents:** Compile and archive all relevant project documentation.
   vi. **Prepare Final Project Report:** Summarize project performance, outcomes, and stakeholder satisfaction.

### Roles

Key roles are essential for clear accountability and effective project execution:

*   **Project Sponsor:** The person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable project success [Ref 4]. They provide high-level governance and strategic direction [Ref 9].
*   **Project Manager:** The individual responsible for coordinating activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 4, Ref 11]. They lead the project team [Ref 8].
*   **Research Team Lead (if applicable):** Oversees the technical aspects of the research, guiding research activities and ensuring methodological rigor.
*   **Research Team Members:** Individuals performing the actual research work, data collection, analysis, and report drafting.
*   **Key Stakeholders:** Any person, group, or organization that has interests in, or can affect, or be affected by, any aspect of the project [Ref 4]. This includes academic departments, funding agencies, ethics committees, and external experts.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project lifecycle where formal review and approval are required to proceed, ensuring accountability and adherence to project governance [Ref 9, Ref 10].

   i.  **Project Charter Approval Gate (Initiation to Planning):**
       *   **Purpose:** To formally authorize the project and commit resources for detailed planning. This gate confirms that the project's objectives align with organizational strategy and that a viable sponsor is in place [Ref 4].
       *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for detailed planning.
       *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor, with endorsement from relevant executive stakeholders.

   ii. **Project Baseline Approval Gate (Planning to Execution):**
       *   **Purpose:** To formally approve the Project Management Plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and risk baselines, before execution begins [Ref 6]. This ensures that all planning is complete and agreed upon.
       *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for project execution.
       *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor, with agreement from key stakeholders.

   iii. **Phase Review/Deliverable Acceptance Gates (During Execution & Monitoring):**
       *   **Purpose:** To review progress, assess the quality of interim deliverables [Ref 12], and re-evaluate project viability before proceeding to the next major stage of research (e.g., after data collection, before final analysis). This allows for adjustments based on research findings or evolving context [Ref 8].
       *   **Decision:** Continue/Adjust/Hold/Cancel the project phase.
       *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor, Project Manager, and relevant Key Stakeholders/Research Team Lead.

   iv. **Final Deliverable Acceptance Gate (Monitoring & Control to Closure):**
       *   **Purpose:** To confirm that all final research deliverables and reports have been completed, reviewed, and accepted by the sponsor and relevant stakeholders [Ref 12]. This ensures the project has met its defined objectives [Ref 4].
       *   **Decision:** Approve final deliverables for formal closure.
       *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor and designated key stakeholders (e.g., funding body representatives).

   v.  **Project Closure Gate (End of Closure):**
       *   **Purpose:** To formally close the project administratively, ensuring all contracts are closed, lessons learned documented, and resources released.
       *   **Decision:** Formally close the project.
       *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor and Project Manager.
