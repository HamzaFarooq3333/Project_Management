### Project Management Process Recommendation: Small IT Marketing Project (ISO Standards)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a project management approach for a small-scale Information Technology marketing project, lasting less than six months. The approach is aligned with ISO standards, particularly ISO 21502, which provides guidance on project management [Ref 3, Ref 11]. This process emphasizes tailoring to the project context [Ref 10], stakeholder engagement [Ref 2], and a focus on delivering value [Ref 6]. The framework incorporates planning, execution, monitoring & controlling, and closure phases, with consideration for risks [Ref 14] and adaptability [Ref 10]. Due to the project's small size, a streamlined approach will be adopted, focusing on essential processes and documentation.

-----

### Phases and Activities

**I. Initiation**

i. **Project Definition:** Define the project objectives, scope, and deliverables. This involves understanding the business needs the marketing project will address [Ref 3].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification & Analysis:** Identify key stakeholders and analyze their interests and influence [Ref 7].
iii. **Project Sponsor Assignment:** Assign a project sponsor responsible for securing resources and making executive decisions [Ref 7].
iv. **Project Charter Development:** Create a project charter that formally authorizes the project.
v. **Initial Risk Assessment:** Perform a preliminary risk assessment to identify potential threats and opportunities [Ref 14].

**II. Planning**

i. **Scope Management:** Define and document the detailed project scope, including product scope and project scope [Ref 1]. Develop a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 5].
ii. **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule with defined tasks, durations, and dependencies [Ref 1].
iii. **Budgeting:** Develop a detailed project budget, including resource costs, materials, and other expenses [Ref 1].
iv. **Resource Planning:** Determine the required resources (personnel, equipment, software) and assign them to specific tasks [Ref 4].
v. **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan outlining how project information will be disseminated to stakeholders [Ref 1].
vi. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a risk management plan that identifies, analyzes, and plans responses to potential risks [Ref 14].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** If needed, define the procurement process for acquiring external resources or services [Ref 1].
viii. **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards and metrics for project deliverables [Ref 10].

**III. Execution**

i. **Task Execution:** Execute project tasks according to the project schedule and plan.
ii. **Team Management:** Manage the project team and ensure effective collaboration [Ref 2].
iii. **Communication:** Implement the communication plan to keep stakeholders informed of project progress.
iv. **Issue Management:** Identify and resolve project issues as they arise.
v. **Change Management:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget through a formal change control process [Ref 1].

**IV. Monitoring & Controlling**

i. **Performance Measurement:** Monitor project performance against the project plan. Track key metrics such as schedule, budget, and scope [Ref 2].
ii. **Variance Analysis:** Analyze variances between planned and actual performance and take corrective actions [Ref 1].
iii. **Risk Monitoring & Control:** Monitor identified risks and implement risk response plans [Ref 14]. Identify and manage new risks that emerge.
iv. **Change Control:** Review and approve or reject change requests [Ref 1].
v. **Status Reporting:** Regularly report project status to stakeholders.

**V. Closure**

i. **Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
ii. **Project Documentation Archiving:** Archive all project documentation.
iii. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a lessons learned session to identify areas for improvement in future projects.
iv. **Project Team Release:** Release project team members.
v. **Final Project Report:** Prepare and distribute a final project report.

-----

### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, resources, and support for the project [Ref 7].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project.
*   **Marketing Team Members:** Responsible for completing marketing tasks, such as content creation, campaign management, and analysis.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by the project [Ref 7].
