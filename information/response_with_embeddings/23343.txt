### Project Management Process Recommendation: Finance Research Project (ISO Standards)

**Process Summary:**

This document outlines a recommended project management process for a large-scale ( > 18 months) finance research project, conducted in accordance with ISO standards. This project will utilize a phased approach, encompassing initiation, planning, execution, monitoring & controlling, and closure. The process will adhere to the guidelines within ISO 21502 [Ref 1, Ref 6], which provides general guidance on project management applicable to any organization. Furthermore, the process will incorporate elements from PMBOK for specific areas like planning [Ref 3] and stakeholder management [Ref 2]. Emphasis will be placed on diligent planning [Ref 3], clear roles and responsibilities, and continuous monitoring to ensure the project remains aligned with its objectives [Ref 7, Ref 15]. Adaptability [Ref 11] and robust change management processes are included, as the project's long duration may necessitate adjustments.

### Phases and Activities

The project will be managed through the following phases, with activities grounded in ISO and PMBOK principles:

**I. Initiation:**

i.  **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's objectives, scope, and high-level requirements. Identify key stakeholders [Ref 4, Ref 7] and the project sponsor [Ref 7].
ii.  **Feasibility Study:** Assess the viability of the research project, considering potential risks and benefits.
iii. **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identifying people, groups or organizations that could impact the project [Ref 4].

**II. Planning:**

i.  **Project Management Plan Development:** Create a comprehensive plan outlining how the project will be executed, monitored, and controlled.
ii.  **Scope Definition:** Develop a detailed work breakdown structure (WBS) [Ref 4] to define the project scope.
iii. **Resource Planning:** Determine the required resources (personnel, budget, equipment) and create a resource allocation plan.
iv.  **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule, estimating activity durations and dependencies [Ref 3].
v.  **Budget Development:** Develop a detailed budget based on resource requirements and schedule [Ref 3].
vi.  **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks [Ref 4, Ref 10], assess their likelihood and impact, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 11].
vii. **Communication Plan:** Establish how to engage stakeholders effectively [Ref 11]

**III. Execution:**

i.  **Research Activities:** Conduct the planned research activities, adhering to the project scope and schedule.
ii.  **Data Collection and Analysis:** Collect and analyze relevant data, ensuring data quality and integrity.
iii. **Deliverable Creation:** Produce the defined project deliverables, such as research reports and presentations [Ref 9].

**IV. Monitoring & Controlling:**

i.  **Performance Monitoring:** Track project progress against the schedule and budget [Ref 3].
ii.  **Risk Monitoring:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation plans as needed [Ref 11].
iii. **Change Management:** Manage any changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget through a formal change control process [Ref 3, Ref 11].
iv.  **Issue Management:** Identify and resolve any issues that arise during the project [Ref 12].
v.  **Stakeholder Communication:** Communicate regularly with stakeholders to provide project updates and address any concerns [Ref 2, Ref 11].

**V. Closure:**

i.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables.
ii.  **Project Documentation Archival:** Archive all project documentation for future reference.
iii. **Lessons Learned Documentation:** Document lessons learned throughout the project to improve future projects [Ref 9].
iv.  **Project Closure Report:** Prepare a final project closure report summarizing project performance and outcomes.

### Roles

i.  **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support for the project [Ref 7].
ii.  **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and closing the project [Ref 1, Ref 15].
iii. **Research Team:** Responsible for carrying out the research activities.
iv.  **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in or are affected by the project [Ref 4, Ref 7].
