iv. High-Level Risk Assessment: Conduct a preliminary risk assessment to identify potential risks and threats [Ref 14].
v. Approval of the Project Charter: Obtain formal approval to proceed with the project [Ref 13].

II. **Planning**

i. Scope Definition: Develop a detailed project scope statement, including the required software features and functionalities [Ref 2].
ii. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) Creation: Break down the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
iii. Schedule Development: Create a detailed project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and milestones [Ref 2].
iv. Budget Development: Develop a project budget, including resource costs, software development costs, and contingency funds [Ref 2].
v. Resource Planning: Identify and allocate resources (personnel, software, hardware) required for the project.
vi. Communication Plan Development: Develop a communication plan outlining how project information will be shared with stakeholders [Ref 2].
vii. Risk Management Planning: Develop a risk management plan to identify, assess, and mitigate potential risks [Ref 10].
viii. Procurement Planning: If applicable, develop a procurement plan for any necessary software or services [Ref 2].
ix. Quality Management Planning: Plan how quality will be built into processes and deliverables [Ref 7].
x. Obtain approval of the project plan.

III. **Execution**

i. Software Development: Perform software development activities in accordance with the project plan.
ii. Configuration: Manually configure software.
iii. Testing and Quality Assurance: Conduct testing and quality assurance activities to ensure the software meets the defined requirements [Ref 8].
iv. Stakeholder Communication: Communicate project progress to stakeholders as defined in the communication plan.
v. Risk Management: Monitor and manage identified risks, implementing mitigation strategies as necessary.
vi. Issue Management: Address and resolve project issues as they arise [Ref 9].
vii. Change Management: Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 2].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**

i. Progress Monitoring: Track project progress against the project plan, monitoring key performance indicators (KPIs).
ii. Schedule Control: Manage and control the project schedule, making adjustments as needed.
iii. Cost Control: Manage and control project costs, ensuring the project remains within budget.
iv. Quality Control: Monitor and control the quality of project deliverables.
v. Risk Monitoring and Control: Monitor and control project risks, implementing mitigation strategies as needed.

#### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project where decisions are made to proceed to the next phase.

i.  **Project Charter Approval:** (Initiation phase) - The sponsor must approve the project charter before project planning can begin.
ii.  **Project Plan Approval:** (Planning phase) - Approval of the complete project plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and risk management plans, is required before execution.
iii.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** (Closure phase) - Formal acceptance of the completed software solution by the stakeholders, signifying successful project completion [Ref 10].
iv.  **Change Requests:** (Throughout the project) - Each change request should be assessed, approved, and integrated with corresponding impacts of schedule and budget.
