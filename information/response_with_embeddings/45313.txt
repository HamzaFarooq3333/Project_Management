### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be divided into sequential phases, with clear activities and decision gates.

#### I. Initiation Phase
This phase focuses on defining the project at a high level and obtaining initial authorization.

i.  **Identify and Engage Stakeholders:** Identify all persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect or be affected by, any aspect of the project [Ref 8]. Understand their needs and expectations [Ref 2].
ii. **Define High-Level Project Objectives and Scope:** Establish the primary goals and boundaries for the IT infrastructure project, recognizing the temporary endeavor to achieve defined objectives [Ref 8].
iii. **Appoint Project Leadership:** Formally appoint the Project Sponsor and Project Manager. The Sponsor is responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 8], while the Project Manager coordinates activities to direct and control [Ref 8].
iv. **Develop Project Charter:** Document the project's purpose, high-level requirements, objectives, and authorize the project manager to begin work. This includes initial risk identification, categorizing them as positive (opportunities) or negative (threats) [Ref 13].
v. **Establish Initial Project Governance:** Define the principles, policies, and framework by which the project will be directed and controlled, ensuring alignment with organizational governance [Ref 6, Ref 10].

#### II. Planning Phase
This is a critical phase for a large-scale project, involving detailed definition and strategizing.

i.  **Develop Detailed Project Scope:** Decompose the product scope and project scope into manageable components, including the creation of a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 1, Ref 4]. This breaks down the defined scope into progressively lower levels of work elements [Ref 4].
ii. **Estimate, Schedule, and Budget:** Develop detailed estimates for resources, effort, and duration. Create a comprehensive project schedule and budget, incorporating these estimates [Ref 1].
iii. **Define Team Composition and Structure:** Determine the required team roles, responsibilities, and reporting structure [Ref 1].
iv. **Develop Management Plans:** Create detailed plans for Communication, Procurement, Resource Management, Quality Management (building quality into processes and deliverables) [Ref 1, Ref 14], and Change Management [Ref 1].
v. **Detailed Risk Management Planning:** Identify individual and overall project risks, analyze their potential impact, and plan appropriate risk responses. This includes optimizing risk responses to be cost-effective and realistic, and ensuring they are agreed upon by stakeholders [Ref 13, Ref 14].
vi. **Define Project Life Cycle and Delivery Approaches:** Establish the defined set of phases from start to end [Ref 10], considering predictive, incremental, iterative, or adaptive delivery approaches as suited to the outputs [Ref 3].
vii. **Tailor Project Management Approach:** Based on the project context and complexity, tailor the project management approach to ensure fit-for-purpose application of principles and practices [Ref 9, Ref 14].

#### III. Execution, Monitoring & Controlling Phase
This phase involves performing the work, managing progress, and ensuring objectives are met.

i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the planned activities to create the project deliverables [Ref 2]. This involves the coordinated activities to achieve agreed objectives [Ref 8].
ii. **Acquire and Manage Resources:** Procure necessary resources (human, material, equipment) and manage their allocation throughout the project. Motivate individuals involved in the project to achieve objectives [Ref 15].
iii. **Implement Communication Plan:** Conduct planned communications to keep stakeholders informed and engaged [Ref 1].
iv. **Monitor and Control Project Progress:** Track actual performance against the baselines (scope, schedule, budget) [Ref 1]. Conduct measurements to assess project performance [Ref 2].
v. **Manage Changes:** Follow the defined change management process to review, approve, and implement changes to the project baselines [Ref 1].
vi. **Manage Risks:** Continuously identify new risks, monitor existing ones, and implement planned risk responses. Optimize responses throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 13, Ref 14]. Address issues that arise during the project [Ref 6].
vii. **Manage Deliverables:** Ensure that unique and verifiable elements required to be produced by the project are developed and meet quality standards [Ref 6].

#### IV. Closure Phase
This phase formally concludes the project and ensures all administrative activities are completed.

i.  **Verify Final Deliverables:** Ensure all project deliverables are completed and meet the defined quality criteria and acceptance requirements.
ii. **Obtain Formal Acceptance:** Secure formal acceptance of the final product, service, or result from the Project Sponsor and relevant stakeholders [Ref 8].
iii. **Close Contracts and Procurements:** Administratively close all project contracts and procurement agreements.
iv. **Release Resources:** Formally release project team members and other project resources.
v. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document successes, challenges, and best practices to improve future projects.
vi. **Final Project Report and Archiving:** Compile final project documentation, reports, and archive relevant project information.

### Roles

The following key roles are essential for the successful delivery of this large IT infrastructure project:

*   **Project Sponsor:** The person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success [Ref 8]. Accountable for project authorization and overall business value.
*   **Project Manager:** The individual responsible for coordinated activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 8, Ref 15]. Leads the planning, execution, and closure of the project.
*   **Key Stakeholders:** Persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of a project [Ref 8, Ref 2]. This includes users, operations, security, legal, and regulatory bodies.
*   **Project Team:** Individuals working on the project, performing the defined work packages. Team composition is a key planning element [Ref 1].
*   **Functional/Resource Managers:** Provide resources and expertise to the project, ensuring their availability and performance.
*   **Quality Assurance Lead:** Ensures that quality standards are met for processes and deliverables [Ref 14].
*   **Risk Manager/Specialist:** Focuses on optimizing risk responses and managing individual and overall risks throughout the project [Ref 13].
*   **Change Control Board (CCB):** A formal group responsible for reviewing and approving or rejecting change requests.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points to ensure that the project remains aligned with strategic objectives and is performing as expected. Project governance principles and procedures dictate project authorization and direction [Ref 10].

1.  **Project Authorization Gate (After Initiation Phase):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally approve the project charter and commit resources for detailed planning.
    *   **Criteria:** High-level scope and objectives defined, initial business case validated, key stakeholders identified, Project Sponsor and Manager appointed, initial risk assessment completed.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Sponsor, Executive Management [Ref 8, Ref 10].
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go decision for proceeding to the Planning Phase.

2.  **Planning Completion Gate (After Planning Phase):**
    *   **Purpose:** To approve the detailed project management plan, baselines, and resource commitments for execution.
    *   **Criteria:** Detailed scope, schedule, budget, resource, communication, procurement, and risk management plans approved. Comprehensive WBS developed, risk responses optimized [Ref 1, Ref 4, Ref 13, Ref 14]. Readiness to execute confirmed.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Sponsor, Change Control Board (if applicable), Executive Management [Ref 10].
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go decision for proceeding to the Execution Phase.

3.  **Key Delivery Gates (During Execution Phase - e.g., Pilot Deployment, Major Infrastructure Rollout):**
    *   **Purpose:** To review significant milestones, ensure quality and readiness before moving to the next major delivery stage.
    *   **Criteria:** Specific deliverables completed and validated [Ref 6], quality checks passed [Ref 14], critical risks mitigated, operational readiness confirmed.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Sponsor, Project Manager, Key Stakeholders (e.g., Operations, Security).
    *   **Outcome:** Approval to proceed with the next stage of execution/deployment.

4.  **Project Closure Gate (After Execution Phase, Before Formal Closure):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally accept the final project deliverables and authorize project closure activities.
    *   **Criteria:** All project objectives achieved, final deliverables formally accepted by the client/users [Ref 8], outstanding issues resolved, operational handover completed.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Sponsor, Executive Management, Key Stakeholders.
    *   **Outcome:** Authorization to proceed with administrative closure.
