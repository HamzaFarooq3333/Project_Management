### Process Recommendation: Finance Infrastructure Project (PRINCE2)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines the key phases, activities, roles, and decision gates for a medium-sized finance infrastructure project following the PRINCE2 methodology.  The project will be managed in a structured and controlled environment, focusing on clear roles and responsibilities, defined deliverables, and effective stakeholder management [Ref 6, Ref 1].  The process is adaptable to the specific needs of the project [Ref 2], but maintains core PRINCE2 principles throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 7]. Planning is a critical element [Ref 3] ensuring scope, estimates, schedule, budget, team composition, communication, procurement, change management and measurement are all addressed.

-----

### Phases and Activities

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU)**

i. **Appoint the Project Management Team:** Define and assign roles such as the Project Board (Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier), Project Manager, and potentially Team Managers. [Ref 5]
ii. **Capture Lessons Learned:** Review past projects for relevant lessons to avoid repeating mistakes and improve project outcomes.
iii. **Draft the Project Brief:** Prepare a high-level overview of the project, including the problem, objectives, scope, and benefits.
iv. **Define the Project Approach:** Select the best method or process suited to the outputs [Ref 2].
v. **Plan the Initiation Stage:** Define the activities, resources, and timelines required to initiate the project.

II. **Initiating a Project (IP)**

i. **Refine the Business Case:** Develop a detailed business case that justifies the project investment, including costs, benefits, risks, and return on investment.
ii. **Develop the Project Management Plan:** Create a comprehensive plan that outlines how the project will be managed, including scope, schedule, budget, quality, risk, and communication. [Ref 3]
iii. **Set up Project Controls:** Establish monitoring and reporting mechanisms to track progress, identify issues, and take corrective action.
iv. **Define the Project Scope:** Work breakdown structure for elements of work [Ref 4].
v. **Assemble the Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Consolidate all key project information into a single document for approval by the Project Board.

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS)**

i. **Authorize Work Packages:** Delegate specific tasks to Team Managers or individual team members.
ii. **Monitor Progress:** Track progress against the project plan, identify deviations, and take corrective action.
iii. **Manage Issues and Risks:** Identify, assess, and respond to project issues and risks. [Ref 4]
iv. **Review Stage Status:** Conduct regular reviews to assess stage progress and determine if the project is on track.
v. **Report Progress:** Communicate project status to stakeholders [Ref 1, Ref 6], including the Project Board and Senior Users.

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP)**

i. **Accept Work Packages:** Receive and review work packages from Team Managers or individual team members.
ii. **Execute Work Packages:** Perform the tasks outlined in the work packages, ensuring quality and adherence to standards.
iii. **Deliver Products:** Submit completed deliverables for acceptance.
iv. **Provide Progress Updates:** Regularly update the Project Manager on progress, issues, and risks.

V. **Managing Stage Boundaries (SB)**

i. **Plan the Next Stage:** Develop a detailed plan for the upcoming stage, including activities, resources, and timelines.
ii. **Update the Project Plan:** Incorporate any changes or lessons learned into the overall project plan.
iii. **Update the Business Case:** Reassess the business case to ensure that the project remains viable and aligned with strategic objectives.
iv. **Report Stage End:** Prepare a report summarizing the stage’s performance and lessons learned.
v. **Get Approval to Proceed:** Obtain approval from the Project Board to proceed to the next stage.

VI. **Closing a Project (CP)**

i. **Confirm Project Completion:** Verify that all project deliverables have been accepted and that the project objectives have been met.
ii. **Evaluate the Project:** Conduct a post-project review to assess the project’s performance, identify lessons learned, and recommend improvements for future projects.
iii. **Archive Project Documentation:** Organize and store all project documentation for future reference.
iv. **Release Resources:** Reassign project resources to other projects or activities.
v. **Communicate Project Closure:** Notify all stakeholders of the project’s completion.

### Roles

*   **Executive:** Provides overall direction and authorization for the project.
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of the users who will benefit from the project’s deliverables.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of the suppliers who are providing resources or expertise to the project.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project. [Ref 5]
*   **Team Manager:** Responsible for managing a team of specialists who are delivering specific work packages.
*   **Project Assurance:** Provides independent oversight to ensure that the project is being managed effectively and in accordance with PRINCE2 principles.
*   **Project Support:** Provides administrative support to the project team.

### Decision Gates

i. **End of Starting Up a Project (SU):** Decision to proceed with the Initiation stage, based on the Project Brief.
ii. **End of Initiating a Project (IP):** Decision to proceed with the first Delivery stage, based on the Project Initiation Documentation (PID). This decision reviews the business case, plan and risk assessment.
iii. **End of Each Stage (SB):** Decision to proceed to the next stage, based on the stage end report and updated business case.
iv. **Project Closure (CP):** Decision to formally close the project, based on the Project Closure Report.
