### Phases and Activities

The project will be divided into the following phases, reflecting the PMBOK's emphasis on planning, execution, monitoring, and closure [Ref 3]:

I. **Initiation:**
    i. Define project goals and objectives in alignment with organizational strategy [Ref 14].
    ii. Identify key stakeholders (patients, healthcare providers, internal marketing team, legal/compliance) [Ref 3, Ref 10].
    iii. Develop a preliminary project charter outlining the project scope, budget, and timeline.
    iv. Obtain project approval and stakeholder buy-in.

II. **Planning:**
    i. Develop a detailed project scope statement and Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
    ii. Create a project schedule with tasks, dependencies, and resource assignments [Ref 1].
    iii. Develop a budget, including cost estimates for all project activities [Ref 1].
    iv. Create a risk management plan, identifying potential risks (e.g., regulatory changes, negative publicity) and developing mitigation strategies [Ref 7, Ref 8].
    v. Develop a communication plan outlining how project information will be disseminated to stakeholders [Ref 1].
    vi. Create a quality management plan to ensure deliverables meet specified standards.
    vii. Create a change management plan to address potential scope changes.
    viii. Define measurement criteria for project success [Ref 1, Ref 3].

III. **Execution:**
    i. Implement the marketing campaign according to the project plan.
    ii. Manage project resources effectively [Ref 5].
    iii. Communicate project progress to stakeholders regularly [Ref 6].
    iv. Track project costs and schedule.
    v. Execute risk mitigation strategies as needed [Ref 7].
    vi. Manage and track project issues.
    vii. Build quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 6].

IV. **Monitoring & Controlling:**
    i. Monitor project progress against the project plan [Ref 3].
    ii. Track key performance indicators (KPIs).
    iii. Identify and address any deviations from the plan [Ref 3].
    iv. Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 1].
    v. Conduct regular project status meetings.
    vi. Evaluate and respond to system interactions [Ref 6].

V. **Closure:**
    i. Obtain final stakeholder acceptance of project deliverables.
    ii. Conduct a project closeout meeting [Ref 9].
    iii. Document lessons learned [Ref 9].
    iv. Archive project documentation.
    v. Release project resources.
    vi. Communicate project completion to stakeholders.

### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, secures funding, and removes roadblocks.
*   **Project Manager:** Plans, executes, monitors, and closes the project. Responsible for team coordination and communication.
*   **Marketing Team:** Develops and executes marketing strategies and tactics.
*   **Legal/Compliance Team:** Ensures compliance with healthcare regulations and ethical guidelines.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with an interest in the project's outcome.

### Decision Gates

I. **Project Approval:** Following the Initiation phase, a formal decision gate is required to approve the project charter and authorize project funding. This gate ensures alignment with organizational strategy.
II. **Plan Approval:** After the Planning phase, stakeholders must approve the project plan before moving to execution. This ensures everyone agrees on the scope, budget, and timeline [Ref 1].
III. **Go/No-Go for Campaign Launch:** Before launching the marketing campaign, a review is conducted to ensure all deliverables meet quality standards and regulatory requirements.
IV. **Project Closure Approval:** After all deliverables are completed and accepted, the project sponsor must formally approve project closure.
