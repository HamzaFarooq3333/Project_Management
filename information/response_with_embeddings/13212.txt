#### 2. Phases and Activities

This project will follow a phased approach, aligned with PRINCE2 principles [Ref 3, Ref 7]. The project life cycle will have defined phases [Ref 10], with a clear set of phases from start to end [Ref 7].

**I. Project Initiation:**

i.  **Project Mandate Review:** Review the project mandate and business case to ensure alignment with organizational strategy and objectives. This helps the project to meet its objectives [Ref 3].
ii.  **Project Definition:** Define the project's scope, objectives, deliverables, and success criteria. A clear definition of the scope of a project is essential [Ref 5].
iii.  **Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify and analyze project stakeholders and their interests to determine communication and engagement strategies. Understanding stakeholders is a key project element [Ref 4].
iv.  **Project Team Assembly:** Assign roles and responsibilities to the project team, ensuring the right skills and experience are available. Team composition is a crucial planning element [Ref 1].
v.  **Project Plan Development:** Create a high-level project plan, including the project's approach, estimated timelines, budgets, and key milestones. Project planning is a central process [Ref 1].
vi. **Risk Assessment:** Perform a preliminary risk assessment, including the identification of potential threats and opportunities [Ref 13].

**II. Planning and Design:**

i.  **Detailed Planning:** Develop detailed plans for each project stage, considering activities, resources, and dependencies.
ii.  **Configuration Requirements Gathering:** Define the specific requirements for manual configuration, which will be the basis for project deliverables [Ref 11].
iii.  **System Design:** Design the IT system architecture and configuration specifications.
iv.  **Resource Allocation:** Allocate resources (personnel, budget, time, tools) required for each configuration task.
v.  **Communication Planning:** Finalize the communication plan, which outlines how stakeholders will be updated about the project's progress. Communication is another key element of a project plan [Ref 1].
vi.  **Procurement Planning (if required):** If any procurement is necessary, define procurement strategies and processes [Ref 1].

**III. Execution and Configuration:**

i.  **Configuration Implementation:** Execute the manual configuration according to the design specifications.
ii.  **Testing and Verification:** Conduct rigorous testing and verification to ensure the configuration meets the defined requirements.
iii.  **Risk Mitigation:** Implement risk mitigation plans to address identified threats [Ref 13].
iv.  **Issue Management:** Manage and resolve project issues promptly [Ref 11].
v.  **Change Management:** Control and manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget [Ref 1].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling:**

i.  **Progress Tracking:** Regularly monitor project progress against the plan and key performance indicators. This is central to project success [Ref 4].
ii.  **Performance Reporting:** Generate and distribute performance reports to stakeholders, outlining progress, risks, and issues.
iii.  **Schedule Control:** Manage schedule variances and take corrective actions as needed.
iv.  **Budget Control:** Monitor project expenditures and manage budget variances.
v.  **Change Control:** Manage change requests, assess their impact, and implement approved changes.
vi.  **Issue Management:** Resolve issues and escalate them as needed.

**V. Project Closure:**

i.  **Project Handover:** Formally hand over the completed system or research findings.
ii.  **Final Testing and Acceptance:** Verify the final system meets acceptance criteria.
iii.  **Project Documentation:** Complete and archive all project documentation.
iv.  **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a lessons-learned review to identify successes and areas for improvement.
v.  **Project Closure Report:** Produce a final project report.

#### 3. Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Accountable for obtaining the resources and executive decisions [Ref 6] and is responsible for overall project success.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for day-to-day management of the project, including planning, execution, and control. This person should be supported by appropriate guidance [Ref 12].
*   **Project Team Members:** Perform the tasks defined in the project plan.
*   **Senior User(s):** Represent the users' interests and ensure the project meets their needs.
*   **Senior Supplier(s):** Represent the suppliers’ interests.
*   **Project Assurance:** Provides independent oversight of the project, ensuring adherence to standards and quality.
*   **Change Authority:** Authorized to approve or reject change requests.

*   **R** = Responsible: The person responsible for completing the task.
*   **A** = Accountable: The person ultimately answerable for the correct and thorough completion of the task. Only one "A" per task.
*   **C** = Consulted: The person(s) who are consulted before a decision or action is taken.
*   **I** = Informed: The person(s) who are kept informed of progress and decisions.
*   **S** = Support (in this context, this is a special form of "Accountable" to represent the Sponsor's accountability)

#### 5. Decision Gates

i.  **Project Initiation Approval:** Before proceeding to the Planning and Design phase, the project sponsor and key stakeholders review the project plan, business case, and risk assessment to authorize the project to proceed [Ref 10].
ii.  **Design Review and Approval:** Before proceeding with Execution and Configuration, the Project Manager, Senior User, Senior Supplier and the Change Authority review the system design, ensuring that it meets requirements and stakeholder needs.
iii.  **Configuration Acceptance:** Before final handoff, verify the configuration meets acceptance criteria [Ref 14].
iv.  **Project Closure Approval:** The Project Sponsor formally approves project closure, based on the delivery of the approved deliverables, completion of the final report, and completion of lessons learned.
