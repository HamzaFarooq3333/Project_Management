### Phases and Activities

The project will proceed through five distinct phases, forming a defined project life cycle [Ref 8], with continuous monitoring and control integrated throughout.

#### I. Project Initiation
i.  **Project Mandate and Business Case Development:** Define the project's objectives and justification, linking it to organizational strategy and potential outcomes [Ref 15].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify all persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, or be affected by the project [Ref 10]. This includes clinical staff, IT, regulatory bodies, and end-users [Ref 3].
iii. **Project Charter Development:** Formalize project authorization, outlining high-level scope, objectives, success criteria, and appointing the Project Manager [Ref 8].
iv. **Establish Project Governance Framework:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project will be authorized, directed, and controlled to accomplish agreed objectives [Ref 8, Ref 13].
v. **Initial Risk Identification:** Conduct a preliminary identification of potential threats and opportunities that could impact the project [Ref 9].

#### II. Project Planning
i.  **Detailed Scope Definition:**
    *   Define the product scope (features, functions) and project scope (work to be done) [Ref 2].
    *   Develop a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) by decomposing the defined scope into progressively lower levels of work elements and work packages [Ref 4].
ii. **Activity Definition and Sequencing:** Break down work packages into specific activities and define their logical order.
iii. **Resource Planning:** Determine required human resources (team composition), equipment, and materials [Ref 2].
iv. **Estimating:** Estimate activity durations, costs, and resource requirements [Ref 2].
v.  **Schedule Development:** Create a project schedule based on sequences, durations, and resource assignments [Ref 2].
vi. **Budget Formulation:** Develop a comprehensive project budget [Ref 2].
vii. **Risk Management Planning:**
    *   Perform detailed risk identification, analysis, and plan appropriate risk responses for individual and overall risks, including threats [Ref 9, Ref 11]. Responses should be cost-effective and realistic [Ref 9].
    *   Define risk owners [Ref 9].
viii. **Quality Management Planning:** Define quality standards and activities to ensure that project processes and deliverables meet requirements, which is crucial in healthcare [Ref 6].
ix. **Communication Management Planning:** Develop a communication plan outlining what, when, to whom, how, and by whom information will be communicated [Ref 2]. Effectively engage with stakeholders [Ref 6].
x.  **Procurement Management Planning:** Plan for external acquisitions if necessary [Ref 2].
xi. **Change Management Planning:** Establish a process for managing changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 2].
xii. **Measurement Framework Definition:** Determine how project performance will be measured and reported [Ref 3].

#### III. Project Execution (Development & Testing)
i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Carry out the planned activities to create deliverables [Ref 3].
ii. **Acquire and Develop Project Team:** Obtain necessary resources and foster a collaborative project team environment [Ref 6].
iii. **Manage Communications:** Implement the communication plan, ensuring effective information flow.
iv. **Conduct Procurements:** Administer any contracts with external vendors.
v.  **Implement Risk Responses:** Execute planned actions to address identified risks [Ref 9].
vi. **Perform Quality Assurance:** Ensure that quality standards are being met throughout the development process [Ref 6].
vii. **Deliverable Production and Verification:** Produce unique and verifiable elements required by the project [Ref 13].
viii. **Software Development and Integration:** Write code, build modules, integrate system components.
ix. **Comprehensive Testing:** Conduct unit, integration, system, user acceptance, and security testing.

#### IV. Monitoring & Controlling
i.  **Monitor Project Work:** Track project progress against the plan [Ref 15]. This is a continuous process throughout the project life cycle [Ref 7].
ii. **Control Scope:** Manage changes to the project scope, ensuring only approved changes are implemented.
iii. **Control Schedule and Costs:** Monitor actual performance against the baseline schedule and budget.
iv. **Perform Quality Control:** Verify that deliverables meet specified quality criteria.
v.  **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Continually engage with stakeholders and manage their expectations [Ref 6].
vi. **Monitor Risks:** Track identified risks, identify new risks, and evaluate the effectiveness of risk responses [Ref 9]. Optimize risk responses as needed [Ref 6, Ref 9].
vii. **Manage Changes:** Process change requests, obtain approvals, and update baselines as necessary [Ref 2].
viii. **Report Performance:** Measure and report project performance to stakeholders [Ref 3].

#### V. Project Closure
i.  **Final Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the final software product from stakeholders.
ii. **Contract Closure:** Close out all project contracts.
iii. **Project Documentation Archiving:** Archive all project documents and organizational process assets.
iv. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a post-project review to identify successes, failures, and best practices for future projects.
v.  **Release Project Team:** Formally release project resources.
vi. **Formal Project Closure:** Obtain formal sign-off that the project is complete.

### Roles

i.  **Project Sponsor:** The person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable project success [Ref 10]. Provides overall project governance and strategic direction [Ref 8].
ii. **Project Manager (PM):** Responsible for coordinating activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 10]. Leads the project team, manages the project lifecycle [Ref 7, Ref 8], and ensures adherence to the plan and standards.
iii. **Development Lead:** Oversees the software development team, ensures technical quality, and guides coding practices.
iv. **Quality Assurance (QA) Lead:** Responsible for defining and executing testing strategies, ensuring the software meets functional, non-functional, and quality requirements [Ref 6].
v.  **Business Analyst (BA):** Bridges the gap between business stakeholders and the development team, translating business needs into detailed requirements.
vi. **Key Stakeholders:** Persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, or be affected by the project [Ref 10]. This includes end-users, regulatory bodies, and operational staff. Effective engagement with stakeholders is crucial [Ref 6].
vii. **Project Team Members:** Individuals responsible for performing the project work, contributing to a collaborative environment [Ref 6].

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points throughout the project life cycle [Ref 7, Ref 8], where project governance principles are applied to authorize and direct the project [Ref 8]. These gates ensure that the project is justified, viable, and aligned with strategic objectives [Ref 15].

i.  **Initiation Gate (After Project Initiation Phase):**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm the project's strategic alignment, feasibility, and readiness to proceed to detailed planning.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for Planning. Approval of Project Charter and initial Business Case by the Project Sponsor and relevant governance bodies [Ref 8, Ref 15].
    *   **Evidence:** Approved Project Charter, Stakeholder Register, High-level Business Case.

ii. **Planning Gate (After Project Planning Phase):**
    *   **Purpose:** To ensure that a comprehensive and realistic plan is in place before significant investment in execution.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for Execution. Approval of Project Management Plan, including detailed scope, schedule, budget, and risk management plan [Ref 2, Ref 9].
    *   **Evidence:** Approved Project Management Plan (PMP), WBS [Ref 4], Detailed Schedule, Budget, Risk Register.

iii. **Development Completion Gate (After Core Development and Initial Testing):**
    *   **Purpose:** To verify that the software development is functionally complete and has passed initial quality checks, ready for user acceptance testing (UAT).
    *   **Decision:** Proceed to UAT. Approval that development work and internal QA are complete [Ref 6].
    *   **Evidence:** Internal QA Report, Traceability Matrix, Completed Code Review Reports, System Test Results.

iv. **Deployment Gate (After User Acceptance Testing):**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm that the software meets user requirements and quality standards, and is ready for deployment.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for Deployment. Formal acceptance by key stakeholders of the solution [Ref 6].
    *   **Evidence:** UAT Sign-off, Training Completion, Deployment Plan, Go-Live Checklist.

v.  **Closure Gate (After Project Closure Phase):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally conclude the project and ensure all administrative and contractual obligations are met.
    *   **Decision:** Formal Project Closure. Approval that all project objectives are met and deliverables accepted [Ref 10, Ref 13].
    *   **Evidence:** Final Project Report, Lessons Learned Report, Stakeholder Acceptance, Contract Closure documents.
