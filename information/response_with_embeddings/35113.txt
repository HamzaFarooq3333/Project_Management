### Process Recommendation: IT Infrastructure Project (ISO Compliant)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a tailored project management approach for a small-scale IT infrastructure project within an IT organization, adhering to ISO standards [Ref 3]. Given the project's size (under 6 months), a simplified, iterative approach is suggested, focusing on clear communication, proactive risk management, and stakeholder engagement [Ref 2, Ref 10]. The process emphasizes adaptability and tailoring [Ref 10] while ensuring alignment with the organization's governance principles [Ref 7]. This recommendation utilizes elements from PMBOK and ISO standards.

### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation Phase**

i.   **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's purpose, objectives, and success criteria. Identify key stakeholders and their expectations [Ref 9]. Establish high-level scope and constraints.
ii.  **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify all stakeholders and their potential impact on the project [Ref 5, Ref 9].
iii. **Project Sponsor Identification:** Identify a sponsor [Ref 9]

II. **Planning Phase**

i.   **Scope Definition:** Develop a detailed project scope statement, including deliverables, acceptance criteria, and exclusions [Ref 1, Ref 5]. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project into manageable tasks [Ref 5].
ii.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and allocate necessary resources (personnel, equipment, software) [Ref 1, Ref 13].
iii. **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule with defined milestones and dependencies using appropriate estimating techniques [Ref 1].
iv.  **Budgeting:** Develop a project budget based on resource requirements and schedule [Ref 1].
v.   **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities), assess their likelihood and impact, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 12, Ref 5].
vi.  **Communication Planning:** Define communication channels, frequency, and responsibilities to ensure effective stakeholder engagement [Ref 1].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** Plan any necessary procurements of equipment or services [Ref 1].

III. **Execution Phase**

i.   **Task Execution:** Execute project tasks according to the project schedule and scope.
ii.  **Resource Management:** Manage resources effectively to ensure timely completion of tasks [Ref 13].
iii. **Communication Management:** Implement the communication plan to keep stakeholders informed of project progress and issues [Ref 1].
iv.  **Issue Management:** Identify, analyze, and resolve project issues promptly [Ref 7].
v.   **Risk Management:** Monitor and manage identified risks, implement mitigation strategies, and identify and address new risks as they arise [Ref 12].
vi.  **Change Management:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget using a defined change management process [Ref 1].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling Phase**

i.   **Performance Measurement:** Monitor project performance against planned baselines (scope, schedule, cost) [Ref 1, Ref 2].
ii.  **Variance Analysis:** Analyze variances between planned and actual performance and identify corrective actions.
iii. **Reporting:** Provide regular project status reports to stakeholders [Ref 1].
iv.  **Quality Control:** Ensure that project deliverables meet defined quality standards [Ref 10].

V. **Closure Phase**

i.   **Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
ii.  **Project Documentation Archiving:** Archive all project documentation (plans, reports, lessons learned).
iii. **Final Reporting:** Prepare a final project report summarizing project outcomes and lessons learned.
iv.  **Project Team Release:** Release project team members and formally close out the project.

### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, secures resources, and approves key decisions [Ref 9].
*   **Project Manager:** Plans, executes, monitors, and controls the project to achieve its objectives [Ref 13].
*   **Technical Lead:** Provides technical expertise and guidance to the project team.
*   **Project Team Members:** Execute assigned tasks and contribute to project deliverables.
*   **Stakeholders:** Provide input, review deliverables, and approve changes.

### Decision Gates

i.   **Initiation Gate:** Review and approval of the Project Charter. Decision point: proceed with planning or terminate the project.
ii.  **Planning Gate:** Review and approval of the Project Management Plan. Decision point: proceed with execution or revise the plan [Ref 1].
iii. **Execution Gate:** Go/no-go decision to ensure the project is on track regarding scope, schedule, and budget. Decision point: continue execution, adjust plan, or terminate project.
iv.  **Closure Gate:** Acceptance of final deliverables and project closure. Decision point: formally close the project or address outstanding issues.
