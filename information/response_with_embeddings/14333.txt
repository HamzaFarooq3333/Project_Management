This project will follow a phased approach, aligning with the project life cycle concepts described in ISO 21502 [Ref 2, Ref 14]. #### II. Phases and Activities

The project will be structured into the following phases:

**I. Initiation**

i.  Project Charter Development: Define the project's objectives, scope, stakeholders, and high-level requirements.  This aligns with defining the project scope [Ref 1].
ii.  Stakeholder Analysis: Identify and analyze all stakeholders, their interests, and influence levels. This supports stakeholder engagement as stated by PMBOK [Ref 6].
iii.  High-Level Requirements Gathering: Capture initial requirements and define project deliverables.
iv.  Risk Identification and Initial Assessment: Identify potential risks and assess their impact and probability [Ref 8].
v.  Develop Communication Plan: Define communication methods, frequency, and stakeholders [Ref 1].
vi. Set up Project Governance Structure: Establish project governance principles and procedures [Ref 13, Ref 14].
**II. Planning**

i.  Detailed Scope Definition: Develop a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
ii.  Schedule Development: Create a realistic project schedule, considering dependencies and resource availability [Ref 1].
iii. Budget Development: Establish a detailed budget, accounting for all project costs [Ref 1].
iv.  Resource Planning: Identify and allocate necessary resources (personnel, equipment, etc.).
v.  Procurement Planning: Determine the need for external procurement and develop a procurement plan [Ref 1].
vi.  Risk Management Plan: Refine risk assessments, develop mitigation strategies, and establish contingency plans [Ref 8].
vii.  Quality Management Planning: Define quality standards, processes, and metrics.
viii. Develop Configuration Management Plan.

**III. Execution**

i.  Execute Work Packages: Carry out the activities outlined in the WBS.
ii.  Manage Project Team: Lead and manage the project team, ensuring effective communication and collaboration [Ref 6].
iii.  Procurement Management: Manage procurement activities, including vendor selection and contract administration.
iv.  Quality Assurance: Conduct quality checks and ensure deliverables meet defined standards.
v.  Risk Management: Implement risk mitigation strategies and monitor risk triggers.
vi.  Issue Management: Address and resolve issues promptly.
vii. Change Management: Implement the change management process [Ref 1].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling**

i.  Performance Monitoring: Track project progress against the plan, using key performance indicators (KPIs).
ii.  Schedule Control: Manage and control the project schedule.
iii.  Cost Control: Monitor and control project costs [Ref 1].
iv.  Quality Control: Monitor the quality of project deliverables.
v.  Risk Monitoring: Track and update the risk register.
vi.  Change Control: Manage and approve/reject change requests [Ref 1].
vii.  Communication Management: Distribute project information to stakeholders per the communication plan [Ref 1].

**V. Closure**

i.  Project Deliverables Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables.
ii.  Final Reporting: Prepare and distribute a final project report.
iii.  Lessons Learned: Conduct a lessons learned session to identify best practices and areas for improvement.
iv.  Contract Closure: Close out any procurement contracts.
v.  Document Archiving: Archive all project documentation.
