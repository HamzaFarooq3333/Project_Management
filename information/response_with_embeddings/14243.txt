**III. Execution**

i.  **Project Kick-off Meeting:** Officially launch the project with all stakeholders.
ii.  **Work Package Execution:** Execute the planned activities within each work package [Ref 4].
iii.  **Monitoring & Controlling:** Track project progress, manage risks, and control changes [Ref 6].
iv.  **Communication & Reporting:** Regularly communicate project status to stakeholders, providing updates on progress, risks, and issues [Ref 3].

#### 5. Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project where decisions are made to proceed to the next phase or take corrective action. These gates help to align the project with stakeholder expectations and the overall project goals [Ref 6].

*   **Initiation Phase Gate:** Approval of the Project Charter. [Ref 6]
*   **Planning Phase Gate:** Approval of the Project Management Plan, including Scope, Schedule, Budget, Communication Plan, Risk Management Plan, Procurement Plan, Change Management Plan, and Quality Management Plan [Ref 3].
*   **Execution Phase Gate:** Approval to move to the next set of work packages or scope of work.
*   **Monitoring & Controlling Phase Gate:** Review of performance metrics and change request approvals.
*   **Closing Phase Gate:** Final acceptance of deliverables and project closure approval.
