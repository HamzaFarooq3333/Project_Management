### Phases and Activities

The project will proceed through five distinct phases, each with defined activities and outputs, guided by the principles of project management standards.

#### I. Project Initiation
This phase establishes the project's foundation and gains formal authorization.
i. Define Project Objectives and Scope (High-Level): Establish what the "temporary endeavour" [Ref 6] aims to achieve.
ii. Identify and Engage Key Stakeholders: Recognize individuals, groups, or organizations "that has interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of a project" [Ref 6], [Ref 5].
iii. Appoint Project Manager and Project Sponsor: Secure the "person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success" [Ref 6] and the individual responsible for project direction.
iv. Conduct Preliminary Risk Identification: Identify potential "threat risk occurrence that would have a negative impact" [Ref 5], [Ref 13].
v. Develop and Obtain Approval for Project Charter: Authorize the project, outlining its purpose, objectives, high-level scope, and preliminary resources. This aligns with "project governance principles, policies and procedures by which a project... is authorized" [Ref 10].
vi. Identify Project Development Approach and Life Cycle: Determine the appropriate methods for delivery, acknowledging ISO 21502's applicability regardless of "delivery approaches, life cycle model used" [Ref 2], and considering the project's research nature.

#### II. Project Planning
This phase details how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed, forming a comprehensive Project Management Plan.
i. Define Detailed Product and Project Scope: Elaborate on "Product scope - Project scope" [Ref 1], ensuring a clear understanding of what the research will deliver and what work is required.
ii. Create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): Decompose the defined scope into "progressively lower levels consisting of elements of work" [Ref 5], down to work packages.
iii. Define, Sequence, and Estimate Activities: Determine all activities required, their logical order, and estimate durations and resources [Ref 1] for "Estimating - Scheduling".
iv. Develop Project Schedule: Create a timeline based on activity estimates, sequences, and resource assignments [Ref 1].
v. Plan Resources (Human, Material, Equipment): Identify and plan for "Team composition" [Ref 1] and other necessary resources.
vi. Develop Project Budget: Create a detailed cost estimate and allocate funds [Ref 1] for "Budget".
vii. Develop Risk Management Plan: Outline strategies for identifying, analyzing, planning responses to, and monitoring project risks, addressing "threat risk occurrence" [Ref 5], [Ref 13].
viii. Establish Communication Plan: Define how information will be exchanged among stakeholders [Ref 1].
ix. Plan for Procurement: Determine if any external goods or services are needed and how they will be acquired [Ref 1].
x. Plan Quality Management: Define quality standards and activities to ensure research outcomes meet expectations.
xi. Develop Change Management Plan: Establish procedures for managing changes to the project baseline [Ref 1].
xii. Finalize Project Management Plan: Integrate all subsidiary plans into a cohesive document, which provides the blueprint for the project's management [Ref 14].

#### III. Project Execution (Research & Development)
This phase involves carrying out the planned work to achieve the project's objectives.
i. Direct and Manage Project Work: Perform the activities defined in the Project Management Plan, coordinating project team members and other resources [Ref 4], [Ref 14].
ii. Acquire, Develop, and Manage Project Team: Assemble and support the research team, fostering collaboration and managing performance [Ref 1].
iii. Implement Research Activities: Conduct the core research, experimentation, and analysis as per the plan.
iv. Manage Stakeholder Engagement: Continuously communicate and work with stakeholders, managing their expectations and involvement [Ref 4], [Ref 5], [Ref 6].
v. Conduct Procurements: Execute procurement activities as per the plan [Ref 1].
vi. Implement Quality Assurance Activities: Ensure that the research processes and intermediate outputs conform to quality standards.

#### IV. Monitoring and Controlling
This phase oversees all project activities to ensure they remain on track and addresses deviations.
i. Monitor and Control Project Work: Track actual progress against the plan, taking corrective action as needed [Ref 14].
ii. Perform Integrated Change Control: Review all change requests, approve changes, and manage implementation of approved changes [Ref 1] for "Change management".
iii. Validate and Control Scope: Ensure that all planned scope is being delivered and manage any deviations from the agreed scope [Ref 1].
iv. Control Schedule: Monitor schedule performance, identify variances, and manage the project timeline [Ref 1].
v. Control Costs: Monitor budget performance, track expenditures, and manage project finances [Ref 1].
vi. Control Quality: Monitor specific project results to determine if they comply with relevant quality standards and identify ways to eliminate causes of unsatisfactory performance [Ref 4].
vii. Monitor Risks: Track identified risks, identify new risks, and evaluate risk management effectiveness [Ref 5], [Ref 13].
viii. Report Performance: Collect and distribute performance information, including status reports, progress measurements, and forecasts [Ref 4].

#### V. Project Closure
This phase formally concludes the project or project phase.
i. Close Project or Phase: Finalize all activities across all process groups to formally complete the project or a project phase [Ref 7], [Ref 14].
ii. Obtain Final Acceptance of Deliverables: Secure formal acceptance from stakeholders for all "unique and verifiable element that is required to be produced by a project" [Ref 11].
iii. Conduct Post-Mortem Analysis and Lessons Learned: Review project successes and failures, documenting insights for future projects.
iv. Release Project Team Members: Transition team members to other assignments.
v. Archive Project Documentation: Organize and store all project records, reports, and intellectual property generated.

### Roles

Key roles are essential for clear responsibilities and successful project delivery, addressing potential "ambiguity regarding who is responsible for what" [Ref 3].

i. **Project Sponsor**: The individual responsible for obtaining resources and making executive decisions, providing overall project direction and support [Ref 6].
ii. **Project Manager**: Accountable for project planning, execution, monitoring, control, and closure, integrating all project management practices [Ref 8], [Ref 14].
iii. **Research Lead**: Responsible for the technical direction and quality of the research work, guiding the technical team.
iv. **Technical/Research Team**: Individuals performing the actual research, development, and analytical tasks [Ref 4], [Ref 8].
v. **Key Stakeholders**: Individuals, groups, or organizations whose interests may be affected by the project, including end-users, subject matter experts, and regulatory bodies [Ref 4], [Ref 5], [Ref 6].

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points where project progress and viability are formally reviewed, leading to a go/no-go decision. These align with project governance principles and procedures [Ref 10].

i. **Gate 1: Project Charter Approval (After Initiation Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To formally authorize the project and allocate initial resources.
    *   **Review**: Project Charter, high-level scope, objectives, initial risk assessment.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor approves to proceed to planning. This is a key point where "project governance principles, policies and procedures by which a project (3.20) is authorized" [Ref 10] are applied.

ii. **Gate 2: Project Management Plan Approval (After Planning Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To obtain formal approval for the detailed project plan, committing resources and setting baselines.
    *   **Review**: Detailed Project Management Plan (including scope, schedule, budget, risk, communication plans).
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor, with input from Key Stakeholders, approves to proceed to execution. This validates the integrated planning approach [Ref 14].

iii. **Gate 3: Research Phase Completion / Intermediate Deliverable Acceptance (Mid-Execution)**
    *   **Purpose**: To review progress on research outcomes, validate key findings, and confirm the project is on track to meet its objectives or adapt if necessary.
    *   **Review**: Key research deliverables, progress reports, quality metrics, updated risk assessments.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor and key stakeholders formally accept intermediate "deliverable unique and verifiable element" [Ref 11], and approve continuation to the final stages of the project or make necessary adjustments.

iv. **Gate 4: Project Closure Approval (After Closure Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To formally close the project, confirm all objectives are met, and ensure all administrative and contractual obligations are fulfilled.
    *   **Review**: Final deliverables, lessons learned, stakeholder acceptance, financial closure.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor formally closes the project, enabling "project closure" [Ref 7] and resource release.
