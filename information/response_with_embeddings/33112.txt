### Process Recommendation: Small-Scale IT Research Project (PRINCE2)

This process recommendation outlines a PRINCE2-based project management approach for a small-scale (< 6 months) IT research project. It leverages relevant PM standards (PMBOK, ISO21502, and PRINCE2) to ensure a structured and controlled project execution. PRINCE2 emphasizes control, planning, and communication which are critical even in smaller projects [Ref 3]. Given the small size, tailoring of PRINCE2 is essential to avoid unnecessary overhead [Ref 7].

-----

#### Phases and Activities

This project will be structured into the following PRINCE2 phases, tailored for a small research project:

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU)**

i.   **Appoint the Project Management Team:** Define roles and responsibilities [Ref 3].
ii.  **Create the Project Brief:** Outline the project's purpose, scope, objectives, and benefits [Ref 3]. This is essential for aligning stakeholder expectations and avoiding ambiguity [Ref 3].
iii. **Define the Project Approach:** Decide on the specific research methodologies and technologies to be used.
iv.  **Plan the Initiation Stage:** Determine the activities and resources needed to initiate the project properly.

II. **Initiating a Project (IP)**

i.   **Refine the Project Brief into a Project Initiation Document (PID):** Elaborate on the project's scope, objectives, benefits, risks, and controls. This should include a detailed plan covering product scope, project scope, estimating, scheduling, budget, team composition, communication plan, procurement, change management and measurement [Ref 1].
ii.  **Define Project Controls:** Establish procedures for managing risks, issues, and changes.
iii. **Create detailed plan.** This includes scope, estimating, scheduling, budget, team composition, communication plan, procurement, change management, and measurement [Ref 1].
iv.  **Secure Project Resources:** Confirm the availability of necessary resources (personnel, equipment, data).

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS)**

i.   **Authorize Work Packages:** Assign specific tasks to team members.
ii.  **Monitor Progress:** Track progress against the project plan [Ref 4] and address any deviations. Measurement of project performance is essential [Ref 4].
iii. **Manage Issues and Risks:** Identify, assess, and respond to any issues or risks that arise.
iv.  **Report Progress:** Communicate project status to stakeholders through regular updates. Stakeholder engagement is key [Ref 4].

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP)**

i.   **Execute Research Activities:** Conduct the planned research activities, adhering to the defined methodologies.
ii.  **Develop Deliverables:** Create the research reports, data analysis, and other project deliverables.
iii. **Quality Review:** Ensure deliverables meet the defined quality standards. Building quality into the process is important [Ref 15].

V. **Managing a Stage Boundary (SB)**

i.   **Review Stage Performance:** Assess the performance of the current stage against the plan.
ii.  **Update Project Plan:** Revise the project plan based on the stage's performance and any changes in the environment.
iii. **Plan the Next Stage:** Develop a detailed plan for the next stage of the project.
iv.  **Seek approval to proceed.**

VI. **Closing a Project (CP)**

i.   **Handover Deliverables:** Formally deliver the project deliverables to the stakeholders.
ii.  **Evaluate Project Performance:** Assess the project's overall performance against the objectives and identify lessons learned.
iii. **Disseminate Research Findings:** Publish or present the research findings to the relevant audience.
iv.  **Archive Project Documentation:** Store all project documentation for future reference.
v.  **Confirm objectives were achieved.**

-----

#### Roles

The following key roles are required for this project:

i.   **Executive (Project Sponsor):** Provides overall direction and resources [Ref 6].
ii.  **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the research findings.
iii. **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those supplying the resources (researchers).
iv.  **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, and controlling the project [Ref 10].
v.   **Research Team Member:** Conducts the research activities and develops the deliverables.

#### Decision Gates

The following are the key decision gates within the project lifecycle:

i.   **End of Starting Up a Project:** Decision on whether to proceed to the Initiation Stage. The Project Board (Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier) must approve the Project Brief [Ref 3].
ii.  **End of Initiating a Project:** Decision on whether to proceed to the Execution Stage.  The Project Board must approve the Project Initiation Document (PID) [Ref 3].
iii. **End of Each Stage:** The Project Board reviews the stage performance and approves the plan for the next stage. This ensures the project remains aligned with the objectives and the benefits are still achievable.
iv.  **Project Closure:** The Project Board approves the project closure and confirms that all objectives have been met.
