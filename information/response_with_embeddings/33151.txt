### Process Recommendation: Small-Scale Education Research Project (PMBOK)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a streamlined project management approach for a small-scale education research project, expected to be completed within six months, following the PMBOK Guide framework [Ref 2, Ref 5]. This approach is tailored for organizations in the education industry and aims to provide a clear and manageable process for achieving research objectives. It acknowledges that project management is the application of methods, tools, techniques, and competencies to enable the project to meet its objectives [Ref 3]. The process emphasizes effective stakeholder engagement [Ref 8], clear planning [Ref 5], and adaptability [Ref 8] to ensure project success. As mentioned in ISO21502, this guidance is applicable to any organization, including public, private and charitable, as well as to any type of project [Ref 1].

-----

### Phases and Activities

This project will be managed using the following phases. All planning stages include considerations for quality as per [Ref 8].

#### I. Initiation

i. **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's purpose, objectives, scope, and stakeholders. Identify the Project Sponsor who is responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success [Ref 6].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify all stakeholders impacted by or who can impact the project [Ref 2].
iii. **High-Level Risk Assessment:** Identify potential risks and constraints [Ref 8, Ref 11].
iv. **Develop a high level Project Management Plan**
-----

#### II. Planning

i. **Scope Definition:** Define the project scope statement, outlining deliverables, acceptance criteria, and exclusions. Include both project and product scope [Ref 5]. This involves decomposing the defined scope into a work breakdown structure [Ref 4].
ii. **Schedule Development:** Create a detailed project schedule with tasks, dependencies, durations, and milestones.
iii. **Resource Planning:** Determine the resources required (personnel, equipment, materials) and their availability.
iv. **Budget Development:** Estimate the costs for each activity and develop a comprehensive project budget [Ref 5].
v. **Communication Planning:** Define communication channels, frequency, and responsible parties for disseminating project information.
vi. **Risk Management Planning:** Identify, analyze, and plan responses to potential risks [Ref 8, Ref 11].
vii. **Quality Planning:** Determine quality standards and processes for ensuring project deliverables meet requirements [Ref 8].
viii. **Procurement Planning (if applicable):** Define procurement needs and plan for acquiring external resources or services.

-----

#### III. Execution

i. **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute tasks according to the project management plan.
ii. **Manage Project Team:** Lead, motivate, and coordinate the project team.
iii. **Manage Communications:** Disseminate project information to stakeholders [Ref 8].
iv. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Maintain relationships with stakeholders and address their concerns.
v. **Implement Approved Changes:** Manage change requests and incorporate approved changes into the project.
vi. **Quality Assurance:** Conduct quality audits and inspections to ensure deliverables meet quality standards.
-----

#### IV. Monitoring and Controlling

i. **Monitor Project Performance:** Track progress against the project plan, schedule, and budget [Ref 2].
ii. **Control Scope:** Manage scope changes and ensure that only approved changes are incorporated.
iii. **Control Schedule:** Monitor schedule performance and take corrective actions to address delays.
iv. **Control Costs:** Monitor budget performance and take corrective actions to address cost overruns.
v. **Control Risks:** Monitor risks and implement risk response plans.
vi. **Control Quality:** Verify that deliverables meet quality requirements [Ref 8].
vii. **Performance Reporting:** Prepare and distribute regular project status reports to stakeholders.
viii. **Change Control:** Address ambiguities related to what the project will deliver, costs, and when [Ref 3].

-----

#### V. Closing

i. **Project Closeout:** Formally close the project and obtain final acceptance of deliverables.
ii. **Contract Closure:** Finalize all contracts and agreements with external vendors.
iii. **Administrative Closure:** Collect and archive project documentation, lessons learned, and other relevant information.
iv. **Lessons Learned:** Document the lessons learned during the project to improve future projects.
v. **Release Project Team:** Release project team members and reassign them to other projects.

-----

### Roles

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support for the project [Ref 6].
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project.
iii. **Research Team Members:** Conduct research activities and contribute to project deliverables.
iv. **Stakeholders:** Provide input and feedback on project requirements and deliverables.

### Decision Gates

i. **End of Initiation:** Approval to proceed with detailed planning based on the Project Charter.
ii. **End of Planning:** Approval of the Project Management Plan and budget before commencing execution [Ref 5].
iii. **Mid-Project Review:** Assessment of progress, risks, and potential changes needed based on project performance [Ref 2].
iv. **Pre-Closure Review:** Verification that all deliverables have been completed and accepted prior to project closure.
