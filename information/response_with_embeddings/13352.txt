#### II. Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be divided into the following phases:

**I. Project Initiation:**

i.  **Project Mandate Review:** Review and validate the project mandate, defining the project's purpose, objectives, and high-level requirements. This sets the stage for the project [Ref 3].
ii.  **Project Initiation Documentation (PID) Development:** Create the PID, which will contain project scope, objectives, approach, roles, responsibilities, and key deliverables. This includes the product scope and the project scope [Ref 6].
iii.  **Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify and analyze stakeholders, documenting their interests, expectations, and influence on the project [Ref 2]. This is critical to effectively engage with stakeholders [Ref 9].
iv.  **Risk Identification:** Conduct an initial risk assessment to identify potential threats and opportunities [Ref 12, Ref 19].
v.  **Project Governance Setup:** Define the project governance structure, including roles, responsibilities, and decision-making processes.  This sets the principles, policies, and procedures by which a project is authorized and directed [Ref 8].

**II. Planning:**

i.  **Scope Definition:** Define the detailed project scope, including deliverables, acceptance criteria, and constraints. Scope will be broken down into a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
ii.  **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and resource allocation. This will consider the "planning" domain [Ref 6].
iii.  **Budget Development:** Develop a comprehensive project budget, including all costs associated with resources, materials, and activities [Ref 6].
iv.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and secure the necessary resources (personnel, equipment, facilities) for the project [Ref 3].
v.  **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan outlining how information will be shared among stakeholders [Ref 6].
vi.  **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a detailed risk management plan to identify, assess, and mitigate potential risks [Ref 6].
vii. **Quality Management Planning:** Establish quality standards and processes to ensure deliverables meet requirements. This is key to building quality into processes [Ref 9].
viii. **Change Management Planning:** Develop a change management plan for handling changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 6].

**III. Execution:**

i.  **Work Package Delivery:** Execute the planned activities within defined work packages [Ref 4], ensuring adherence to quality standards and project plans.
ii.  **Resource Management:** Manage and allocate resources effectively, monitoring their performance and addressing any issues [Ref 3].
iii.  **Communication and Reporting:** Regularly communicate project status, progress, and issues to stakeholders according to the communication plan [Ref 6].
iv.  **Risk Management:** Implement the risk management plan, monitoring risks, and taking corrective actions as needed. This includes optimizing risk responses [Ref 9].
v.  **Issue Management:** Identify, document, and resolve project issues promptly [Ref 11].
vi.  **Change Management:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget, following the change management plan [Ref 6].
vii. **Product Scope and Project Scope Implementation** - The project scope is defined [Ref 6].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling:**

i.  **Progress Tracking:** Monitor project progress against the baseline schedule and budget. Use appropriate metrics for measurement [Ref 2].
ii.  **Performance Reporting:** Generate performance reports, highlighting variances from the plan and providing insights into project performance [Ref 2].
iii.  **Issue and Risk Management:** Continuously monitor and manage risks and issues, escalating as necessary. This includes recognizing, evaluating, and responding to system interactions [Ref 9].
iv.  **Quality Assurance:** Conduct quality assurance activities to verify that deliverables meet requirements [Ref 9].
v.  **Change Control:** Manage change requests, assessing their impact on the project, and obtaining approvals as needed [Ref 6].

**V. Project Closure:**

i.  **Project Deliverables Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables from the stakeholders.
ii.  **Final Project Report:** Prepare a final project report summarizing project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations for future projects.
iii.  **Project Documentation Archival:** Archive all project documentation for future reference.
iv.  **Project Closure Meeting:** Conduct a project closure meeting to officially close the project, release resources, and celebrate successes.

#### III. Roles

i.  **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for providing resources and executive decisions to enable success [Ref 5, Ref 17].
ii.  **Project Manager:** Responsible for the overall planning, execution, monitoring, and closure of the project [Ref 13]. The project manager applies methods, tools, techniques, and competencies [Ref 3].
iii.  **Project Team:** Responsible for executing the tasks and activities required to deliver the project's outputs.
iv.  **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with an interest in the project [Ref 5].
v.  **Project Board:** A body that provides direction and support to the Project Manager, ensuring the project aligns with strategic objectives.
vi. **Quality Assurance:** Ensure quality standards are being adhered to.

#### V. Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project where the project's progress and direction are reviewed, and decisions are made regarding whether to proceed.

i.  **Project Initiation Approval:** Before proceeding with the project, the project sponsor and/or Project Board must approve the PID [Ref 3].
ii.  **Planning Phase Approval:** Approval of the complete project plan (scope, schedule, budget, resources) is required before execution can begin.
iii.  **Execution Phase Gate Review:** Regular review of project progress, risk assessments, and issue management to evaluate performance and make corrections or adjustments as needed.
iv.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** Formal acceptance of deliverables is required from the stakeholder before project closure.
v.  **Project Closure Approval:** Final approval from the Project Board to formally close the project after all deliverables are accepted, and the final report is approved.
