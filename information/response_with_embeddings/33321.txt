This document outlines a comprehensive project management process for a large-scale construction research project (duration > 18 months), adhering to the PMBOK framework. The process emphasizes stakeholder engagement, thorough planning, risk management, and adaptable execution. **2. Phases and Activities**

The project will be executed in the following phases, with activities aligned to PMBOK knowledge areas and ISO guidance:

I. **Initiation:**
 i.  Define Project Scope: Clearly outline the project objectives, deliverables, and boundaries [Ref 2].
 ii. Identify Stakeholders: Determine all individuals or groups who have an interest in the project [Ref 9]. Create a stakeholder register [Ref 1].
 iii. Develop Project Charter: Formally authorize the project, defining the project manager's authority and overall objectives.
 iv. Secure Project Sponsorship: Obtain commitment from the project sponsor regarding resource availability and decision-making support [Ref 9].

II. **Planning:**
 i. Develop Project Management Plan: Integrate all subsidiary plans (scope, schedule, cost, quality, resource, communication, risk, procurement, stakeholder) into a cohesive document [Ref 2].
 ii. Define Scope Baseline: Create a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 5].
 iii. Create Schedule Baseline: Develop a project schedule, including activity sequencing, resource allocation, and critical path analysis.
 iv. Develop Cost Baseline: Establish a budget for the project, including cost estimation, allocation, and control.
 v. Perform Risk Assessment: Identify potential risks and develop mitigation strategies.
 vi. Develop Communication Plan: Define how project information will be communicated to stakeholders.
vii. Determine team composition [Ref 2]

III. **Execution:**
 i. Direct and Manage Project Work: Execute the tasks outlined in the project management plan.
 ii. Manage Project Team: Lead and motivate the project team to achieve project objectives.
 iii. Implement Quality Assurance: Ensure that project deliverables meet the required quality standards.
 iv. Manage Communications: Disseminate project information to stakeholders as defined in the communication plan.
 v. Manage Stakeholder Engagement: Actively engage with stakeholders to address their concerns and manage their expectations [Ref 11].
vi. Manage procurement [Ref 2]

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:**
 i. Monitor Project Progress: Track project performance against the project management plan.
 ii. Control Scope: Manage changes to the project scope and ensure that only approved changes are incorporated.
 iii. Control Schedule: Monitor the project schedule and take corrective action to keep the project on track.
 iv. Control Costs: Manage project costs to stay within the approved budget.
 v. Control Quality: Verify that project deliverables meet the required quality standards.
 vi. Manage Risks: Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies.
vii. Change Management [Ref 2]
viii. Measurement [Ref 2]

V. **Closure:**
 i. Finalize Project Deliverables: Ensure that all project deliverables are complete and meet the acceptance criteria.
 ii. Obtain Formal Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the stakeholders.
 iii. Document Lessons Learned: Capture the lessons learned during the project to improve future projects.
 iv. Close Out Contracts: Finalize all contracts with vendors and suppliers.
 v. Release Project Resources: Release project team members and other resources.

**3. Roles**

Key project roles and responsibilities include:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, resources, and support for the project [Ref 9].
*   **Project Manager:** Leads and manages the project team, ensuring that the project is completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.
*   **Project Team:** Executes the project tasks as defined in the project management plan.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project and can influence its outcome [Ref 1, Ref 9].
*   **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):** Provide specialized knowledge and expertise to the project team.

**5. Decision Gates**

Critical decision gates for this project include:

i. **End of Initiation Phase:** Approval to proceed to the Planning Phase. Requires sign-off on the Project Charter and Stakeholder Register.
ii. **End of Planning Phase:** Approval to proceed to the Execution Phase. Requires sign-off on the Project Management Plan, including all baselines (scope, schedule, cost).
iii. **Mid-Project Review (after 9 months):** Review progress against the plan, re-evaluate risks, and make necessary adjustments. This gate verifies ongoing project viability and alignment with objectives. Requires approval to proceed with the second half of the project.
iv. **End of Monitoring and Controlling Phase:** Approval to proceed to Closure Phase. Verifies all requirements are met per the scope baseline.
v. **End of Closure Phase:** Formal project closure and sign-off. Requires sign-off on all deliverables and completion of the lessons learned documentation.
