### Process Summary

This recommendation outlines a PRINCE2-aligned project management process for a medium-scale healthcare infrastructure project with an estimated duration of 6-18 months. The process emphasizes a structured approach to planning, execution, monitoring, and control, ensuring the project meets its objectives within scope, budget, and schedule constraints. The process acknowledges the importance of stakeholder engagement, risk management, and adaptability, as highlighted in both PRINCE2 and PMBOK ([Ref 4], [Ref 6], [Ref 7], [Ref 11], [Ref 12]). This process is tailored for the healthcare industry, considering its unique regulatory requirements and safety concerns. The project lifecycle will be managed through distinct stages with defined decision gates to ensure continued alignment with strategic goals.

### Phases and Activities

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU)**

i. Define the Project Mandate: Establish the initial reason and authority for the project.
ii. Appoint the Project Management Team: Assign roles and responsibilities.
iii. Develop the Project Brief: Create a high-level overview of the project, including objectives, scope, and constraints.
iv. Plan the Initiation Stage: Define the activities required to thoroughly plan the project.

II. **Initiating a Project (IP)**

i. Refine the Business Case: Develop a detailed justification for the project, including benefits, costs, and risks.
ii. Define the Project Approach: Select the most appropriate delivery approach (e.g., predictive, iterative) [Ref 15].
iii. Design the Project Management Team Structure and Roles: Clarify roles, responsibilities, and reporting lines.
iv. Plan Project Quality: Define quality criteria and assurance activities.
v. Plan Project Communication: Establish communication channels and protocols [Ref 1].
vi. Plan Project Risk Management: Identify, assess, and plan responses to potential risks [Ref 10].
vii. Create the Project Initiation Documentation (PID): Compile all planning outputs into a comprehensive document.

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS)**

i. Authorize Work Packages: Assign specific tasks to team members.
ii. Monitor Progress: Track progress against the project plan [Ref 2].
iii. Manage Issues and Risks: Address any problems or threats that arise [Ref 10], [Ref 11].
iv. Report Progress: Provide regular updates to stakeholders [Ref 1].
v. Take Corrective Action: Implement changes to address deviations from the plan.

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP)**

i. Accept a Work Package: Receive and review assigned tasks.
ii. Execute Work Package: Perform the activities outlined in the work package.
iii. Deliver Work Package: Submit completed deliverables for review.

V. **Managing Stage Boundaries (SB)**

i. Plan the Next Stage: Develop a detailed plan for the upcoming stage.
ii. Update the Project Plan: Incorporate any changes or lessons learned.
iii. Update the Business Case: Reassess the project's justification based on current performance.
iv. Report Stage End: Provide a summary of the stage's performance.

VI. **Directing a Project (DP)**

i. Authorize Initiation: Approve the project initiation documentation.
ii. Authorize Stage: Approve the stage plan.
iii. Give Ad Hoc Direction: Provide guidance and support to the Project Manager as needed.
iv. Authorize Project Closure: Approve the project closure report.

VII. **Closing a Project (CP)**

i. Prepare Planned Closure:  Ensure all products have been delivered and accepted.
ii. Prepare Premature Closure:  Document reasons for early termination if necessary.
iii. Evaluate the Project:  Conduct a lessons learned review.
iv. Recommend Project Closure:  Seek formal approval to close the project.

### Roles

*   **Project Board:** Provides overall direction and control of the project.
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's outputs.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those who will provide the resources to the project.
*   **Executive:** Ultimately accountable for the project’s success.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project.
*   **Team Manager:** Manages a team of specialists delivering project outputs.
*   **Project Assurance:** Ensures that the project is being run correctly, providing an independent check [Ref 3].
*   **Change Authority:**  The individual or group responsible for approving or rejecting change requests.

### Decision Gates

i. **End of Starting Up a Project:** Approval to proceed to the Initiation Stage. Requires a review of the Project Brief and the planned Initiation Stage activities.
ii. **End of Initiating a Project:** Approval to proceed to the first delivery stage. Requires review and acceptance of the Project Initiation Documentation (PID) by the Project Board [Ref 8].
iii. **End of Each Delivery Stage:** Approval to proceed to the next stage. Requires review of the Stage End Report, updated Project Plan, and updated Business Case [Ref 7].  The Project Board must confirm that the project remains viable and aligned with strategic objectives.
iv. **Project Closure:** Approval to formally close the project. Requires review and acceptance of the Project Closure Report and confirmation that all project objectives have been met.
