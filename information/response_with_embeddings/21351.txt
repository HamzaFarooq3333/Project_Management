### Roles

i.  **Project Sponsor:** Person responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions to enable project success [Ref 6]. Provides high-level direction and support.
ii. **Project Manager:** Coordinates activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 6], integrating practices to initiate, plan, execute, monitor, control, and close the project [Ref 15].
iii. **Business Analyst:** Responsible for eliciting, analyzing, communicating, and validating requirements for changes to business processes, policies, and information systems.
iv. **Software Architect:** Designs the overall structure and components of the software system.
v.  **Development Lead:** Oversees the software development team, ensuring technical quality and adherence to architecture.
vi. **Software Developers:** Design, code, and test software components.
vii. **Quality Assurance Lead (QA Lead):** Manages the testing process and ensures software quality.
viii. **Testers:** Execute test plans, identify defects, and verify fixes.
ix. **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs - Education):** Provide expertise on educational content, pedagogical approaches, and user requirements for the target audience.
x.  **Stakeholders (End-users, Leadership, IT Support):** Individuals or groups with interests in, or affected by, the project [Ref 3], [Ref 6].

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points to ensure the project remains aligned with objectives and has the necessary approvals to proceed. Project governance provides the "principles, policies and procedures by which a project is authorized and directed" [Ref 12].

i.  **Project Authorization Gate (End of Initiating Phase):**
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for detailed planning.
    *   **Evidence:** Approved Project Charter, confirmed high-level objectives and business case.
    *   **Stakeholders:** Project Sponsor (Accountable), Steering Committee (Consulted/Informed).
ii. **Baseline Approval Gate (End of Planning Phase):**
    *   **Decision:** Approve the comprehensive Project Management Plan, establishing baselines for scope, schedule, and cost.
    *   **Evidence:** Approved PMP, including detailed scope, WBS [Ref 3], schedule, budget [Ref 4], and risk management plan.
    *   **Stakeholders:** Project Sponsor (Accountable), Project Manager (Responsible), Steering Committee (Consulted).
iii. **Phase Completion Gates (Throughout Executing Phase):**
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go to proceed to the next major development increment or phase.
    *   **Evidence:** Completed phase deliverables, quality reports, updated risk logs, resource availability.
    *   **Stakeholders:** Project Manager (Accountable), Project Sponsor (Consulted/Informed), Key Stakeholders (Consulted for acceptance).
iv. **User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Completion Gate (Before Final Deployment):**
    *   **Decision:** Readiness for final deployment/rollout based on user validation.
    *   **Evidence:** Signed-off UAT reports, confirmation that the software meets user requirements.
    *   **Stakeholders:** Education SMEs (Accountable for acceptance), Project Manager (Responsible), Project Sponsor (Informed).
v.  **Project Closure Gate (End of Closing Phase):**
    *   **Decision:** Formal closure of the project and transition to operations.
    *   **Evidence:** Final product acceptance, lessons learned documentation, closed contracts, formal project closure report.
    *   **Stakeholders:** Project Sponsor (Accountable), Project Manager (Responsible), Steering Committee (Informed).
