#### 2. Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will consist of the following phases and activities:

I. **Initiation**

   i. Define Project Scope: Clearly articulate the research objectives, deliverables, and project boundaries. This aligns with the emphasis on scope definition in PMBOK [Ref 1].
   ii. Identify Stakeholders: Identify and document all stakeholders and their needs and expectations, ensuring effective engagement throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 5].
   iii. Develop Project Charter: Formalize the project's authorization, objectives, and high-level requirements.
   iv. Secure Sponsor Approval: Obtain the sponsor's commitment and support for the project. The sponsor is responsible for obtaining resources and making executive decisions [Ref 6].
   v. Define initial project plan, including deliverables, timelines and budget [Ref 1].

II. **Planning**

   i. Scope Management: Develop a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope [Ref 5].
   ii. Schedule Management: Create a realistic project schedule, considering dependencies and resource availability [Ref 1].
   iii. Budget Management: Develop a detailed budget, accounting for all anticipated costs [Ref 1].
   iv. Resource Management: Identify and allocate the necessary resources (personnel, equipment, software, etc.).
   v. Quality Management: Define the quality standards and processes for the research project.
   vi. Communication Plan: Establish a communication plan to ensure timely and effective information sharing with stakeholders [Ref 1].
   vii. Risk Management: Identify potential risks, assess their impact, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 13].
   viii. Procurement Plan (if applicable): Plan for any external procurement needs.
   ix. Establish Measurement criteria. Consider tailoring the methodology to the specific context [Ref 15].

III. **Execution**

   i. Perform Research Activities: Conduct the research according to the project plan.
   ii. Manage Resources: Efficiently manage project resources and resolve resource conflicts.
   iii. Manage Stakeholder Engagement: Maintain open communication and actively manage stakeholder expectations.
   iv. Monitor and Control: Continuously monitor project progress, track performance against the plan, and implement corrective actions as needed. Project management integrates the practices included in this document to direct, initiate, plan, monitor, control and close the project [Ref 10].
   v. Change Management: Implement the change management process to handle changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 1].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**

   i.  Track performance using established metrics [Ref 1].
   ii.  Regular performance reviews: Conduct regular performance reviews against the project plan.
   iii.  Issue Management: Address any issues, risks, or conflicts promptly.
   iv.  Change Management: Implement the change management process to handle any deviation from the plan.
   v.  Deliverables should be unique and verifiable [Ref 11].

V. **Closing**

   i. Project Completion: Verify that all project deliverables are completed and accepted.
   ii. Final Documentation: Prepare the final project report, documenting the project's outcomes, lessons learned, and recommendations.
   iii. Stakeholder Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the stakeholders.
   iv. Project Closure: Formally close the project, ensuring all contracts are closed out and resources are released.

#### 3. Roles

*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for overall project planning, execution, monitoring, and closing. They will be actively leading the project throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 9].
*   **Sponsor:** Provides project funding, guidance, and high-level decision-making. The sponsor is responsible for obtaining the resources [Ref 6].
*   **Research Team:** Responsible for conducting the research activities and delivering the project deliverables.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by the project or who can influence it [Ref 5]. Their input and feedback are crucial throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 4].
*   **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):** Provide technical expertise and guidance on specific research areas.
