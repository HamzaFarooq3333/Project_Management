### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation Phase:**
    i.  Define the project objectives and scope at a high level, ensuring alignment with healthcare strategic goals [Ref 1].
    ii. Identify key stakeholders and their needs, creating a stakeholder register [Ref 2].
    iii. Develop a project charter outlining the project's purpose, objectives, high-level requirements, and initial budget [Ref 12].
    iv. Appoint a project manager and assemble the core project team [Ref 1].
    v. Conduct a feasibility study to assess the project's viability and potential risks [Ref 10].

II. **Planning Phase:**
    i.  Develop a detailed project scope statement, defining project deliverables and acceptance criteria [Ref 1].
    ii. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project into manageable tasks [Ref 5].
    iii. Estimate resources (human, equipment, materials) required for each task [Ref 1].
    iv. Develop a project schedule using techniques like critical path method [Ref 1].
    v. Create a comprehensive budget, including contingency reserves for unforeseen costs [Ref 1].
    vi. Develop a risk management plan, identifying potential risks and mitigation strategies [Ref 10].
    vii. Develop a communication plan, outlining how project information will be disseminated to stakeholders [Ref 1].
    viii. Develop a procurement plan, outlining the process for acquiring goods and services from external vendors [Ref 1].
    ix.  Define quality standards and develop a quality management plan [Ref 4].

III. **Execution Phase:**
    i.  Implement the project plan, managing tasks according to the schedule and budget [Ref 2].
    ii. Manage the project team, providing leadership and guidance [Ref 4].
    iii. Manage communications, ensuring that stakeholders are informed of project progress [Ref 4].
    iv. Procure goods and services according to the procurement plan [Ref 1].
    v. Monitor and control project risks, implementing mitigation strategies as needed [Ref 10].
    vi. Ensure adherence to quality standards, conducting regular quality audits [Ref 4].
    vii. Manage change requests, following a defined change management process [Ref 1].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling Phase:**
    i.  Track project progress against the schedule and budget, using performance metrics [Ref 2].
    ii. Monitor and control project risks, implementing mitigation strategies as needed [Ref 10].
    iii. Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget [Ref 1].
    iv. Communicate project status to stakeholders [Ref 4].
    v. Identify and resolve project issues [Ref 9].
    vi. Conduct regular project reviews to assess progress and identify areas for improvement [Ref 1].
    vii. Measure project performance and take corrective actions as needed [Ref 1].

V. **Closure Phase:**
    i.  Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders [Ref 2].
    ii. Document project lessons learned [Ref 7].
    iii. Archive project documentation [Ref 7].
    iv. Close out contracts with vendors [Ref 1].
    v. Release project resources [Ref 6].
    vi. Communicate project closure to stakeholders [Ref 4].
    vii. Conduct a post-project review to assess project success and identify areas for improvement [Ref 7].

### Decision Gates

i.  **Project Charter Approval:** Approval of the project charter by the project sponsor, signaling formal authorization to proceed to the planning phase [Ref 12].
ii. **Baseline Plan Approval:** Approval of the project management plan (including scope, schedule, and budget baselines) by key stakeholders, signifying agreement on the project's approach [Ref 1].
iii. **Go/No-Go Decisions:** Regular decision points throughout the execution phase to assess project progress and determine whether to continue, modify, or terminate the project [Ref 4].
iv. **Phase Gate Review at Project Closing:** Approve the final deliverable. Stakeholder and SME signoff [Ref 2].
