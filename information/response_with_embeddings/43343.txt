i.  **Project Sponsor:** The person responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions, enabling project success, and providing overall direction and support [Ref 7, Ref 15].
ii. **Steering Committee / Project Governance Board:** The body responsible for project governance, providing high-level guidance, strategic alignment, and oversight [Ref 8, Ref 10, Ref 12].
iii. **Project Manager:** The individual responsible for the day-to-day management, coordination, and control of the project to achieve agreed objectives [Ref 7, Ref 14, Ref 15].
iv. **Research Lead / Subject Matter Expert (SME):** Provides technical expertise and guidance on the finance research content, methodology, and analysis.
v. **Project Team Members:** Individuals responsible for executing specific tasks and producing deliverables, including researchers, analysts, and support staff [Ref 2, Ref 3, Ref 11, Ref 14].
vi. **Quality Assurance (QA) Specialist:** Ensures that processes and deliverables meet defined quality standards [Ref 11].
vii. **Stakeholders:** Any person, group, or organization that has interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, the project [Ref 2, Ref 4, Ref 7, Ref 11].

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project lifecycle where formal reviews occur, and decisions are made whether to proceed to the next phase, re-plan, or terminate the project. These gates are essential for maintaining project governance and control [Ref 8, Ref 12].

i.  **Project Authorization Gate (End of Phase I - Initiation):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally authorize the project to proceed to detailed planning.
    *   **Decision Criteria:** Clear project objectives, identified stakeholders, defined high-level scope, estimated budget/timeline ranges, and a signed Project Charter.
    *   **Decision Maker:** Project Sponsor and Steering Committee [Ref 7, Ref 8].

ii. **Baseline Approval Gate (End of Phase II - Planning):**
    *   **Purpose:** To approve the comprehensive project management plan, including the detailed scope, schedule, budget, risk management plan, and quality plan. This establishes the project baselines.
    *   **Decision Criteria:** Approved WBS, detailed schedule, approved budget, comprehensive risk register, communication plan, and resource plan.
    *   **Decision Maker:** Project Sponsor and Steering Committee [Ref 8, Ref 12].

iii. **Major Deliverable Review Gates (During Phase III - Execution):**
    *   **Purpose:** To review and formally accept key interim research deliverables or milestones, ensuring quality and alignment with objectives before proceeding with subsequent work.
    *   **Decision Criteria:** Completion of specified work packages, adherence to quality standards, and stakeholder review.
    *   **Decision Maker:** Project Manager, Research Lead, and relevant Stakeholders (e.g., end-users of research) with approval from the Sponsor [Ref 12].

iv. **Final Deliverable Acceptance Gate (End of Phase III - Execution):**
    *   **Purpose:** To obtain formal acceptance of all final research outputs and products, confirming they meet defined requirements and quality standards.
    *   **Decision Criteria:** All final deliverables completed, verified, and validated against acceptance criteria.
    *   **Decision Maker:** Project Sponsor and Key Stakeholders [Ref 9, Ref 12].

v. **Project Closure Gate (End of Phase V - Closure):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally close the project, confirm all administrative and contractual obligations are met, and release resources.
    *   **Decision Criteria:** All deliverables accepted, financial closure, lessons learned captured, and resources released.
    *   **Decision Maker:** Project Sponsor and Steering Committee [Ref 8, Ref 9, Ref 12].
