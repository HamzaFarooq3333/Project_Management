This approach encompasses the core project management principles outlined in PMBOK, focusing on planning, execution, monitoring and controlling, and closure. Furthermore, it considers the importance of tailoring the approach based on the project context [Ref 12], adapting to the specific needs of the IT marketing landscape. The processes should be aligned with the industry best practices while focusing on achieving the defined project objectives. The project team will use methods, tools, techniques, and competencies to enable the project to meet its objectives [Ref 4].

#### 2. Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation**

   a. **Develop Project Charter:** Define the project's purpose, objectives, high-level scope, and key stakeholders. Ensure alignment with organizational strategy.
   b. **Identify Stakeholders:** Identify all relevant stakeholders, their interests, and their influence on the project [Ref 2, 7].
   c. **Define High-Level Requirements:** Capture initial project requirements and create a preliminary scope statement.
   d. **Determine Project Feasibility:** Evaluate the technical, economic, and operational feasibility of the project.

II. **Planning** (Considered a significant area in the project lifecycle, [Ref 1])

   a. **Develop Project Management Plan:** Create a comprehensive project management plan including the scope, schedule, budget, risk management, communication, and procurement plans [Ref 1].
   b. **Define Scope:** Create a detailed work breakdown structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope [Ref 5].
   c. **Develop Schedule:** Estimate activity durations, define dependencies, and create a project schedule.
   d. **Develop Budget:** Determine the project budget based on resource requirements, cost estimations, and contingency planning.
   e. **Develop Communication Plan:** Define how project information will be communicated to stakeholders [Ref 1].
   f. **Identify Risks and Plan Risk Responses:** Identify potential risks, assess their impact, and plan mitigation strategies [Ref 15].
   g. **Develop Procurement Plan:** If applicable, define the procurement process for external resources or services [Ref 1].
   h. **Team Composition:** Determine the resources needed to compose the project team [Ref 1].

III. **Execution**

   a. **Manage Project Team:** Lead and manage the project team, ensuring they have the necessary resources and support.
   b. **Perform Planned Activities:** Execute the planned activities as defined in the project management plan.
   c. **Manage Stakeholder Expectations:** Communicate with stakeholders, manage their expectations, and address any issues or concerns.
   d. **Procure Resources:** Procure the necessary resources as outlined in the procurement plan.

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**

   a. **Monitor and Control Project Work:** Track project performance against the plan, identify variances, and implement corrective actions.
   b. **Perform Integrated Change Control:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget [Ref 1].
   c. **Manage Risks:** Monitor identified risks, track their status, and implement risk response plans.
   d. **Control Schedule:** Monitor progress against the schedule and implement corrective actions as needed.
   e. **Control Costs:** Monitor project costs and implement cost control measures.
   f. **Control Quality:** Ensure the project deliverables meet the required quality standards.

V. **Closure**

   a. **Close Project or Phase:** Formally close the project or phase.
   b. **Obtain Stakeholder Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from stakeholders.
   c. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document lessons learned and share them with the organization for future projects.
