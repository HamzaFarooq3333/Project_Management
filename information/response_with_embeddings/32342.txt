#### 2. Phases and Activities

This project will be structured into distinct phases, each with specific activities and deliverables, overseen by decision gates. The lifecycle will follow a structured approach to project delivery and closure [Ref 6].

**I. Project Initiation:**

i.  **Define Project Objectives and Scope:** Clearly define the project's objectives, scope, and success criteria. Establish the project's Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4]. This should align with stakeholder expectations and industry standards.
ii.  **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify all stakeholders and analyze their interests and influence [Ref 1, Ref 7, Ref 18].
iii.  **Risk Assessment and Management Plan:** Identify and assess potential risks, and develop a risk management plan [Ref 13].
iv.  **Project Management Plan Development:** Develop the overarching project management plan, encompassing all aspects including scope, schedule, budget, quality, resources, communication, and change management [Ref 2].
v.  **Project Board Establishment:** Appoint the Project Board and define their roles and responsibilities.
vi.  **Project Initiation Document (PID) Creation:** Create the Project Initiation Document (PID) that encompasses the project scope, objectives, approach, plan, risks, etc.

**II. Project Planning:**

i.  **Detailed Scope Definition:** Refine and decompose the project scope into detailed requirements and deliverables [Ref 4].
ii.  **Scheduling and Resource Allocation:** Develop a detailed project schedule, allocating resources effectively.
iii.  **Budget Development:** Create a comprehensive project budget, including all costs.
iv.  **Communication Plan:** Develop a detailed communication plan to keep stakeholders informed [Ref 2].
v.  **Quality Management Plan:** Define quality standards and processes.
vi.  **Procurement Planning:** Plan for procurement needs and identify potential vendors.

**III. Project Execution (Phased Approach):**

i.  **Construction Activities:** Execute construction activities according to the approved plans.
ii.  **Progress Monitoring and Reporting:** Regularly monitor progress, track milestones, and generate reports.
iii.  **Issue and Change Management:** Manage issues and changes effectively.
iv.  **Quality Control:** Conduct quality control checks and inspections.
v.  **Risk Management:** Continuously monitor and manage risks.

**IV. Project Closure:**

i.  **Project Deliverables Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all deliverables.
ii.  **Final Project Documentation:** Complete and archive all project documentation.
iii.  **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a lessons-learned review to identify successes and areas for improvement.
iv.  **Project Evaluation:** Evaluate the project's performance against its objectives.
v.  **Project Closure Report:** Produce and distribute the Project Closure Report.

*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for day-to-day management of the project, leading the project team, and ensuring the project delivers its objectives.
*   **Project Team:** Responsible for performing the tasks as defined in the project plan.

#### 5. Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial checkpoints to assess the project's progress and make informed decisions, ensuring the project aligns with its objectives and stakeholder expectations. Each gate involves a review of project progress, risk assessment, and validation of the business case.

i.  **Project Initiation Approval:** Before proceeding with detailed planning [Ref 6]. Key decision: Does the project have a viable business case, and is it worth proceeding?
ii.  **Stage Boundaries:** (Throughout execution, often corresponding to specific construction phases) Review project performance, assess risks, approve stage plans for the subsequent phase, and authorize continued project work.
iii.  **Project Closure Approval:** Final review of deliverables, ensuring all objectives are met and the project is formally closed [Ref 6]. Key decision: Has the project met its objectives and is it ready for closeout?
iv.  **Change Requests Evaluation:** Evaluation and approval of change requests to keep project scope and budget in-line.
