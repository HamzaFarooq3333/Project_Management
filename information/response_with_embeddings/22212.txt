### Phases and Activities

The project will follow the seven PRINCE2 processes, adapted and enriched with practical activities derived from PMBOK and ISO standards.

#### I. Starting Up a Project (SU)
i.  **Appoint Executive & Project Manager:** The Project Board Executive and Project Manager are appointed, establishing initial accountability [Ref 4], [Ref 8]. The Executive is responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 8].
ii. **Capture Previous Lessons:** Reviewing lessons from similar IT construction projects to inform the current project's approach.
iii. **Design & Appoint Project Management Team:** Identify and appoint key roles including Senior User, Senior Supplier, and Team Managers, ensuring clarity of responsibility [Ref 4].
iv. **Prepare Outline Business Case:** Develop a preliminary justification for the project, aligning it with organizational strategy [Ref 13].
v. **Select Project Approach & Assemble Project Brief:** Define the overall strategy and high-level project scope, including requirements and an initial plan for team composition [Ref 1].
vi. **Plan Initiation Stage:** Detail the activities and resources required for the subsequent Initiation Stage.

#### II. Directing a Project (DP)
i.  **Authorize Initiation:** The Project Board reviews the Project Brief and authorizes the Project Manager to proceed with the Initiation Stage [Ref 11].
ii. **Authorize the Project:** At the end of the Initiation Stage, the Project Board approves the Project Initiation Documentation (PID) and authorizes the project to proceed to its first delivery stage [Ref 11].
iii. **Authorize a Stage or Exception Plan:** The Project Board grants approval for each subsequent delivery stage or any exception plans.
iv. **Give Ad-hoc Direction:** The Project Board provides ongoing guidance and makes decisions as required throughout the project lifecycle.
v. **Authorize Project Closure:** The Project Board confirms successful completion and formal closure of the project [Ref 6]. This phase encompasses the principles, policies, and framework by which the project is directed and controlled [Ref 7], [Ref 11].

#### III. Initiating a Project (IP)
i.  **Plan the Project:** Develop a comprehensive Project Plan encompassing product scope, project scope, estimating, scheduling, budget, team composition, communication plan, procurement, and change management [Ref 1].
ii. **Refine the Business Case:** Detail the financial and qualitative justification for the project.
iii. **Establish Project Controls:** Define how the project will be monitored and controlled, including performance measurement [Ref 2].
iv. **Create Project Management Strategies:** Develop strategies for Risk Management (identifying threats and opportunities [Ref 3], [Ref 15]), Quality Management (building quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 14]), Configuration Management, and Communication Management [Ref 1].
v. **Set Up Project Files:** Establish the project's information management system.
vi. **Assemble Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Consolidate all plans and strategies into the PID, which serves as the project's foundational document.
vii. **Identify & Engage Stakeholders:** Thoroughly identify all persons, groups, or organizations that can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by the project, and plan for their effective engagement [Ref 2], [Ref 3], [Ref 8], [Ref 14].

#### IV. Controlling a Stage (CS)
i.  **Authorize a Work Package:** The Project Manager assigns work to Team Managers, detailing the scope and expected deliverables [Ref 3].
ii. **Review Work Package Status:** Monitor progress of work packages against defined criteria.
iii. **Review Stage Status:** Regularly assess the overall health and progress of the current stage against the Stage Plan and Project Plan.
iv. **Report Highlights:** Provide regular progress reports to the Project Board.
v. **Take Corrective Action:** Address deviations from the plan and manage issues and risks as they arise [Ref 7]. This phase focuses on the day-to-day management of project work and its measurement [Ref 2].

#### V. Managing Product Delivery (MP)
i.  **Accept, Execute & Deliver Work Package:** Team Managers accept work packages, execute the defined activities, and deliver the specified products.
ii. **Implement Quality Controls:** Ensure that products are developed and reviewed according to defined quality standards [Ref 14].
iii. **Report Checkpoint Information:** Provide regular updates on work package progress to the Project Manager.
iv. **Acquire & Manage Resources:** Ensure necessary resources (human, technical, material) are available and optimized for efficient delivery [Ref 1]. This phase directly supports the "Delivery" performance domain [Ref 2].

#### VI. Managing a Stage Boundary (SB)
i.  **Plan the Next Stage:** Prepare a detailed plan for the upcoming stage.
ii. **Update the Project Plan:** Revise the overall Project Plan based on actual performance and forecasts.
iii. **Update the Business Case:** Re-evaluate the project's continued viability and justification.
iv. **Report Stage End:** Provide a comprehensive report to the Project Board on the performance of the completed stage, including lessons learned.
v. **Produce an Exception Plan (if needed):** If the stage deviates significantly from plan, propose an exception plan for Project Board approval. This phase involves critical planning and measurement activities [Ref 1], [Ref 2], ensuring alignment across the project life cycle [Ref 11].

#### VII. Closing a Project (CP)
i.  **Decommission Project Assets:** Secure and reallocate project resources and assets (e.g., IT infrastructure, tools).
ii. **Hand Over Products:** Ensure all final deliverables are formally handed over to the operational environment and accepted by the Senior User [Ref 7].
iii. **Evaluate Project:** Conduct a post-project review to assess project performance against objectives and benefits.
iv. **Recommend Project Closure:** The Project Manager recommends formal project closure to the Project Board [Ref 6].
v. **Compile Lessons Learned Report:** Document insights gained for future projects.
vi. **Prepare Follow-on Actions:** Identify any outstanding actions required post-project for benefits realization.
vii. **Inform All Stakeholders:** Notify relevant stakeholders of the project's closure.

### Roles

Based on PRINCE2, augmented by PMBOK and ISO principles, the key project roles are:

i.  **Executive:** Ultimately accountable for the project's success, ensuring it delivers value and remains aligned with organizational objectives [Ref 8], [Ref 14]. Represents the Business Case.
ii. **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's products or who will be affected by them. Responsible for defining user needs and confirming acceptance [Ref 8].
iii. **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those who provide the specialist resources and expertise (internal or external) needed to create the project's products.
iv. **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project, including planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing it [Ref 8]. Manages the various project performance domains [Ref 2].
v. **Team Manager:** Responsible for managing a team to create their products as defined in a Work Package.
vi. **Project Assurance:** Monitors the project on behalf of the Project Board, ensuring compliance with standards and addressing quality, business, and user aspects.
vii. **Project Support:** Provides administrative and project management support functions to the Project Manager and Project Board.
viii. **Stakeholders:** A broader term encompassing anyone with an interest in or affected by the project, requiring effective engagement [Ref 2], [Ref 8], [Ref 14].

### Decision Gates

Crucial decision gates ensure project viability, control, and successful progression, directly linked to PRINCE2's management by stages principle and project governance [Ref 11].

i.  **Authorization to Initiate:** Occurs at the end of the Starting Up a Project process. The Project Board reviews the Project Brief and outline Business Case to decide whether to authorize the Project Manager to proceed with the Initiation Stage. This ensures initial justification and alignment [Ref 11], [Ref 13].
ii. **Authorization of the Project:** Occurs at the end of the Initiating a Project process. The Project Board reviews the Project Initiation Documentation (PID) and updated Business Case to decide whether to authorize the project to proceed to its first delivery stage. This is a critical point for committing resources and formalizing the project [Ref 11], [Ref 13].
iii. **Authorization to Proceed to Next Stage:** Occurs at the end of each Management a Stage Boundary process. The Project Board reviews the End Stage Report, the updated Project Plan and Business Case, and the Next Stage Plan to decide whether to authorize progression to the subsequent stage. This maintains continuous business justification and control [Ref 11], [Ref 13].
iv. **Authorization of Exception Plan:** Occurs whenever an exception is raised due to a significant deviation from the Stage Plan. The Project Board reviews the Exception Report and proposed Exception Plan to decide on corrective action, ensuring deviations are formally managed [Ref 11].
v. **Authorization to Close the Project:** Occurs at the end of the Closing a Project process. The Project Board reviews the End Project Report and Lessons Learned Report to formally confirm that the project has delivered its objectives and can be closed [Ref 6], [Ref 11], [Ref 13].
