#### 2. Phases and Activities

This project will be organized into the following phases:

I. **Initiation:** Define the project and get it authorized.

   i.  **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's purpose, objectives, scope, and high-level requirements.  This aligns with the need for defining project objectives [Ref 6].
   ii. **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify all stakeholders [Ref 1], their interests, and their influence on the project.
   iii. **Risk Assessment (High-Level):**  Identify potential risks [Ref 8, Ref 15] and opportunities early on.
   iv. **Budget and Schedule Establishment (High-Level):** Create a preliminary budget and schedule to estimate project feasibility.  This includes estimating and scheduling activities [Ref 3].

II. **Planning:** Elaborate on the project scope and develop detailed plans.

   i.  **Scope Definition:** Define the detailed scope of the marketing campaign, deliverables, and any work breakdown structure required [Ref 4].
   ii. **Detailed Requirements Gathering:** Define detailed requirements for all marketing activities, messaging, and target audience.
   iii. **Detailed Budgeting and Scheduling:** Refine the budget and schedule with specific activities, resources, and dependencies [Ref 3].
   iv. **Communication Plan Development:** Create a communication plan to manage stakeholder expectations and information flow [Ref 3].
   v. **Risk Management Plan:** Develop a detailed risk management plan to identify, analyze, and mitigate project risks [Ref 15].
   vi. **Procurement Plan (If Applicable):** If external vendors are required, define the procurement process [Ref 3].
   vii. **Change Management Plan:** Establish a process for managing changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].

III. **Execution:** Carry out the planned marketing activities.

   i.  **Marketing Campaign Implementation:** Execute the marketing activities as defined in the plan.
   ii. **Resource Management:** Manage and allocate resources effectively.
   iii. **Issue and Risk Management:**  Address any issues and mitigate risks as they arise.
   iv. **Change Request Management:**  Manage change requests according to the change management plan.
   v. **Performance Monitoring:** Monitor project performance against the plan, schedule, and budget [Ref 3].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:** Track project performance, manage changes, and ensure the project stays on track.

   i.  **Progress Tracking:**  Track project progress regularly against the schedule and budget.
   ii. **Performance Reporting:**  Generate performance reports for stakeholders based on measurement [Ref 1].
   iii. **Change Control:** Implement the change control process to manage approved changes.
   iv. **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor and manage identified risks and their impacts.

V. **Closure:** Formally close the project and document lessons learned.

   i.  **Final Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables.
   ii. **Project Documentation:** Archive all project documentation.
   iii. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a lessons-learned session to identify areas for improvement in future projects.
   iv. **Final Report:** Prepare a final project report summarizing the project's performance.
