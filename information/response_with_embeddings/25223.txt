#### II. Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will consist of the following phases and associated activities:

**I. Initiation Phase**

i.  **Project Definition & Scope:** Define project objectives, deliverables, and high-level requirements [Ref 3, Ref 5].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify and analyze all stakeholders [Ref 1, Ref 9], defining their needs and expectations.
iii. **Project Charter Development:** Create a project charter that formally authorizes the project and outlines the initial scope, objectives, and high-level requirements.
iv. **Preliminary Risk Assessment:** Identify potential risks and opportunities [Ref 13], initiating the risk management process.
v. **Team Assembly:** Assemble the project team, defining roles and responsibilities.

**II. Planning Phase**

i.  **Detailed Scope Definition:** Develop a detailed work breakdown structure (WBS) [Ref 5] and define activities and tasks.
ii. **Scheduling:** Develop a detailed project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and resource allocation.
iii. **Budgeting:** Develop a detailed budget, including cost estimates for all project activities.
iv. **Resource Planning:** Identify and secure required resources (labor, materials, equipment).
v.  **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan outlining how information will be shared among stakeholders [Ref 2].
vi. **Procurement Planning:** Develop a procurement plan identifying the necessary goods and services to be procured [Ref 2].
vii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a detailed risk management plan [Ref 13], including risk identification, analysis, response planning, and monitoring.
viii. **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards and processes to ensure the project meets the required quality expectations.
ix. **Change Management Planning:** Define a change management process to control and manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget [Ref 2].

**III. Execution Phase**

i.  **Project Execution:** Execute planned activities according to the project plan.
ii. **Resource Management:** Manage and monitor resources to ensure efficient allocation and utilization.
iii. **Team Management:** Lead and manage the project team, fostering collaboration and communication [Ref 8].
iv. **Communication Management:** Distribute project information according to the communication plan.
v.  **Procurement Management:** Manage procurement activities, including vendor selection, contract administration, and invoice processing.
vi. **Risk Response Implementation:** Implement planned risk responses to mitigate threats and capitalize on opportunities [Ref 13].
vii. **Issue Management:** Address and resolve project issues promptly.
viii. **Change Request Processing:** Manage and process change requests according to the change management plan [Ref 2].
ix. **Quality Assurance:** Conduct quality assurance activities to ensure that project deliverables meet quality standards.

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling Phase**

i.  **Performance Monitoring:** Track project performance against the plan, including schedule, cost, and scope.
ii. **Progress Reporting:** Generate regular progress reports to communicate project status to stakeholders.
iii. **Variance Analysis:** Analyze variances between planned and actual performance and take corrective actions as needed.
iv. **Risk Monitoring:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as required [Ref 13].
v.  **Change Control:** Manage and control changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget.
vi. **Quality Control:** Conduct quality control activities to ensure deliverables meet the required quality standards.

**V. Closure Phase**

i.  **Project Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the client.
ii. **Contract Closure:** Close out all contracts with vendors and suppliers.
iii. **Final Reporting:** Prepare a final project report summarizing project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations.
iv. **Documentation:** Archive all project documentation.
v.  **Team Dissolution:** Release the project team members.
vi. **Post-Project Review:** Conduct a post-project review to identify lessons learned and best practices for future projects.

#### III. Roles

Key project roles include:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Person responsible for obtaining resources and making executive decisions to enable success [Ref 9].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for managing the project and achieving its objectives.
*   **Project Team:** Individuals contributing to the project tasks.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or organizations who have interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by the project [Ref 9].
*   **Contractors/Vendors:** External entities providing goods or services.
*   **Quality Assurance Manager:** Ensures quality standards are met.
*   **Risk Manager:** Manages the identification, assessment, and mitigation of project risks.
