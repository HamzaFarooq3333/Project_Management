### Phases and Activities

The project will follow a phased approach, largely aligning with PRINCE2 processes, ensuring comprehensive coverage from initiation to closure.

#### I. Pre-Project (Corresponding to PRINCE2: Starting Up a Project)

i.  **Appoint Project Management Team:** Formalize the roles of the Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier, and Project Manager. This establishes clear accountability from the outset [Ref 5, Ref 6].
ii. **Capture Previous Lessons:** Review lessons from similar projects to inform initial planning and risk assessment.
iii. **Design and Appoint Project Management Team:** Ensure that the right people are in the right roles with clear responsibilities [Ref 5].
iv. **Prepare Project Brief:** Develop an initial outline of the project's objectives, desired outcomes, scope, and initial business justification. This forms the basis for project authorization [Ref 13].
v.  **Identify Project Stakeholders:** Thoroughly identify all individuals, groups, or organizations who may be affected by or perceive themselves to be affected by the project [Ref 3, Ref 6]. This aligns with PMBOK's Stakeholder Performance Domain [Ref 2].
vi. **Outline Business Case:** Develop a preliminary business case justifying the project's existence and expected benefits.
vii. **Select Project Approach and Assemble Project Team:** Determine the high-level delivery approach (e.g., iterative development within PRINCE2 stages) and identify initial team composition [Ref 2, Ref 4].

#### II. Initiation (Corresponding to PRINCE2: Initiating a Project)

i.  **Develop Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Create a comprehensive PID, integrating project plan, business case, risk management strategy, quality management strategy, change control strategy, and communication management strategy. This defines the project baseline.
ii. **Define Project Scope:** Clearly articulate the product scope and project scope, including what is in and out of scope, leveraging concepts of Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and work packages for decomposition [Ref 3, Ref 4].
iii. **Detailed Planning:** Develop detailed plans for scheduling, budgeting, and resource allocation for the entire project and the first stage [Ref 4, Ref 15].
iv. **Establish Project Governance:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project is authorized and directed, ensuring alignment with organizational objectives [Ref 12].
v.  **Develop Risk Management Strategy:** Identify, analyze, plan responses, and establish a process for monitoring risks, including threats and opportunities, aligning with PMBOK's Uncertainty Performance Domain and ISO's definitions of threat [Ref 3, Ref 9, Ref 10].
vi. **Develop Communication Plan:** Define how, when, and by whom project information will be communicated to stakeholders [Ref 4].
vii. **Tailor PRINCE2 to Context:** Adapt PRINCE2's principles, practices, and processes to fit the specific needs and complexity of this large-scale education software development project [Ref 8, Ref 9].

#### III. Direction (Corresponding to PRINCE2: Directing a Project)

i.  **Authorize Project Initiation:** Project Board reviews the Project Brief and Business Case to authorize the initiation stage.
ii. **Authorize Project:** Project Board reviews the PID and Business Case to authorize the project to proceed and commit resources for the first delivery stage.
iii. **Authorize Stages:** At the end of each stage, the Project Board reviews performance and authorizes progression to the next stage.
iv. **Provide Ad-hoc Direction:** The Project Board provides ongoing guidance and makes strategic decisions throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 11, Ref 15].
v.  **Authorize Project Closure:** The Project Board formally authorizes project closure upon satisfactory completion and handover.

#### IV. Staged Delivery (Corresponding to PRINCE2: Controlling a Stage, Managing Product Delivery, Managing a Stage Boundary)

i.  **Stage Planning:** For each new stage, develop a detailed Stage Plan, including specific work packages, tasks, resources, and schedule [Ref 4].
ii. **Authorize Work Packages:** Team Managers accept and execute work packages, ensuring clear understanding of deliverables, tolerances, and reporting requirements [Ref 3].
iii. **Progress Monitoring and Control:** Continuously monitor progress against the Stage Plan, manage deviations, and take corrective actions. This involves tracking scope, schedule, budget, and quality [Ref 15].
iv. **Quality Assurance:** Implement processes to build quality into processes and deliverables, ensuring the software meets defined requirements and standards [Ref 9].
v.  **Issue and Change Management:** Identify, analyze, and manage issues and changes according to the defined change control strategy [Ref 4, Ref 5].
vi. **Risk Monitoring:** Continuously monitor known risks and identify new ones, implementing planned responses and escalating as necessary [Ref 9, Ref 10].
vii. **Communication:** Regularly report stage progress and exceptions to the Project Board and relevant stakeholders.
viii. **Manage Stage Boundary:** At the end of each stage, review performance, update the Project Plan and Business Case, and prepare the plan for the next stage for Project Board approval.

#### V. Project Closure (Corresponding to PRINCE2: Closing a Project)

i.  **Decommission Project Organization:** Formally disband the project team and release resources.
ii. **Handover Products:** Ensure all final products (software, documentation, training materials) are handed over to the operational team or end-users.
iii. **Evaluate Project Performance:** Assess the project against its original objectives, including the realization of benefits, and document actual performance.
iv. **Lessons Learned:** Facilitate a comprehensive lessons learned exercise, documenting successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
v.  **Formal Project Closure:** Obtain formal sign-off from the Project Board, confirming the project's completion and closure. This concludes the temporary endeavor [Ref 13].

### Roles

Based on PRINCE2 and aligned with general project management best practices:

*   **Executive (Sponsor):** Accountable for the business case and overall project success, ensuring continued business justification. Acts as the ultimate decision-maker for the Project Board [Ref 6, Ref 12].
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the final product and/or those who will be impacted by it. Ensures the solution meets user needs.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those who will provide the resources and expertise to develop the product. Ensures the technical feasibility and quality of the solution.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project within the delegated authority and tolerances set by the Project Board. Manages project work, resources, and motivates the team to achieve objectives [Ref 15].
*   **Team Manager:** Responsible for producing products allocated by the Project Manager. Manages a team of specialists (e.g., software developers, testers).
*   **Project Assurance:** Independent role to monitor the project's performance and compliance on behalf of the Project Board.
*   **Project Support:** Provides administrative and project management support to the Project Manager and Project Team.
*   **Change Authority:** An optional role responsible for reviewing and approving (or rejecting) changes and issues, often a subset of the Project Board.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points where the Project Board (or relevant authority) formally reviews progress, performance, and the business case to decide whether to continue with the project, adjust it, or terminate it. These align with PRINCE2's "Authorize" processes and the concept of a project lifecycle [Ref 12].

i.  **Authorization to Initiate:** Occurs at the end of the Pre-Project phase. The Project Board reviews the Project Brief and Business Case to decide whether to proceed with the detailed initiation phase.
ii. **Authorization to Deliver the Project (End of Initiation):** The Project Board reviews the complete Project Initiation Documentation (PID) and the updated Business Case. This is the main authorization to commit significant resources and proceed with the first delivery stage.
iii. **Authorization to Deliver a Stage (End of Each Stage):** At the end of each delivery stage, the Project Board reviews the Stage End Report, the updated Business Case, and the plan for the next stage. This gate is crucial for maintaining control and ensuring continued business justification throughout the large (>18 months) project [Ref 12].
iv. **Authorization to Close the Project (End of Final Stage):** The Project Board reviews the End Project Report, Lessons Learned Report, and confirms that all products have been handed over and benefits realization plans are in place. This formally closes the project [Ref 8, Ref 13].
