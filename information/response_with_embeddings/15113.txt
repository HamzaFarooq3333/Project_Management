#### II. Phases and Activities

This project will follow the following phases, with associated activities:

**I. Initiation**

i.  **Define Project Objectives and Scope:** Clearly articulate the project's goals, deliverables, and boundaries. This involves understanding the requirements and defining a preliminary scope statement, and Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 5].
ii.  **Identify Stakeholders:** Identify and analyze project stakeholders, their expectations, and influence. This is crucial for effective communication and managing expectations [Ref 2, Ref 9].
iii.  **Develop Project Charter:** Document the project’s high-level objectives, scope, and key stakeholders.
iv.  **Risk Identification:** Begin to identify potential risks and threats [Ref 5, Ref 6] and potential opportunities.

**II. Planning**

i.  **Develop Project Management Plan:** Create a comprehensive plan encompassing scope, schedule, budget [Ref 1], communication, risk management, and procurement (if applicable) [Ref 1].
ii.  **Scope Management:**  Refine the scope and define the deliverables [Ref 7]. Establish a baseline for project scope.
iii. **Schedule Development:** Create a project schedule based on task dependencies and estimated durations.
iv.  **Budgeting:** Develop the project budget, considering resource costs, materials, and other expenses [Ref 1].
v.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and allocate necessary resources (personnel, equipment, etc.) [Ref 1].
vi.  **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan outlining how project information will be shared among stakeholders [Ref 1].
vii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a plan to address identified risks and opportunities [Ref 12].

**III. Execution**

i.  **Execute the Project Plan:** Perform the activities defined in the project management plan.
ii.  **Configure IT Infrastructure:** Implement the planned configuration according to the project specifications.
iii. **Manage Project Team:**  Manage and motivate the project team, ensuring they have the necessary resources and support [Ref 13].
iv.  **Issue Management:** Identify and resolve issues that arise during project execution [Ref 7].
v.  **Risk Response:** Implement planned risk responses to mitigate threats or capitalize on opportunities [Ref 12].
vi.  **Procurement (If Applicable):** Manage vendor contracts and ensure timely delivery of necessary resources (If required).

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling**

i.  **Monitor Project Performance:** Track progress against the plan, measuring schedule, budget, and scope [Ref 1].
ii.  **Performance Reporting:** Generate performance reports to stakeholders, comparing actual progress against planned baselines.
iii.  **Change Management:** Manage changes to scope, schedule, and budget through a formal change control process [Ref 1].
iv.  **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor identified risks, triggering responses if needed.
v. **Quality Assurance:** Ensure that the implemented configurations meet the defined quality standards [Ref 10].

**V. Closure**

i.  **Project Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the deliverables from the stakeholders.
ii.  **Project Documentation:** Finalize all project documentation, including lessons learned, reports, and a project closure report.
iii.  **Finalize Financial Closure:** Settle all financial obligations.
iv.  **Project Closure Meeting:** Conduct a formal project closure meeting, gathering feedback and recognizing team contributions.

#### III. Roles

i.  **Project Manager:** Responsible for overall project management, including planning, execution, monitoring, and closure. Directs and controls the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 9].
ii.  **IT Infrastructure Specialist(s):** Responsible for the technical configuration and implementation of the IT infrastructure.
iii.  **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have interests in or are affected by the project [Ref 9, Ref 18] and the end user of the IT infrastructure.
iv.  **Project Sponsor:** Person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success [Ref 9].
