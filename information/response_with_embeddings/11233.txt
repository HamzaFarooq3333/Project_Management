### II. Phases and Activities

#### I. Initiation Phase
i. **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's purpose, objectives, scope, and high-level requirements.  This will involve identifying stakeholders [Ref 3, Ref 10] and their expectations.
ii. **Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify and analyze stakeholders, documenting their influence and interests. [Ref 3, Ref 10]
iii. **Preliminary Risk Assessment:** Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities) and develop initial mitigation strategies [Ref 9].
iv. **Project Governance Setup:** Establish the project's governance framework including decision-making processes. [Ref 8]

#### II. Planning Phase
i. **Detailed Requirements Gathering:** Define detailed software requirements, including functional and non-functional requirements.
ii. **Scope Definition:** Create a detailed work breakdown structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope [Ref 4].
iii. **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and milestones [Ref 2].
iv. **Budgeting:** Develop a detailed budget, accounting for all project costs [Ref 2].
v. **Resource Planning:** Identify and allocate necessary resources (personnel, equipment, etc.) [Ref 5].
vi. **Communication Plan:** Develop a communication plan outlining how project information will be disseminated [Ref 2].
vii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a comprehensive risk management plan, including risk identification, analysis, response planning, and monitoring. [Ref 9]
viii. **Procurement Planning:** If applicable, plan for the acquisition of external goods or services [Ref 2].
ix. **Quality Management Planning:** Define quality standards and processes to be followed throughout the project.

#### III. Execution Phase
i. **Software Development:** Execute software development activities according to the planned schedule.
ii. **Configuration:** Perform manual configuration tasks as per requirements.
iii. **Testing:** Conduct unit testing, integration testing, system testing, and user acceptance testing (UAT).
iv. **Communication & Reporting:** Execute the communication plan and regularly report project progress to stakeholders [Ref 2].
v. **Issue Management:** Document and resolve any issues or problems that arise. [Ref 13]
vi. **Change Management:** Manage change requests through a defined change control process [Ref 2].
vii. **Risk Response:** Implement risk mitigation plans [Ref 9].

#### IV. Monitoring and Controlling Phase
i. **Progress Monitoring:** Track project progress against the plan (schedule, budget, scope, and quality).
ii. **Performance Reporting:** Generate performance reports to stakeholders.
iii. **Change Control:** Manage and control changes to the project baseline. [Ref 2]
iv. **Risk Monitoring:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies. [Ref 9]
v. **Issue Tracking:** Monitor and resolve all issues in a timely manner. [Ref 13]
vi. **Quality Control:** Monitor deliverables to ensure they meet quality standards.

#### V. Closure Phase
i. **Project Closure:** Formally close the project.
ii. **Final Deliverables:** Ensure all deliverables are complete and accepted by the client.
iii. **Final Documentation:** Prepare and archive all project documentation.
iv. **Post-Project Review:** Conduct a post-project review to identify lessons learned.
v. **Final Reporting:** Prepare and distribute the final project report.
