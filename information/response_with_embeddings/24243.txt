    iv.  Establish a high-level budget and timeline.
    v.  Conduct a preliminary risk assessment to identify potential threats [Ref 4, Ref 8].

II. **Planning**
    i.  Develop a detailed project scope statement, including product and project scope [Ref 3]. This should include a work breakdown structure (WBS) to decompose the project into manageable tasks [Ref 4].
    ii.  Create a detailed schedule, including task dependencies, resource allocation, and a critical path analysis [Ref 3].
    iii.  Develop a comprehensive budget and cost management plan [Ref 3].
    iv.  Create a communication plan to define communication frequency, methods, and stakeholders [Ref 3].
    v.  Develop a risk management plan, including risk identification, assessment, response planning, and monitoring [Ref 3].
    vi.  Define procurement processes if external vendors or resources are required.

III. **Execution**
    i.  Execute the activities outlined in the project management plan.
    ii.  Manage the project team and resources, ensuring effective collaboration and communication [Ref 2].
    iii.  Implement the marketing campaign deliverables, adhering to industry best practices and compliance requirements specific to the finance industry.
    iv.  Monitor project progress against the plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and quality.
    v.  Manage and control changes through a formal change management process [Ref 3].
    vi.  Document all project-related information, changes, and decisions.

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**
    i.  Track project performance metrics (KPIs) against the baseline plan.
    ii.  Conduct regular status meetings to review progress, identify issues, and implement corrective actions.
    iii.  Manage stakeholder expectations and address any concerns.
    iv.  Monitor and control risks, implementing mitigation plans as needed.
    v.  Manage and control changes, ensuring that all changes are properly assessed, approved, and implemented.
    vi. Measure project performance against the defined metrics [Ref 2].

V.  **Closure**
    i.  Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
    ii.  Conduct a post-project review to document lessons learned and identify areas for improvement.
    iii.  Close out contracts and procurements.
    iv.  Archive project documents and records.
    v.  Communicate project closure to stakeholders.
