### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be structured around PRINCE2's seven processes, adapted for a small, <6 month project, ensuring control and rapid progression.

#### I. Starting Up a Project (SU)
This phase focuses on ensuring there is a sound basis for the project before significant resources are committed.
i.  **Appoint Executive & Project Manager:** Define the project's ownership and day-to-day management roles. The Executive is responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 7].
ii. **Capture Previous Lessons:** Review lessons from similar projects to inform planning and avoid common pitfalls. This contributes to building quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 10].
iii. **Design and Appoint Project Management Team:** Establish a clear organizational structure and assign responsibilities, addressing ambiguity regarding who is responsible for what [Ref 5]. Consider the overall team composition early [Ref 2].
iv. **Prepare Project Brief:** Develop an initial outline of the project's objectives, scope (product and project scope), desired outcomes, and potential benefits. This forms the initial justification for the project [Ref 2, Ref 15].
v.  **Plan the Initiation Stage:** Detail the activities required to properly set up the project, leading to the creation of the Project Initiation Documentation (PID).

#### II. Initiating a Project (IP)
This phase establishes a solid foundation for the project, ensuring all stakeholders have a common understanding and commitment.
i.  **Create Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Consolidate and refine the Project Brief, detailing the project's scope, objectives, benefits, project management team structure, control mechanisms, and management approaches.
ii. **Detailed Planning:**
    *   **Define Product Scope and Project Scope:** Clearly delineate what the project will deliver and the work required to deliver it [Ref 2]. Utilize a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the scope into progressively lower levels of work elements [Ref 4].
    *   **Estimating and Scheduling:** Develop realistic timeframes and resource estimates for all activities [Ref 2].
    *   **Budgeting:** Create a comprehensive project budget [Ref 2].
    *   **Team Composition:** Finalize roles and responsibilities, fostering a collaborative project team environment [Ref 2, Ref 10].
    *   **Communication Plan:** Develop a strategy for effective engagement with all stakeholders [Ref 2, Ref 10].
    *   **Procurement Strategy:** Outline how required goods and services (e.g., construction contractors, materials) will be acquired [Ref 2].
    *   **Change Management Approach:** Define the process for managing changes to the project baseline [Ref 2].
    *   **Measurement Approach:** Establish metrics for project performance and success criteria [Ref 2, Ref 1].
iii. **Perform Risk Analysis:** Identify and assess potential threats (risk occurrence that would have a negative impact) and opportunities, and plan for optimized risk responses [Ref 4, Ref 10].
iv. **Establish Quality Management Approach:** Define quality standards and activities to build quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 10].
v.  **Set up Project Controls:** Define how progress will be monitored and deviations from plan will be managed.

#### III. Directing a Project (DP)
This process enables the Project Board to make key decisions and provide overall direction and control to the project.
i.  **Authorize Initiation:** The Project Board reviews the Project Brief and Stage Plan for the Initiation Stage and authorizes the Project Manager to proceed with initiation activities.
ii. **Authorize a Stage:** At the end of each management stage, the Project Board reviews the End Stage Report and the next Stage Plan to decide whether to authorize continuation. This is a critical decision gate [Ref 6, Ref 15].
iii. **Give Ad Hoc Direction:** Respond to issues and exceptions as they arise, providing guidance to the Project Manager.
iv. **Authorize Project Closure:** Upon project completion, the Project Board approves the project closure based on the End Project Report.

#### IV. Controlling a Stage (CS)
This phase manages the day-to-day work within an approved management stage, ensuring it stays on track.
i.  **Authorize a Work Package:** The Project Manager assigns a "work package" (a group of activities with a defined output) to a Team Manager or individual team member [Ref 4].
ii. **Review Work Package Status:** Regularly monitor progress against agreed tolerances and quality criteria.
iii. **Manage Issues and Risks:** Identify and address new issues (event that arises during a project [Ref 9]) and risks, updating the risk register and planning appropriate responses [Ref 10].
iv. **Report Progress:** Provide regular status updates to the Project Board and relevant stakeholders, detailing progress against plans and highlighting any deviations (measurement [Ref 1]).
v.  **Take Corrective Action:** Implement necessary adjustments to bring the stage back on track or escalate exceptions to the Project Board.
vi. **Manage Change:** Process and assess change requests using the defined change management approach [Ref 2].

#### V. Managing Product Delivery (MP)
This process ensures that products (deliverables) are created, quality-checked, and approved as specified in the work packages.
i.  **Accept Work Package:** Team Manager formally accepts the assigned work package from the Project Manager.
ii. **Execute Work Package:** Team members perform the activities to create the products/deliverables [Ref 9].
iii. **Implement Quality Checks:** Apply defined quality methods and checks to ensure products meet specified standards.
iv. **Report Checkpoint:** Team Manager reports progress and status of work packages to the Project Manager.
v.  **Deliver Work Package:** Team Manager formally delivers the completed products to the Project Manager for acceptance.

#### VI. Closing a Project (CP)
This phase provides a controlled close to the project, ensuring all deliverables are handed over and lessons learned are captured.
i.  **Hand Over Products:** Ensure all final deliverables (e.g., the completed construction) are formally accepted by the operational users.
ii. **Evaluate Project Performance:** Review the project's performance against its objectives, benefits, and baseline metrics (measurement [Ref 1]).
iii. **Capture Lessons Learned:** Document successes, challenges, and recommendations for future projects. This contributes to organizational learning and continuous improvement.
iv. **Decommission Project Resources:** Release project team members and other resources.
v.  **Prepare End Project Report:** Summarize the project's overall performance, including how well objectives were met, and provide recommendations for post-project benefit reviews.
vi. **Recommend Project Closure:** The Project Manager formally recommends project closure to the Project Board.

### Roles

Based on PRINCE2, the following key roles are essential for this project:

*   **Executive (Sponsor):** Accountable for the project's success, providing resources and making executive decisions [Ref 7, Ref 13]. Represents the business interests.
*   **Senior User:** Represents the needs of those who will use the project's products or who will be affected by them. Ensures the solution meets user requirements.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those designing, developing, and implementing the project's products (e.g., the construction firm, internal technical teams). Ensures technical viability and quality.
*   **Project Manager:** Manages the project on a day-to-day basis within the tolerances set by the Project Board. Coordinates activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 7, Ref 14].
*   **Project Assurance:** Independent oversight of the project's performance and compliance with standards. Can be assigned to an individual or group.
*   **Change Authority:** Manages changes to the project's scope, budget, and schedule. For a small project, this role may be part of the Project Board or Project Manager.
*   **Team Manager:** Responsible for delivering products assigned in a Work Package, often a lead contractor or functional manager for construction.

### Decision Gates

Key decision gates are integral to the PRINCE2 "manage by stages" principle, providing controlled progression and ensuring continued justification. These gates are typically reviews by the Project Board.

i.  **Go/No-Go for Project Initiation:**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm the project is justified and viable enough to invest in detailed planning.
    *   **Trigger:** Completion of the "Starting Up a Project" process, with the Project Brief and Stage Plan for Initiation.
    *   **Decision:** Project Board decides whether to authorize the Initiation Stage. This aligns with justifying the project [Ref 15].

ii. **Stage Gate Review (End Stage Assessment):**
    *   **Purpose:** To review the performance of the current stage, approve the completion of its deliverables, and authorize the next stage.
    *   **Trigger:** Completion of all activities within a management stage.
    *   **Decision:** Project Board reviews the End Stage Report, Product Status Account, and the next Stage Plan to decide whether to authorize the next stage or terminate the project [Ref 6, Ref 15]. For a short project, there might be only one or two such gates after initiation.

iii. **Project Closure Authorization:**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm that the project has met its objectives, delivered its products, and is ready to be formally closed.
    *   **Trigger:** Completion of all planned project work and the "Closing a Project" activities.
    *   **Decision:** Project Board reviews the End Project Report and Lessons Learned Report to authorize project closure [Ref 6].
