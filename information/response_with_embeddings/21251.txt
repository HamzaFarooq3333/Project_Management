### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be structured into five distinct phases, ensuring systematic progression and control.

#### I. Project Initiation
i.  **Identify and Engage Stakeholders:** Identify all persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by, any aspect of the project [Ref 3, Ref 6]. This includes both internal and external parties, such as educators, students, administrators, and technical teams. Effectively engage with stakeholders to understand their needs and expectations [Ref 8].
ii. **Define High-Level Objectives and Scope:** Establish the project's purpose and one or more defined objectives [Ref 6, Ref 15]. Outline the initial product and project scope to provide a clear understanding of what the project aims to deliver.
iii. **Appoint Project Manager and Core Team:** Officially appoint the Project Manager who will be responsible for leading the coordinated activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 6, Ref 13].
iv. **Obtain Project Authorization:** Secure formal authorization for the project, typically in the form of a Project Charter, which will include the project's initial scope, objectives, and the Project Sponsor [Ref 6] who is responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 6]. This also establishes the initial project governance structure [Ref 12].

#### II. Planning
i.  **Develop Project Management Plan:** Create a comprehensive document that defines how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed. This plan will be tailored to the project context [Ref 8, Ref 9].
ii. **Define Product and Project Scope:** Elaborate on the product scope (features and functions of the software) and project scope (work to be performed). This includes creating a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the defined scope into progressively lower levels of work elements [Ref 3, Ref 4].
iii. **Estimate and Schedule:** Develop detailed estimates for activities and create a project schedule, defining the timeline for project deliverables [Ref 4].
iv. **Budget Development:** Establish the project budget, allocating resources and funds for all planned activities [Ref 4].
v.  **Team and Resource Planning:** Plan for team composition, roles, responsibilities, and resource acquisition [Ref 4]. This includes defining the skills required for software development, UI/UX design, quality assurance, etc.
vi. **Communication Plan:** Define how, when, and by whom project information will be communicated to stakeholders [Ref 4].
vii. **Procurement Management:** Identify any external services or products needed and plan for their acquisition (e.g., third-party tools, specialized consultancy) [Ref 4].
viii. **Risk Management:** Identify potential threats (risk occurrences with negative impact) and opportunities, analyze their impact and probability, and plan appropriate risk responses [Ref 3, Ref 4, Ref 11]. Optimize risk responses [Ref 8].
ix. **Quality Management:** Plan how quality will be built into processes and deliverables [Ref 8], including software development standards, testing strategies, and acceptance criteria.
x.  **Change Management Plan:** Establish the process for managing changes to the project scope, schedule, budget, and other aspects [Ref 4].
xi. **Development Approach and Life Cycle Definition:** Clearly define the software development approach (e.g., agile, waterfall, hybrid) and the project life cycle, including specific phases or iterations for design, development, testing, and deployment [Ref 2].

#### III. Execution (Project Work)
i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Carry out the work defined in the Project Management Plan to achieve the project's objectives [Ref 2, Ref 6]. This involves managing the day-to-day software development activities.
ii. **Acquire, Develop, and Manage Project Team:** Obtain the necessary team members and foster a collaborative project team environment [Ref 8]. Provide guidance and support to the team, including software developers, QA testers, business analysts, etc.
iii. **Perform Quality Assurance:** Implement the planned quality activities to ensure that project processes and deliverables adhere to the defined quality standards [Ref 8]. This includes code reviews, unit testing, and integration testing.
iv. **Manage Communications:** Distribute project information according to the communication plan, ensuring effective engagement with stakeholders [Ref 8].
v.  **Conduct Procurements:** Administer any contracts with external vendors or suppliers as planned.
vi. **Software Development Activities:** This core activity includes detailed design, coding, database development, integration, and initial testing cycles.

#### IV. Monitoring & Controlling
i.  **Monitor and Control Project Work:** Track, review, and report project progress and performance against the Project Management Plan [Ref 2].
ii. **Control Scope, Schedule, and Cost:** Manage changes to the project baselines, ensuring they are approved through the change management process [Ref 4].
iii. **Perform Integrated Change Control:** Review all change requests, approve changes, and manage changes to deliverables, organizational process assets, and the Project Management Plan [Ref 4].
iv. **Manage Quality:** Monitor specific project results to determine if they comply with relevant quality standards and identify ways to eliminate causes of unsatisfactory performance.
v.  **Manage Risks:** Implement risk responses as planned and monitor residual risks and new risks [Ref 3, Ref 11]. Optimize risk responses [Ref 8].
vi. **Monitor Stakeholder Engagement:** Continuously engage with stakeholders to ensure their continued involvement and support, and address any concerns or issues [Ref 2, Ref 8].
vii. **Report Performance:** Generate performance reports to provide information on project status, progress, and forecasts.

#### V. Project Closure
i.  **Close Project or Phase:** Finalize all activities across all process groups to formally complete the project or a project phase.
ii. **Obtain Final Product Acceptance:** Secure formal acceptance of the developed software from the customer or sponsor. This is a critical delivery point [Ref 2].
iii. **Transfer Deliverables:** Hand over the final product, services, or results to the operational team or end-users.
iv. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document project successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
v.  **Release Resources:** Release project team members and other resources.
vi. **Close Procurements:** Finalize all contractual agreements with vendors and suppliers.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial checkpoints where the project's viability, progress, and adherence to objectives are reviewed, and authorization to proceed to the next phase is granted. These gates ensure appropriate governance and control [Ref 12].

i.  **Initiation Review (Go/No-Go for Planning):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally authorize the project to proceed into the detailed planning phase.
    *   **Inputs:** Approved Project Charter, high-level scope and objectives, initial stakeholder register.
    *   **Decision:** Project Sponsor (Accountable) reviews and approves the Project Charter, confirming the project's alignment with organizational strategy and granting authority to proceed with planning [Ref 6, Ref 12].
    *   **Outcome:** Project Charter approval or project termination.

ii. **Planning Review (Go/No-Go for Execution):**
    *   **Purpose:** To review and approve the comprehensive Project Management Plan, ensuring all aspects are adequately planned before significant resources are committed to execution.
    *   **Inputs:** Project Management Plan (including detailed scope, WBS, schedule, budget, risk management plan, quality plan, resource plan, communication plan) [Ref 4].
    *   **Decision:** Project Sponsor and key stakeholders (Accountable/Consulted) review and approve the Project Management Plan, confirming feasibility and readiness for execution [Ref 2, Ref 12].
    *   **Outcome:** Approved Project Management Plan and baseline for performance measurement.

iii. **Development Completion / Major Milestone Reviews:**
    *   **Purpose:** To assess the progress and quality of deliverables at significant points during the execution phase (e.g., design completion, alpha/beta release, UAT readiness).
    *   **Inputs:** Design documents, developed software modules, test reports, quality metrics.
    *   **Decision:** Project Manager, Technical Lead, and relevant stakeholders (Responsible/Consulted) review the deliverables against defined quality standards and acceptance criteria. Sponsor is Informed [Ref 8].
    *   **Outcome:** Approval to proceed to the next development iteration or major testing phase.

iv. **User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Sign-off:**
    *   **Purpose:** To ensure the developed software meets the functional and non-functional requirements and is acceptable to the end-users and client.
    *   **Inputs:** Fully developed and tested software, UAT plan and results, issue log.
    *   **Decision:** Key stakeholders, particularly the client or end-user representatives (Accountable), provide formal sign-off on the software's acceptance. Project Manager (Responsible) facilitates this process [Ref 2].
    *   **Outcome:** Formal acceptance of the software for deployment/delivery.

v.  **Project Closure Review:**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm that all project work is completed, objectives are met, and final deliverables are accepted.
    *   **Inputs:** Final project report, lessons learned document, formal acceptance of the product/service, closure of contracts.
    *   **Decision:** Project Sponsor (Accountable) formally reviews and approves the project closure, confirming all objectives have been achieved and value delivered [Ref 6, Ref 15].
    *   **Outcome:** Formal project closure and release of resources.
