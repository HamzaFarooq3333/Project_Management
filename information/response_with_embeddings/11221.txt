#### II. Phases and Activities

The project will be divided into the following phases:

**I. Initiation**

i.  Define project objectives, scope, and deliverables, aligning them with stakeholder needs [Ref 7].
ii.  Identify stakeholders and their requirements [Ref 1, Ref 4].
iii.  Develop a Project Charter outlining project goals, scope, and initial resource allocation [Ref 7].
iv.  Conduct a preliminary risk assessment and identify potential threats [Ref 4].
v.  Establish project governance principles and procedures [Ref 10, Ref 11].

**II. Planning**

i.  Develop a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
ii.  Define activities, sequence them, and estimate resources required for each task [Ref 2].
iii.  Develop a project schedule and budget [Ref 2].
iv.  Create a communication plan to manage stakeholder expectations and facilitate information flow [Ref 2].
v.  Develop a procurement plan if external vendors or resources are required [Ref 2].
vi.  Create a risk management plan to address identified risks [Ref 4].
vii.  Develop a change management process [Ref 2].
viii. Define Quality Management Plan that incorporates building quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 8].

**III. Execution**

i.  Execute the project plan, managing the work according to the defined scope and schedule [Ref 1].
ii.  Manage the project team, ensuring effective communication, collaboration, and performance [Ref 8].
iii.  Conduct the software development activities, including manual configuration and testing.
iv.  Implement the communication plan and manage stakeholder relationships [Ref 1, Ref 8].
v.  Procure any necessary resources as per the procurement plan [Ref 2].
vi.  Manage identified risks and implement mitigation strategies [Ref 10].
vii. Monitor and control project performance and track progress [Ref 1].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling**

i.  Monitor project progress against the baseline schedule and budget [Ref 1, Ref 2].
ii.  Control project scope, schedule, and budget, implementing the change management process [Ref 2].
iii.  Manage quality and ensure deliverables meet the defined quality standards [Ref 8].
iv.  Track and manage risks, implementing risk response plans [Ref 4].
v.  Control the project by performing a regular review with key stakeholders.
vi. Regularly assess, evaluate, and respond to system interactions [Ref 8].

**V. Closing**

i.  Verify that all project deliverables are complete and meet the acceptance criteria [Ref 7].
ii.  Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the stakeholders [Ref 7].
iii.  Conduct a post-project review and document lessons learned [Ref 6].
iv.  Close out contracts and procurements [Ref 2].
v.  Archive project documents and records.
