### Process Recommendation for Medium-Sized IT Infrastructure Project (ISO Standards)

**1. Process Summary**

This process recommendation outlines a project management approach for a medium-sized IT infrastructure project, spanning 6-18 months, leveraging ISO standards and best practices. It integrates the principles of planning, execution, monitoring & controlling, and closure, while emphasizing stakeholder engagement, risk management, and adaptability [Ref 3, Ref 12].  The methodology is designed to be flexible, allowing for tailoring to the specific context of the project [Ref 3]. The project management activities will focus on defining and controlling the project scope, schedule, and budget, while ensuring effective communication and collaboration among the project team and stakeholders [Ref 1].

-----

**2. Phases and Activities**

I. **Initiation Phase:**

   i.  **Project Definition:** Define the project objectives, scope, and deliverables. [Ref 1]
   ii. **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify all relevant stakeholders and their interests. [Ref 8, Ref 12]
   iii. **Project Charter Development:** Create a project charter outlining the project's purpose, objectives, and high-level requirements.
   iv. **Feasibility Study:** Conduct a feasibility study to assess the project's viability.

II. **Planning Phase:**

   i.  **Scope Management Planning:** Define the project scope, create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 5], and establish scope control procedures.
   ii. **Schedule Management Planning:** Develop a project schedule, estimate activity durations, and identify the critical path. [Ref 1]
   iii. **Cost Management Planning:** Develop a project budget, estimate costs, and establish cost control procedures. [Ref 1]
   iv. **Resource Management Planning:** Plan for the acquisition and management of project resources, including human resources and equipment.
   v.  **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks, assess their impact and probability, and develop risk response strategies. [Ref 12]
   vi. **Communication Management Planning:** Develop a communication plan to ensure effective communication among stakeholders. [Ref 1]
   vii. **Procurement Management Planning:** Plan for the procurement of goods and services required for the project. [Ref 1]
   viii. **Quality Management Planning:** Develop a quality management plan to ensure that project deliverables meet the defined quality standards.

III. **Execution Phase:**

   i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the project activities according to the project plan. [Ref 2]
   ii. **Manage Project Team:** Lead and manage the project team, providing guidance and support.
   iii. **Manage Communications:** Implement the communication plan to ensure effective communication among stakeholders.
   iv. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Engage with stakeholders to ensure their ongoing support and involvement. [Ref 12]
   v.  **Conduct Procurements:** Acquire the goods and services required for the project according to the procurement plan.

IV. **Monitoring & Controlling Phase:**

   i.  **Monitor and Control Project Work:** Track project progress, identify variances, and take corrective actions. [Ref 2]
   ii. **Perform Integrated Change Control:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget. [Ref 1]
   iii. **Control Scope:** Ensure that the project scope is maintained and that only approved changes are incorporated.
   iv. **Control Schedule:** Ensure that the project schedule is followed and that any delays are addressed promptly.
   v.  **Control Costs:** Ensure that the project budget is adhered to and that any cost overruns are addressed promptly.
   vi. **Control Quality:** Monitor the quality of project deliverables and take corrective actions as needed. [Ref 12]
   vii. **Control Risks:** Monitor identified risks and implement risk response strategies as needed.

V. **Closure Phase:**

   i.  **Close Project or Phase:** Formalize project completion, obtain final acceptance of deliverables, and document lessons learned. [Ref 6]
   ii. **Archive Project Documents:** Archive all project documents for future reference.

-----

**3. Roles**

i.  **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support for the project. [Ref 8]
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project.
iii. **Project Team Members:** Responsible for completing assigned project tasks.
iv. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project. [Ref 8]

**5. Decision Gates**

i.  **End of Initiation Phase:** Review and approval of the project charter and feasibility study. This decision gate confirms the project's viability and authorizes the start of the planning phase.
ii. **End of Planning Phase:** Review and approval of the project management plan. This decision gate ensures that the project is well-defined and ready for execution.
iii. **End of Execution Phase:** Review of deliverables and project performance. This gate ensures the project meets requirements before advancing.
iv. **Project Closure:** Acceptance of final deliverables and project closure documentation.
