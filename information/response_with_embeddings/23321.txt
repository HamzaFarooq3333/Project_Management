#### Phases and Activities

This project will be structured in the following phases. Each phase will have its own deliverables and decision points, as well as stakeholder input. [Ref 7, Ref 8]

**I. Project Initiation:**

   i.  Define Project Objectives and Scope: Clearly articulate the research goals, deliverables, and success criteria. Establish the project scope, considering product and project scope [Ref 2].
   ii. Identify Stakeholders: Identify all stakeholders and their interests [Ref 9]. Define a communication plan to ensure that they are aware of the project's progress [Ref 2].
   iii. Develop Project Charter: Formally authorize the project and assign the project manager. [Ref 10]
   iv. High-Level Risk Assessment: Conduct an initial risk assessment to identify potential threats and opportunities. [Ref 5]
   v. Define Project Governance: Outline the project governance structure, including roles, responsibilities, and decision-making processes. [Ref 7, Ref 8]

**II. Planning:**

   i.  Detailed Scope Definition: Break down the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 5]. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS). [Ref 5]
   ii. Schedule Development: Create a detailed project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and resource allocation. [Ref 2]
   iii. Budget Development: Develop a detailed project budget, including cost estimates for all activities and resources. [Ref 2]
   iv. Resource Planning: Determine the resources required (human, equipment, materials) and how they will be acquired. [Ref 4]
   v. Risk Management Planning: Develop a detailed risk management plan, including risk identification, assessment, response planning, and monitoring. [Ref 11]
   vi. Communication Planning: Develop a comprehensive communication plan to ensure timely and effective information flow among stakeholders. [Ref 2]
   vii. Procurement Planning: If applicable, develop a procurement plan for acquiring necessary goods or services. [Ref 2]
   viii. Change Management Planning: Establish a change management process to control and manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget. [Ref 2]
   ix. Quality Management Planning: Establish quality standards and define processes to ensure deliverables meet those standards [Ref 8].

**III. Project Execution:**

   i.  Manage Project Work: Execute the planned activities according to the project plan.
   ii. Team Management: Manage and lead the project team, providing guidance and support. [Ref 1]
   iii. Information distribution: Communicate project updates and information effectively to all stakeholders [Ref 2].
   iv. Procurement Management: Manage contracts and vendors according to the procurement plan.
   v.  Quality Assurance: Conduct quality checks and reviews to ensure deliverables meet the defined quality standards.

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling:**

   i.  Performance Reporting: Regularly track and report project performance against the baseline plans (scope, schedule, cost, quality). [Ref 1]
   ii. Change Control: Manage changes to the project through the established change management process. [Ref 2]
   iii. Risk Monitoring and Control: Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies. [Ref 11]
   iv. Issue Management: Address and resolve project issues promptly. [Ref 7]
   v. Stakeholder Management: Manage stakeholder expectations and address any concerns. [Ref 1]
   vi. Corrective Actions: Initiate corrective actions as needed to bring the project back on track.

**V. Project Closure:**

   i.  Project Deliverable Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables from the stakeholders.
   ii. Final Reporting: Prepare a final project report summarizing project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations.
   iii. Contract Closure: Close out any outstanding contracts with vendors or suppliers.
   iv. Knowledge Transfer: Document and archive project information for future reference.
   v. Team Dissolution: Release the project team members and reassign resources.
