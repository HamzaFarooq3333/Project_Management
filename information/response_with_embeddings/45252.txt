### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be divided into distinct management stages, following the PRINCE2 process model, integrated with relevant PMBOK and ISO concepts.

#### I. Pre-Project Activities (PRINCE2: Starting Up a Project)
*   **Objective:** Establish a feasible and worthwhile project by ensuring there is a sound basis before committing to significant expenditure.
*   **Activities:**
    *   i. **Appoint Project Board and Project Manager:** Establish the project's governance by appointing the Executive (ultimate accountability), Senior User (representing user interests), Senior Supplier (representing supplier interests), and the Project Manager (day-to-day management) [Ref 6, Ref 8].
    *   ii. **Capture Project Brief:** Develop an initial outline of the project's purpose, scope, objectives, desired outcomes, and benefits [Ref 5].
    *   iii. **Outline Business Case:** Create a high-level justification for the project, including initial cost estimates and expected benefits [Ref 8].
    *   iv. **Identify Key Stakeholders:** Systematically identify all persons, groups, or organizations that have an interest in, can affect, or are affected by the project [Ref 1, Ref 4, Ref 6, Ref 7].
    *   v. **Plan the Initiation Stage:** Develop a plan detailing the activities required to establish the project's foundation.

#### II. Initiation Phase (PRINCE2: Initiating a Project)
*   **Objective:** Establish a solid foundation for the project, ensuring a shared understanding of what needs to be delivered, why, when, and how.
*   **Activities:**
    *   i. **Refine and Complete Business Case:** Develop a comprehensive business justification, including detailed costs, benefits, and risk analysis, ensuring value for the organization [Ref 8, Ref 15].
    *   ii. **Define Project and Product Scope:** Clearly define what the project will deliver (product scope) and the work required to deliver it (project scope). This includes creating a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the scope into progressively lower levels of work packages [Ref 3, Ref 4].
    *   iii. **Develop Project Plan:** Create a detailed plan covering estimating, scheduling, budget allocation, team composition, and resource management [Ref 3].
    *   iv. **Establish Project Controls:** Define strategies and plans for managing risks (including threats) [Ref 3, Ref 4, Ref 10], quality, configuration, communication, and change management [Ref 3].
    *   v. **Develop Communication and Procurement Management Plans:** Outline how information will be shared with stakeholders and how external resources will be acquired [Ref 3].
    *   vi. **Create Project Initiation Document (PID):** Compile all foundational project management documentation into a single, comprehensive document for Project Board approval [Ref 8].

#### III. Execution and Control Phases (PRINCE2: Directing a Project, Controlling a Stage, Managing Product Delivery, Managing a Stage Boundary)
*   **Objective:** Deliver the project products according to the agreed plans, manage deviations, and ensure continuous business justification. This occurs in controlled stages [Ref 8].
*   **Activities:**
    *   i. **Directing the Project:** The Project Board provides overall direction and control, authorizing the project, individual stages, and ultimately project closure. This aligns with project governance principles [Ref 8, Ref 9].
    *   ii. **Controlling a Stage:** The Project Manager manages each stage on a day-to-day basis, assigning work packages, monitoring progress, managing issues, and reporting stage status to the Project Board [Ref 8, Ref 12].
    *   iii. **Managing Product Delivery:** Team Managers and teams execute the detailed work packages, producing the required deliverables as defined in the project and product scope [Ref 8, Ref 12].
    *   iv. **Managing Stage Boundaries:** At the end of each stage, the Project Manager reviews performance against the stage plan, updates the overall Project Plan and Business Case, and prepares a plan for the next stage to seek Project Board authorization [Ref 8].
    *   v. **Continuous Stakeholder Engagement:** Regularly engage with all identified stakeholders, managing their expectations and ensuring their continued involvement and support [Ref 1, Ref 7].
    *   vi. **Continuous Risk and Issue Management:** Proactively identify, assess, and respond to risks (including threats) and manage any issues that arise, optimizing responses to protect project objectives [Ref 7, Ref 10].
    *   vii. **Continuous Measurement and Quality Assurance:** Monitor project performance against baselines for scope, schedule, and budget [Ref 1, Ref 3]. Build quality into all processes and deliverables to ensure they meet specified requirements [Ref 7].
    *   viii. **Change Management:** Implement a defined process to manage and control any changes to the project's baselines [Ref 3].

#### IV. Closure Phase (PRINCE2: Closing a Project)
*   **Objective:** Formally close the project, confirm that all objectives have been met (or formally terminate if necessary), and ensure operational handover and lessons learned are captured.
*   **Activities:**
    *   i. **Decommission Project Products:** Ensure all project-related products are properly handed over, transferred to operations, or decommissioned [Ref 8].
    *   ii. **Evaluate Project Performance:** Conduct a post-project review to assess the project's success against its objectives and capture lessons learned for future projects [Ref 8].
    *   iii. **Assess Benefits Realization:** Review the achievement of expected benefits (or establish plans for future benefits realization monitoring) [Ref 13].
    *   iv. **Archive Project Documentation:** Ensure all relevant project documentation is properly archived for future reference and compliance.
    *   v. **Formal Project Closure Report:** Prepare a final report summarizing the project's performance, outcomes, and lessons learned for Project Board approval [Ref 8].
    *   vi. **Release Resources:** Formally release project team members and other resources.

### Roles

The following key roles are essential for the effective management and governance of this education infrastructure project, as defined by PRINCE2:

*   **Corporate/Programme Management (or the Customer):** Provides the overall mandate and context for the project, appointing the Executive and ensuring alignment with organizational strategy.
*   **Executive:** Has overall accountability for the project's success and ensuring it delivers value for money. Responsible for the Business Case [Ref 6].
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's products (e.g., educational staff, students, facilities management). Ensures the project delivers fit-for-purpose outputs.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those who provide the specialist resources and expertise to deliver the project's products (e.g., construction firms, IT vendors).
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project, including planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing it [Ref 3, Ref 6, Ref 8].
*   **Team Manager:** Responsible for managing a team to deliver specific work packages according to agreed specifications.
*   **Project Support:** Provides administrative services, advice, and guidance to the Project Manager and Project Board.
*   **Project Assurance:** Provides independent monitoring and review of project progress and adherence to standards and strategies.
*   **Change Authority (Optional):** A delegated body (often comprising Project Board members) that approves significant changes to the project's baselines.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points, often referred to as "Management by Exception" in PRINCE2, where the Project Board reviews progress and viability before authorizing the next steps [Ref 8]. This ensures the project remains aligned with business objectives and provides necessary governance [Ref 9].

*   **Project Mandate / Start-up Authorization:** Before the Initiation Phase, the Project Board (or senior management) formally authorizes the project to proceed with the initiation activities based on the initial Project Brief and Outline Business Case. This confirms the strategic alignment and initial viability.
*   **Project Initiation Document (PID) Approval:** At the end of the Initiation Phase, the Project Board formally approves the comprehensive Project Initiation Document. This is a critical gate that authorizes the project to proceed into its first delivery stage, committing significant resources based on detailed plans and a firm Business Case [Ref 8].
*   **Stage Boundary Authorizations:** At the end of each management stage (Execution & Control Phases), the Project Board reviews the performance of the completed stage, approves the updated Business Case, and authorizes the plan for the next stage. This ensures continuous business justification and control throughout the project's lifecycle [Ref 8].
*   **Project Closure Authorization:** At the end of the Closure Phase, the Project Board formally reviews the End Project Report, confirms that project objectives have been met (or that a justifiable early closure is required), accepts the final products, and authorizes the formal closure of the project, allowing for the release of resources [Ref 8].
