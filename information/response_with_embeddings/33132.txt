### Process Summary

This process recommendation outlines a PRINCE2-aligned approach for a small healthcare research project expected to be completed in under 6 months. Given the project size and duration, a streamlined PRINCE2 implementation is recommended, focusing on the core principles and tailoring the processes to minimize overhead while maintaining robust control [Ref 7]. The process will emphasize clear roles and responsibilities, proactive risk management, and consistent stakeholder engagement to ensure project success. The framework incorporates planning, execution, control, and closure activities, ensuring the project delivers the expected research outcomes within defined constraints [Ref 4]. The project team should focus on delivering value and adapt to any changes required throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 6].

-----

### Phases and Activities

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU)**
  i. Appoint the Project Management Team: Identify and appoint the Project Board (Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier), Project Manager, and any required team members. This ensures clarity of roles and responsibilities from the outset [Ref 4].
  ii. Develop the Project Brief: Create a concise document outlining the project's purpose, objectives, scope, benefits, and high-level risks.
  iii. Plan the Initiation Stage: Define the activities and resources required for the initiation stage, including the development of the Project Initiation Documentation (PID).

II. **Initiating a Project (IP)**
  i. Refine the Business Case: Conduct a thorough analysis of the project's justification, including benefits, costs, risks, and potential return on investment [Ref 1].
  ii. Develop the Project Management Plan (PID): Create a comprehensive plan that details how the project will be managed, including scope, schedule, budget, quality, risk, and communication plans [Ref 1].
  iii. Define Project Controls: Establish mechanisms for monitoring progress, managing issues, and controlling changes throughout the project lifecycle.

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS)**
  i. Authorize Work Packages: Delegate specific tasks or activities to team members through Work Packages, clearly defining deliverables, timelines, and responsibilities [Ref 5].
  ii. Monitor Progress: Track progress against the plan, identifying any deviations or issues. Regularly review performance against key performance indicators (KPIs).
  iii. Manage Issues and Risks: Identify, assess, and manage any issues or risks that arise during the stage. Implement appropriate responses to mitigate threats and capitalize on opportunities [Ref 15].
  iv. Report Progress: Provide regular reports to the Project Board on project status, issues, and risks.

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP)**
  i. Execute Work Packages: Team members perform the tasks outlined in the Work Packages.
  ii. Quality Assurance: Ensure that deliverables meet the required quality standards through regular reviews and testing.
  iii. Product Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of deliverables from the relevant stakeholders [Ref 8].

V. **Managing Stage Boundaries (SB)**
  i. Review Stage Performance: Assess the overall performance of the current stage against the plan.
  ii. Update the Project Plan: Revise the Project Plan based on the performance of the previous stage and any changes in the project environment.
  iii. Plan the Next Stage: Develop a detailed plan for the next stage, including activities, resources, and timelines.

VI. **Closing a Project (CP)**
  i. Confirm Project Closure: Obtain formal confirmation from the Project Board that the project has met its objectives and can be closed.
  ii. Review Lessons Learned: Conduct a review to identify lessons learned during the project, documenting successes and areas for improvement.
  iii. Archive Project Documentation: Ensure that all project documentation is properly archived for future reference.

### Roles

*   **Executive:** Ultimately accountable for the project's success.
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's outputs.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those who will supply the resources and expertise required by the project.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, organizing, and controlling the project activities.
*   **Team Member:** Responsible for completing assigned tasks and delivering the required outputs.

### Decision Gates

i. **End of Initiation Stage:** Decision by the Project Board to proceed with the project based on the completed Project Initiation Documentation (PID) and refined Business Case. This ensures the project is viable before significant investment is made.
ii. **End of Each Stage:** Review of stage performance against the plan, updated risk assessment, and approval to proceed to the next stage. This allows for course correction and ensures that the project remains aligned with its objectives.
iii. **Project Closure:** Formal sign-off by the Project Board confirming that the project objectives have been met and the project can be closed. This ensures that all deliverables have been accepted and all outstanding issues have been resolved.
