### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be divided into four distinct phases, closely aligned with PRINCE2's process model but tailored for brevity.

#### I. Pre-Project / Starting Up a Project (SU)
This phase establishes the project's initial viability and lays the groundwork for detailed planning.
i.  **Appoint Executive & Project Manager:** The Project Board Executive and Project Manager are appointed, with clear definition of responsibilities to avoid ambiguity [Ref 4].
ii. **Capture Project Mandate:** Receive and understand the initial project idea or mandate, confirming its business context and high-level objectives [Ref 14].
iii. **Appoint Project Team:** Identify and appoint key members of the Project Team, considering their competencies and availability [Ref 1].
iv. **Prepare Project Brief:** Develop an initial outline of the project, including its objectives, scope, expected outputs (deliverables), and high-level timescales and costs [Ref 2, Ref 7].
v. **Plan Initiation Stage:** Define the activities required for the next phase, Initiation, including resources and timescales.

#### II. Initiation / Initiating a Project (IP)
This phase establishes a firm foundation for the project before significant investment in delivery.
i.  **Plan Project (Project Initiation Documentation - PID):** Develop detailed plans including product scope, project scope, estimating, scheduling, budget, team composition, communication plan, and procurement [Ref 2]. This involves creating a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the scope into manageable work packages [Ref 3].
ii. **Refine Business Case:** Develop a robust business justification for the project, confirming its alignment with organizational strategy and expected benefits [Ref 14].
iii. **Set Up Project Controls:** Establish how the project will be monitored, controlled, and reported, including tolerances for cost, time, scope, and quality [Ref 1, Ref 6].
iv. **Create Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Compile all key management products (Business Case, Project Plan, Controls, Strategies) into a single document for approval.

#### III. Delivery & Control / Controlling a Stage (CS) & Managing Product Delivery (MP)
This combined phase focuses on the execution of planned work and continuous monitoring, specifically tailored for a single, consolidated delivery stage in a short project.
i.  **Authorize Work Package:** The Project Manager authorizes specific work packages to the construction team, detailing products to be delivered, effort, and tolerances [Ref 3, Ref 6].
ii. **Execute Work Package:** The construction team performs the activities to produce the defined deliverables [Ref 1].
iii. **Monitor Progress:** Continuously track progress against the plan, measuring performance, and comparing actuals to forecasts [Ref 1]. This includes monitoring project schedule, budget, and quality [Ref 2].
iv. **Manage Issues & Risks:** Proactively identify, assess, and manage any issues or threats that arise, implementing appropriate risk responses to minimize negative impacts [Ref 1, Ref 3, Ref 7, Ref 14].
v. **Report Progress:** Regularly communicate project status, progress, and any significant deviations or issues to relevant stakeholders and the Project Board [Ref 1].

#### IV. Closing a Project (CP)
This phase ensures the orderly closure of the project and formalizes acceptance of its products.
i.  **Decommission Project:** Ensure all temporary project assets are properly accounted for and disposed of or transferred.
ii. **Hand Over Products:** Obtain formal acceptance of the final deliverables from the Senior User and hand them over to the operational environment [Ref 7].
iii. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a review to identify what went well, what could be improved, and capture lessons learned for future projects [Ref 6].
iv. **Evaluate Project Performance:** Assess the project's performance against its original objectives and business case [Ref 1].
v. **Gain Final Approval:** Secure formal closure approval from the Project Board and notify all stakeholders.

### Roles

Key roles are defined as per PRINCE2's management structure, tailored for a small construction project:

*   **Executive:** Accountable for the project's business justification and ultimately for its success. Represents the business perspective and ensures the project delivers value [Ref 10].
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's products or who will be affected by them. Accountable for specifying user needs and realizing benefits [Ref 8].
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those designing and constructing the project's products. Accountable for the quality and integrity of the delivered products.
*   **Project Board:** (Composed of the Executive, Senior User, and Senior Supplier) - Provides overall direction and decision-making for the project, operating on a "management by exception" principle.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project within the boundaries set by the Project Board. Manages all aspects of the project, including planning, executing, and controlling [Ref 8, Ref 11].
*   **Project Assurance:** Provides an independent oversight function for the Project Board, reviewing project performance and compliance.
*   **Project Support:** Provides administrative and project management support services to the Project Manager and Project Board.
*   **Construction Team:** Individuals and teams responsible for physically carrying out the work and producing the construction deliverables.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points where the Project Board (or Executive) reviews the project's viability and authorizes the next steps, aligning with PRINCE2's "manage by stages" and "manage by exception" principles [Ref 6].

1.  **Project Start Authorization:**
    *   **Description:** Approval to proceed from the "Starting Up a Project" phase to "Initiation." This confirms the initial project idea is worthwhile to investigate further.
    *   **Trigger:** Completion of the Project Brief and Plan for the Initiation Stage.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Board (Executive) [Ref 13].
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go for detailed planning.

2.  **Project Initiation Authorization:**
    *   **Description:** Approval to proceed from "Initiation" to the "Delivery & Control" phase. This is the most significant gate, as it commits resources to deliver the project's products.
    *   **Trigger:** Completion and approval of the Project Initiation Documentation (PID) and updated Business Case.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Board (Executive) [Ref 13].
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go for project execution, or approval to proceed with modifications.

3.  **Project Closure Authorization:**
    *   **Description:** Formal approval to close the project, confirming that all products have been delivered, accepted, and the project's objectives have been met (or that the project should be prematurely closed).
    *   **Trigger:** Completion of all planned products, handover, and the Final Report/Lessons Learned.
    *   **Decision Authority:** Project Board (Executive) [Ref 6, Ref 7].
    *   **Outcome:** Formal project closure, resource release, and sign-off on lessons learned.
