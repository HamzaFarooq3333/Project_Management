### Process Recommendation: Finance Research Project (PMBOK)

**1. Process Summary**

This document outlines a recommended project management process for a medium-sized (6-18 months) finance research project, guided by the PMBOK methodology and incorporating elements from ISO21502 and ISO21500. The process emphasizes a structured approach to planning, execution, monitoring, and control, ensuring that the project delivers valuable research within the defined scope, budget, and timeline. The process is designed to be adaptable and tailored to the specific needs of the research project [Ref 9], recognizing that "delivery approach can be any method or process suited to the type of outputs" [Ref 1]. The process also emphasizes stakeholder engagement to enable success [Ref 7, Ref 9].

-----

**2. Phases and Activities**

The project will be managed across the following phases, aligning with the PMBOK Guide's process groups and incorporating planning elements [Ref 3].

#### I. Initiation

i. **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's purpose, objectives, high-level requirements, and key stakeholders. Secure project sponsorship and authorization [Ref 10].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify all individuals, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect the project [Ref 7, Ref 12]. Document their needs and expectations.
iii. **Project Team Formation:** Assemble the core project team, defining roles and responsibilities.

#### II. Planning

i. **Scope Definition:** Define and document the detailed project scope, including deliverables, acceptance criteria, and exclusions [Ref 3]. Develop a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 5].
ii. **Schedule Development:** Create a detailed project schedule, identifying tasks, dependencies, durations, and critical path. Use appropriate scheduling techniques (e.g., critical path method) [Ref 3].
iii. **Cost Estimation and Budgeting:** Estimate the costs associated with each task and activity. Develop a comprehensive project budget [Ref 3].
iv. **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks and threats to the project [Ref 5, Ref 11], assess their likelihood and impact, and develop mitigation strategies.
v. **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan that outlines how project information will be disseminated to stakeholders.
vi. **Procurement Planning:** Determine if any external resources or services are required and plan for their procurement [Ref 3].
vii. **Resource Planning:** Determine the resources required for each project activity, to include Human and Non-Human resources.
viii. **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards and processes to ensure deliverables meet the stakeholders expectations [Ref 9].

#### III. Execution

i. **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the tasks and activities outlined in the project schedule. Manage resources, track progress, and address issues as they arise.
ii. **Manage Communications:** Implement the communication plan, ensuring that stakeholders are informed of project progress and any relevant issues.
iii. **Conduct Procurements:** Manage the procurement process, ensuring that external resources and services are acquired in a timely and cost-effective manner [Ref 3].
iv. **Perform Quality Assurance:** Implement the quality management plan and conduct quality audits to ensure deliverables meet the defined standards [Ref 9].

#### IV. Monitoring and Controlling

i. **Monitor and Control Project Work:** Track project progress against the plan, identify variances, and take corrective actions as needed. [Ref 2, Ref 3]
ii. **Manage Scope Changes:** Implement a change control process to manage changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].
iii. **Manage Risks:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as needed. Identify and respond to new risks that emerge.
iv. **Control Costs:** Track project expenditures against the budget and take corrective actions to control costs.
v. **Control Schedule:** Monitor the project schedule and take corrective actions to ensure that the project stays on track.
vi. **Report Performance:** Regularly report project performance to stakeholders, providing updates on progress, issues, and risks.

#### V. Closing

i. **Project Closure:** Finalize all project activities, obtain final acceptance of deliverables, and formally close the project.
ii. **Lessons Learned:** Document lessons learned throughout the project to improve future project performance.
iii. **Archiving Project Documents:** Archive all project documents and records for future reference.
iv. **Release Project Resources:** Deallocate project resources and return them to their respective departments or organizations.

**3. Roles**

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support for the project, secures resources, and approves key decisions [Ref 7].
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project. Leads the project team and manages stakeholder expectations.
iii. **Research Lead:** Responsible for the research execution, manages the research team, and ensures that the research meets the quality standards.
iv. **Project Team Members:** Individuals responsible for completing specific tasks and activities within the project.
v. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups that have an interest in the project or can be affected by its outcome [Ref 7, Ref 12].

**5. Decision Gates**

i. **Initiation Gate:** After the Initiation phase, review the Project Charter to ensure alignment with organizational goals and strategic objectives. Approve or reject the project based on its feasibility and potential value [Ref 10].
ii. **Planning Gate:** After the Planning phase, review the Project Management Plan to ensure that it is comprehensive, realistic, and aligned with stakeholder expectations. Approve or reject the plan based on its completeness and feasibility [Ref 3].
iii. **Execution Gate:** After the completion of key project milestones, review the progress of the project against the plan. Approve or reject the continuation of the project based on its performance and remaining feasibility.
iv. **Closure Gate:** At the end of the project, review the project deliverables and documentation to ensure that they meet the defined acceptance criteria. Approve or reject the project closure based on its completeness and adherence to quality standards.
