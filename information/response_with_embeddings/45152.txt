### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be structured into distinct phases, aligned with PRINCE2 principles but streamlined for a small-scale project.

#### I. Starting Up a Project (SU)
This phase focuses on ensuring there's a sound basis for the project and appointing the necessary management team before significant work begins.
i.  **Appoint Executive and Project Manager:** Establish clear leadership and overall accountability for the project [Ref 5].
ii. **Capture Previous Lessons:** Review lessons from similar education infrastructure projects to inform planning and avoid common pitfalls.
iii. **Design and Appoint Project Management Team:** Define and assign roles, including a Senior User and Senior Supplier, to represent stakeholder interests [Ref 7].
iv. **Prepare Project Brief:** Develop an initial outline of the project's purpose, scope, and objectives.
v.  **Plan the Initiation Stage:** Detail the activities required to properly plan the project.

#### II. Initiating and Directing the Project (IP/DP - Streamlined for small projects)
This consolidated phase ensures the project is well-planned, justifiable, and authorized before delivery commences. This covers many elements of PMBOK's 'Planning' domain [Ref 3].
i.  **Define Project Scope:** Clearly articulate both the product scope (what the infrastructure will deliver) and the project scope (the work required to deliver it), including breaking down work into manageable elements and work packages [Ref 3, Ref 4].
ii. **Conduct Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify all persons, groups, or organizations affected by or perceiving themselves to be affected by the project [Ref 1, Ref 4, Ref 7]. Develop a comprehensive Communication Plan to effectively engage with these stakeholders [Ref 3, Ref 6].
iii. **Develop Detailed Project Plan:**
    *   **Estimating:** Determine effort, cost, and duration for project activities [Ref 3].
    *   **Scheduling:** Create a timeline for project completion, considering dependencies [Ref 3].
    *   **Budget:** Allocate financial resources for the project [Ref 3].
    *   **Team Composition:** Define required skills and allocate team members [Ref 3].
    *   **Procurement Strategy:** Plan for acquiring necessary goods and services, relevant for infrastructure projects [Ref 3].
    *   **Change Management Approach:** Establish procedures for managing changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].
    *   **Measurement:** Define metrics for project performance and success [Ref 1, Ref 3].
iv. **Develop Benefits Management Approach:** Outline how the educational benefits of the infrastructure will be realized and measured.
v.  **Refine Risk Management Strategy:** Identify, assess, and plan responses for potential threats and opportunities that could impact objectives [Ref 4, Ref 9]. Optimize risk responses to enhance project outcomes [Ref 6].
vi. **Establish Quality Management Approach:** Define how quality will be assured and controlled throughout the project, building quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 6].
vii. **Create Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Compile all planning documents into a comprehensive project mandate for approval.

#### III. Managing Delivery Stage(s) (CS, MP, SB - Streamlined for small projects)
This phase covers the execution and control of the project's deliverables, likely consisting of a single or maximum of two delivery stages given the project's small size.
i.  **Authorize a Stage:** The Project Board reviews the Project Initiation Documentation (PID) and approves the commencement of the first (and possibly only) delivery stage.
ii. **Control a Stage:**
    *   **Assign Work Packages:** Distribute work packages to the delivery team [Ref 4].
    *   **Monitor Progress:** Track work against the plan, manage issues and risks as they arise [Ref 10], and escalate exceptions.
    *   **Report Highlights:** Provide regular progress updates to the Project Board.
iii. **Manage Product Delivery:** The Team Manager or delivery team executes the assigned work packages, ensuring the production of unique and verifiable deliverables [Ref 10].
iv. **Manage Stage Boundary (if applicable):** If there are multiple stages, the Project Manager reviews the performance of the current stage, updates the overall Project Plan, and prepares for the next stage. For this small project, this might only occur if a significant change dictates a formal review point.

#### IV. Closing a Project (CP)
This phase ensures the project is formally closed, products are handed over, and lessons are learned.
i.  **Hand Over Products:** Ensure all final infrastructure deliverables are transferred to operations and accepted by the user community.
ii. **Evaluate Project Performance:** Review the project against its initial objectives, assessing overall delivery, value realization, and how well uncertainty was managed [Ref 1].
iii. **Assure the Project:** Confirm that all products have been delivered, and the project objectives have been met.
iv. **Identify Follow-on Actions:** Document any remaining work, unresolved issues, or recommendations for future improvements.
v.  **Create End Project Report:** Summarize project performance, lessons learned, and recommendations.
vi. **Release Team Members:** Formally disband the project team.

### Roles

The following key roles are essential for the effective management of this education infrastructure project, aligned with PRINCE2's principles of defined roles and responsibilities:

*   **Executive (EX):** Accountable for the project's overall business justification and value [Ref 6]. Serves as the ultimate decision-maker for the Project Board.
*   **Senior User (SU):** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's products (e.g., school staff, students). Ensures the project delivers the required functionality.
*   **Senior Supplier (SS):** Represents the interests of those who provide the products or services for the project (e.g., construction firms, IT vendors). Ensures technical feasibility and resource availability.
*   **Project Board (PB):** Composed of the Executive, Senior User, and Senior Supplier. Responsible for overall direction and authorization of the project, delegating day-to-day management to the Project Manager [Ref 13].
*   **Project Manager (PM):** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project on behalf of the Project Board [Ref 14]. Manages planning, monitoring, and control aspects [Ref 5, Ref 14].
*   **Team Manager (TM):** (Optional, depending on project complexity) Manages the teams that produce the specialist products. Directly responsible for assigning and overseeing work packages [Ref 4].
*   **Project Support (PS):** Provides administrative and coordination support to the Project Manager and Project Board.

**Key:**
*   **R** - Responsible: The individual(s) who do the work.
*   **A** - Accountable: The individual ultimately answerable for the correct and thorough completion of the deliverable or task. There is only one Accountable per task/deliverable.
*   **C** - Consulted: Individual(s) whose opinions are sought, typically subject matter experts. Two-way communication.
*   **I** - Informed: Individual(s) who are kept up-to-date on progress, usually at the completion of a task or deliverable. One-way communication.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical control points within the project lifecycle, aligned with PRINCE2's principle of "manage by stages" and ensuring continued business justification. These gates require formal approval from the Project Board.

i.  **Initiation Authorization (Go/No-Go for Detailed Planning):**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm the project is viable and worth the effort of detailed planning.
    *   **Decision:** Based on the Project Brief and a high-level assessment of feasibility.
    *   **Authority:** Project Board.
    *   *Citation:* This aligns with PRINCE2's 'Starting Up a Project' process, ensuring a sound basis before significant investment [Ref 8].
ii. **Stage Authorization (Go/No-Go for Execution):**
    *   **Purpose:** To authorize the Project Manager to proceed with the next (or only) delivery stage.
    *   **Decision:** Based on the Project Initiation Documentation (PID), ensuring the project plan is robust and the business justification remains valid.
    *   **Authority:** Project Board.
    *   *Citation:* This is a core element of PRINCE2's 'Directing a Project' process and the concept of a defined project lifecycle with phases [Ref 13].
iii. **Project Closure Authorization (Acceptance & Formal Close):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally accept the project's deliverables and authorize the project's closure.
    *   **Decision:** Based on the End Project Report and verification that all objectives have been met and products handed over.
    *   **Authority:** Project Board.
    *   *Citation:* Part of PRINCE2's 'Closing a Project' process, ensuring proper handover and evaluation of project success [Ref 8].
