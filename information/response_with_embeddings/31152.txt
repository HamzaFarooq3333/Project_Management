#### I. Process Summary

This project management approach emphasizes a phased approach, focusing on delivering value incrementally and adapting to changing requirements within the constraints of a small project [Ref 1, Ref 2]. The methodology incorporates the PRINCE2 principles of a business justification, defined roles, product focus, adaptive planning, stage management, control, and risk management [Ref 5, Ref 9]. It acknowledges that a project is a temporary endeavour to achieve one or more defined objectives [Ref 6, Ref 14]. The project will be managed using a set of practices, incorporating principles from PRINCE2, to direct, initiate, plan, monitor, control and close the project [Ref 13].

This process focuses on project work, delivery, and measurement, with close attention to stakeholders, the team, and a tailored development approach [Ref 2]. Effective engagement with stakeholders is paramount [Ref 7], as well as ensuring a collaborative team environment [Ref 7].

#### II. Phases and Activities

The project will be managed through the following phases:

**I. Project Initiation**

   i. **Project Definition:** Define project scope, objectives, and deliverables. This includes identifying stakeholders and their expectations [Ref 3].
   ii. **Project Team Formation:** Assemble the project team, defining roles and responsibilities.
   iii. **Business Case Development:** Create a documented business case outlining the project's justification, benefits, and costs [Ref 5].
   iv. **Risk Assessment:** Conduct a preliminary risk assessment to identify potential threats and opportunities [Ref 10, Ref 19].
   v. **Planning:** Develop a project plan outlining tasks, timelines, budget, and resource allocation. Incorporate planning for product scope, project scope, and communication plans [Ref 4].
   vi. **Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Compile all project initiation information into a single, accessible document.

**II. Stage 1: Requirements Gathering and Design**

   i. **Requirements Elicitation:** Gather detailed requirements from stakeholders, including functional and non-functional requirements.
   ii. **Requirements Analysis:** Analyze and prioritize the gathered requirements.
   iii. **System Design:** Design the software architecture, user interface (UI), and database structure.
   iv. **Technical Design Documentation:** Create detailed documentation for the design elements.
   v. **Stage Planning:** Plan for the subsequent development stage.

**III. Stage 2: Development and Testing**

   i. **Software Development:** Develop the software modules based on the approved design documents.
   ii. **Unit Testing:** Conduct unit testing to ensure individual modules function correctly.
   iii. **Integration Testing:** Integrate modules and conduct integration testing.
   iv. **User Acceptance Testing (UAT):** Involve end-users in testing the software and verifying that it meets their requirements.
   v. **Defect Management:** Track, manage, and resolve identified defects.
   vi. **Stage Planning:** Plan for the final stage, including deployment.

**IV. Stage 3: Deployment and Acceptance**

   i. **Software Deployment:** Deploy the software to the target environment.
   ii. **User Training:** Train end-users on how to use the software.
   iii. **Acceptance Testing:** Conduct final acceptance testing to ensure the software meets the agreed-upon acceptance criteria.
   iv. **Post-Implementation Review:** Conduct a post-implementation review to assess the project’s success, lessons learned, and identify areas for improvement.
   v. **Project Closure:** Formally close the project, document final outcomes, and archive project documentation.

#### III. Roles

Key project roles and their responsibilities include:

*   **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for providing resources and executive decisions [Ref 6, Ref 17]. This person obtains the necessary resources and makes decisions to enable project success [Ref 6].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project. Manages the project's activities and resources [Ref 6].
*   **Team Lead:** Leads the development team, overseeing development activities and ensuring quality.
*   **Business Analyst (BA):** Gathers and documents requirements.
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Analyst:** Responsible for testing and ensuring the quality of the software.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by the project [Ref 3, Ref 6, Ref 18]. They may influence the project, be affected by it, or perceive themselves to be affected by it [Ref 3].
