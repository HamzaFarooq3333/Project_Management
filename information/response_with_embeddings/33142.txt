### Process Recommendation: Finance Research Project (PRINCE2, Small Scale)

#### Process Summary
This process recommendation outlines a project management framework for a small-scale finance research project, lasting less than 6 months, utilizing the PRINCE2 methodology. It adheres to project management guidance outlined in standards like ISO21502 [Ref 1], PMBOK [Ref 2], and emphasizes tailoring the approach to the project context [Ref 9]. It focuses on effectively planning project scope [Ref 3], engaging stakeholders [Ref 8, Ref 7], and managing potential ambiguity [Ref 4]. The approach emphasizes adaptability and resiliency [Ref 8] to ensure project success within the dynamic finance industry.

-----

#### Phases and Activities

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU):** This phase focuses on the initial assessment of the project's feasibility and justification, as well as defining the project mandate.
    i. Define the Project Mandate: Articulate the problem or opportunity driving the research.
    ii. Conduct a Project Brief: Develop a preliminary outline of the project's scope, objectives, and potential benefits, aligning with PRINCE2's emphasis on clarifying project deliverables [Ref 4].
    iii. Appoint the Project Management Team: Assign roles and responsibilities, establishing clear accountability [Ref 4].

II. **Initiating a Project (IP):** This phase builds upon the foundation laid in SU, creating a detailed project plan.
    i. Define Project Scope: Clearly articulate the research question, objectives, and deliverables. This needs to match the definition of project scope [Ref 3].
    ii. Develop the Project Initiation Documentation (PID): This comprehensive document outlines all aspects of the project, including the business case, project management plan, communication plan, and risk management approach.
    iii. Create a detailed schedule: Include tasks, durations, and dependencies to develop a detailed schedule for project tasks [Ref 3].

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS):** This phase focuses on the day-to-day management of the project activities within a defined stage. It involves monitoring progress, managing issues, and taking corrective actions as needed.
    i. Authorize Work Packages: Assign specific tasks to team members with clear deliverables and deadlines.
    ii. Monitor Progress: Track actual progress against the project plan, using techniques like earned value management.
    iii. Manage Issues and Risks: Identify, assess, and manage potential issues and risks that could impact the project's objectives [Ref 5, Ref 10].
    iv. Report Stage Progress: Provide regular updates to stakeholders on project progress, risks, and issues.

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP):** This process governs the creation and quality control of the project's deliverables.
    i. Execute Work Packages: Conduct the research activities as defined in the work packages.
    ii. Quality Review: Ensure that all deliverables meet the defined quality criteria.
    iii. Obtain Approval: Seek formal approval of completed deliverables from the relevant stakeholders.

V. **Managing Stage Boundaries (SB):** This process occurs at the end of each stage, assessing performance, updating the project plan, and seeking approval to proceed to the next stage.
    i. Review Stage Performance: Assess the stage's performance against the agreed-upon objectives and metrics.
    ii. Update the Project Plan: Refine the project plan based on the lessons learned from the previous stage.
    iii. Obtain Approval to Proceed: Seek approval from the Project Board to move to the next stage.

VI. **Closing a Project (CP):** This final phase involves formally closing the project, documenting lessons learned, and releasing resources.
    i. Confirm Project Completion: Verify that all project objectives have been met and deliverables have been accepted.
    ii. Document Lessons Learned: Capture key lessons learned throughout the project to improve future projects.
    iii. Release Resources: Reassign project resources to other projects or activities.
    iv. Conduct Project Closure Meeting: Formally close the project with key stakeholders.

#### Roles

*   **Executive:** Provides overall direction and ensures alignment with organizational strategy.
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of the users of the research findings.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of the individuals providing the research expertise.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project.
*   **Team Manager:** Responsible for managing a team of researchers.
*   **Project Assurance:** Monitors the project's progress and ensures adherence to PRINCE2 principles.

#### Decision Gates

I. **End of Starting Up a Project (SU):** Is the project feasible and justified? Approval is required to proceed to the Initiating a Project phase.
II. **End of Initiating a Project (IP):** Is the project plan comprehensive and realistic? Approval is required to proceed to the first delivery stage.
III. **End of Each Stage (SB):** Has the stage achieved its objectives? Are there any significant deviations from the project plan? Decision on whether to proceed to the next stage or to close the project.
IV. **Project Closure (CP):** Have all project objectives been met and deliverables accepted? Decision on formal project closure. ISO21502 outlines what is expected here [Ref 14].
