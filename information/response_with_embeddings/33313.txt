### Process Recommendation for Large-Scale IT Research Project (ISO Standards)

**1. Process Summary**

This process recommendation outlines a structured approach for managing a large-scale ( > 18 months) information technology research project, adhering to ISO standards as a methodology. The process emphasizes a phased approach with distinct activities, clear roles and responsibilities, and defined decision gates to ensure project success [Ref 2].  It incorporates planning, execution, monitoring and control, and closure phases, drawing upon best practices from PMBOK and PRINCE2 to complement the ISO framework [Ref 1, Ref 3]. Stakeholder engagement and adaptability are critical components [Ref 5, Ref 15]. The process is designed to be tailored to the specific context of the project [Ref 15].

-----

**2. Phases and Activities**

The project lifecycle will be divided into the following phases:

I. **Initiation:**
  i. Define project objectives and scope [Ref 1, Ref 6].
  ii. Identify key stakeholders [Ref 5, Ref 18].
  iii. Develop a project charter and secure approval [Ref 7, Ref 10].
  iv. Conduct a preliminary risk assessment [Ref 4, Ref 14].
  v. Establish project governance principles [Ref 7].

II. **Planning:**
 i. Develop a detailed project management plan, including scope, schedule, budget, resources, communication, risk, and quality management plans [Ref 1, Ref 13].
 ii. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
 iii. Define project deliverables and acceptance criteria [Ref 10].
 iv. Estimate effort, cost, and duration for each activity [Ref 1].
 v. Develop a realistic project schedule using appropriate scheduling techniques [Ref 1].
 vi. Identify and plan for resource requirements [Ref 3].
 vii. Establish a change management process [Ref 1].
 viii. Develop a communication plan to ensure effective stakeholder engagement [Ref 1, Ref 5].
 ix. Formulate a procurement strategy [Ref 1].

III. **Execution:**
 i. Execute project activities according to the project management plan [Ref 13].
 ii. Manage and coordinate project team resources [Ref 13].
 iii. Implement the communication plan to keep stakeholders informed [Ref 1].
 iv. Manage risks and issues proactively [Ref 10, Ref 14].
 v. Track project progress against the baseline schedule and budget [Ref 5].
 vi. Ensure quality standards are met [Ref 15].

IV. **Monitoring and Control:**
 i. Monitor project performance against the project management plan [Ref 13].
 ii. Track key performance indicators (KPIs) [Ref 5].
 iii. Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget using the change management process [Ref 1].
 iv. Identify and resolve issues promptly [Ref 10].
 v. Report project status to stakeholders regularly [Ref 5].
 vi. Perform regular risk assessments and implement mitigation strategies [Ref 4, Ref 14].

V. **Closure:**
 i. Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders [Ref 10].
 ii. Conduct a post-project review to identify lessons learned [Ref 9].
 iii. Document and archive project artifacts [Ref 9].
 iv. Close out contracts and finalize procurement activities [Ref 1].
 v. Celebrate project success and recognize team contributions [Ref 15].

**3. Roles**

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, resources, and support for the project [Ref 6].
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing the project [Ref 13].
iii. **Research Lead:** Responsible for the technical direction of the research and ensuring its quality.
iv. **Team Members:** Responsible for completing assigned tasks and contributing to the project deliverables [Ref 2, Ref 11].
v. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in or are affected by the project [Ref 6].

**5. Decision Gates**

i. **End of Initiation Phase:** Decision gate to approve the project charter and authorize the project to proceed to the planning phase. This involves reviewing the defined objectives, scope, and stakeholder analysis [Ref 7].
ii. **End of Planning Phase:** Decision gate to approve the project management plan. This involves reviewing the detailed plans for scope, schedule, budget, resources, communication, risk, and quality management [Ref 1, Ref 13].
iii. **Mid-Project Review:** A decision gate at the halfway point of the project (approximately 9 months) to assess project performance, identify any issues or risks, and determine whether to continue, modify, or terminate the project [Ref 5].
iv. **Pre-Closure Review:** Verify that all deliverables have been completed and accepted, and that all project documentation is up to date. [Ref 10].
