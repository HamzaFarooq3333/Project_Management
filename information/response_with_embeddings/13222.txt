#### 2. Phases and Activities

The project will be structured in phases, each with specific activities aligned with PRINCE2 principles.

**I. Project Initiation**

*   **Activities:**
    i. Define the Project Scope: Detail the specific objectives of the research project, deliverables, and success criteria. [Ref 2, Ref 5, Ref 7].
    ii. Conduct a Feasibility Study: Assess the project's viability, including technical feasibility, resource availability, and potential risks.
    iii. Develop the Project Initiation Documentation (PID): This document will formalize the project's foundation, including the project mandate, project brief, project approach, and key stakeholder information.
    iv. Identify Stakeholders and their Interests: Identify all stakeholders impacted by the project, including their requirements and expectations [Ref 8].
    v. Establish Project Governance: Define the reporting structure, decision-making processes, and project board composition. [Ref 9]

**II. Project Planning**

*   **Activities:**
    i. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): Decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 5].
    ii. Develop the Project Schedule: Create a detailed schedule outlining tasks, dependencies, and timelines.
    iii. Estimate Costs and Budget: Determine the resources required and develop a comprehensive budget [Ref 2].
    iv. Develop a Risk Management Plan: Identify, assess, and plan responses to potential risks [Ref 10].
    v. Develop a Communication Plan: Define communication channels, frequency, and stakeholders [Ref 2].
    vi. Develop a Quality Management Strategy: Define how quality will be assured throughout the project lifecycle.
    vii. Plan for resource procurement. [Ref 2]

**III. Project Execution and Monitoring**

*   **Activities:**
    i. Execute Work Packages: Carry out the activities defined in the WBS.
    ii. Monitor Progress: Track progress against the schedule, budget, and scope.
    iii. Manage Risks: Implement risk responses as defined in the risk management plan.
    iv. Manage Issues: Address and resolve issues as they arise [Ref 7].
    v. Manage Changes: Implement the change control process to handle scope changes. [Ref 2]
    vi. Manage communications: Ensure project information is shared properly with stakeholders

**IV. Project Closure**

*   **Activities:**
    i. Project Product Acceptance: Confirm that project deliverables meet the agreed acceptance criteria.
    ii. Project Evaluation: Conduct a post-project review to capture lessons learned.
    iii. Update Documentation: Finalize project documentation and archive project records.
    iv. Communicate Project Closure: Inform stakeholders of the project's completion.

#### 3. Roles

*   **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 8].
*   **Project Board:** Provides direction and overall management of the project, including approving plans, and changes.
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for day-to-day management of the project, including planning, execution, and control.
*   **Team Manager:** Manages the resources, activities, and deliverables of a specific team or area of the project
*   **Quality Assurance:** Responsible for ensuring quality standards are met
*   **Project Support:** Assists the project manager with administrative tasks, reporting, and other support activities.
