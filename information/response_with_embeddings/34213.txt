### Process Recommendation for Medium-Sized IT Marketing Project (ISO Compliant)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a project management approach for a medium-sized (6-18 months) marketing project within the Information Technology industry, adhering to ISO project management standards, specifically ISO 21502 [Ref 3] and informed by PMBOK principles [Ref 1, Ref 2]. The process emphasizes a structured approach incorporating planning, execution, monitoring, controlling, and closure. The plan highlights stakeholder engagement [Ref 7], risk management [Ref 15], and adaptability [Ref 12] to address project complexity. The focus is on defining clear objectives, managing resources efficiently, and delivering value [Ref 12]. The methodology is designed to be adaptable to different delivery approaches (predictive, incremental, iterative, adaptive, or hybrid) [Ref 3], enabling tailoring based on the project's specific needs.

### Phases and Activities:

This project will adhere to a phased approach, including:

**I. Initiation:**

i. **Project Definition:** Clearly define the project objectives, scope, and deliverables [Ref 3].
ii. **Stakeholder Identification:** Identify all relevant stakeholders (internal and external) and their interests [Ref 7, Ref 5].
iii. **Project Charter Development:** Create a project charter outlining the project's purpose, high-level requirements, and initial budget [Ref 1, Ref 2].
iv. **Feasibility Assessment:** Assess the feasibility of the project, considering resources, technology, and market conditions [Ref 10, Ref 14].
v. **Sponsor Assignment:** Identify and assign a project sponsor responsible for securing resources and making executive decisions [Ref 7].

**II. Planning:**

i. **Scope Management:** Define the project scope in detail, creating a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to break down the project into manageable work packages [Ref 5].
ii. **Schedule Development:** Develop a project schedule, estimating activity durations, dependencies, and milestones [Ref 1].
iii. **Resource Planning:** Identify and allocate the necessary resources (human, financial, and technological) [Ref 4, Ref 14].
iv. **Budgeting:** Develop a detailed project budget based on resource requirements and activity durations [Ref 1].
v. **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities), assess their impact and probability, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 15, Ref 8].
vi. **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan outlining how project information will be disseminated to stakeholders [Ref 1].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** Plan for any necessary procurements, including vendor selection and contract management [Ref 1].

**III. Execution:**

i. **Task Execution:** Execute the project tasks according to the project schedule and plan [Ref 2].
ii. **Team Management:** Manage the project team, providing guidance, support, and motivation [Ref 2].
iii. **Quality Assurance:** Implement quality assurance processes to ensure that project deliverables meet the required standards [Ref 12].
iv. **Communication:** Communicate project status, issues, and risks to stakeholders [Ref 1].
v. **Change Management:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget using a formal change management process [Ref 1].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling:**

i. **Performance Measurement:** Monitor project performance against the project plan, tracking progress, costs, and risks [Ref 1, Ref 2].
ii. **Variance Analysis:** Analyze variances between planned and actual performance, identifying root causes and implementing corrective actions.
iii. **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as needed [Ref 15].
iv. **Change Control:** Manage change requests, assessing their impact on the project and implementing approved changes.
v. **Reporting:** Regularly report project status to stakeholders, providing updates on progress, issues, and risks.

**V. Closure:**

i. **Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders [Ref 2].
ii. **Project Documentation:** Finalize project documentation, including project plans, reports, and lessons learned.
iii. **Team Release:** Release the project team members [Ref 6].
iv. **Contract Closure:** Close out any contracts with vendors or suppliers.
v. **Final Reporting:** Prepare a final project report summarizing project outcomes and lessons learned [Ref 6].
vi. **Project Archiving:** Archive project documentation for future reference.

### Roles:

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, secures resources, and makes key decisions [Ref 7].
ii. **Project Manager:** Plans, executes, monitors, and controls the project [Ref 14].
iii. **Marketing Team:** Executes marketing tasks and activities.
iv. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project or are affected by it [Ref 5].
v. **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):** Provides technical expertise and guidance.
