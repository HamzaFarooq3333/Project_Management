iv. **Appoint Project Sponsor and Project Manager:** Formally assign the project sponsor, responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 6], and the project manager, who will lead coordinated activities to direct and control project objectives [Ref 6].
v.  **Formulate Project Governance Structure:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project will be authorized and directed to accomplish agreed objectives [Ref 9, Ref 12].

#### II. Project Planning

iii. **Estimate, Schedule, and Budget:** Develop detailed estimates for resources, duration, and costs [Ref 3]. Create a project schedule that sequences activities and assigns timelines, and establish a comprehensive budget.
iv. **Plan for Team Composition and Resource Acquisition:** Determine the required skills and roles for the project team, and plan for their acquisition and development [Ref 3]. Foster a collaborative project team environment [Ref 7].
v.  **Develop Communication Plan:** Outline how project information will be exchanged, when, and with whom [Ref 3], ensuring effective engagement with stakeholders [Ref 7].

vii. **Establish Risk Management Strategy:** Identify potential threats (risk occurrences with negative impact) and opportunities, analyze their impact, and plan appropriate responses [Ref 4, Ref 10]. Optimize risk responses [Ref 7].
viii. **Develop Change Management Plan:** Define the process for requesting, evaluating, and approving changes to the project baseline [Ref 3], enabling change to achieve the envisioned future state [Ref 7].
ix. **Build Quality Management into Processes:** Define how quality will be assured and controlled throughout the project, building quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 7].

### Roles

The success of this infrastructure project relies on clear roles and responsibilities.

i.  **Project Sponsor:** The person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable project success [Ref 6]. They are the ultimate authority for the project and champion its objectives.
ii. **Project Manager:** The individual who leads the coordinated activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 6]. Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project.
iii. **Project Team Members:** Individuals performing the work of the project, responsible for specific tasks and deliverables. They contribute to creating a collaborative project team environment [Ref 7].
iv. **Key Stakeholders:** Persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by, any aspect of the project [Ref 6, Ref 2]. This includes users (students, faculty), facilities management, local community representatives, and regulatory bodies.
v.  **Functional Managers:** Managers of departments or functional units who provide resources or expertise to the project.
vi. **Quality Assurance/Control Lead:** Responsible for ensuring that processes and deliverables meet quality standards [Ref 7].

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project lifecycle where formal reviews occur, ensuring that the project remains aligned with objectives and standards, and is authorized to proceed to the next phase [Ref 9]. These gates serve as governance checkpoints [Ref 12].

i.  **Gate 1: Project Authorization (After Initiation Phase)**
    *   **Purpose:** To confirm the project's strategic alignment, feasibility, and the readiness of the Project Charter.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for detailed planning.
    *   **Evidence:** Approved Project Charter, preliminary stakeholder analysis, initial risk assessment.
    *   **Citation:** Project governance involves principles, policies, and procedures by which a project is authorized and directed [Ref 9, Ref 12].

ii. **Gate 2: Plan Approval (After Planning Phase)**
    *   **Purpose:** To ensure the comprehensive project management plan is complete, realistic, and acceptable to all key stakeholders.
    *   **Decision:** Approval to proceed with project execution.
    *   **Evidence:** Approved Project Management Plan (including scope, schedule, budget, risk, quality, communication, procurement, and change management plans) [Ref 3].
    *   **Citation:** Planning encompasses defining product/project scope, estimating, scheduling, budgeting, and various management plans [Ref 3].

iii. **Gate 3: Mid-Project Performance Review (Mid-Execution Phase)**
    *   **Purpose:** To assess actual project performance against the plan, address any significant deviations, and confirm continued viability.
    *   **Decision:** Continue, adjust, or potentially re-plan.
    *   **Evidence:** Performance reports, updated risk register, issue log, quality metrics.
    *   **Citation:** Measurement is a key project performance domain [Ref 1].

iv. **Gate 4: Pre-Closure Review (End of Execution/Start of Closure)**
    *   **Purpose:** To verify that all major deliverables have been completed, tested, and are ready for final acceptance.
    *   **Decision:** Authorize formal project closure activities.
    *   **Evidence:** Completed deliverables, test results, stakeholder acceptance forms.
