### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will follow a predictive approach, given the research nature and ISO standard compliance, but will allow for incremental or iterative delivery within phases where appropriate [Ref 3, Ref 9].

#### I. Project Initiation
The initial phase focuses on defining the project at a high level and establishing foundational elements.
i.  **Define Project Purpose and Objectives:** Clearly articulate the research goals and desired outcomes, recognizing the project as a temporary endeavor to achieve defined objectives [Ref 8].
ii. **Identify and Engage Key Stakeholders:** Identify all persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, can affect, or be affected by the project [Ref 5, Ref 8]. Develop a strategy for effective engagement [Ref 10].
iii. **Develop Project Charter:** Document the high-level project scope, objectives, initial budget, timeline, and assign the Project Manager.
iv. **Establish Project Governance:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project will be authorized and directed to accomplish its agreed objectives [Ref 7, Ref 9].
v.  **Formulate Initial Research Scope:** Outline the broad areas of construction research to be undertaken.
vi. **Appoint Project Manager and Research Lead:** Assign leadership roles to guide the project effectively.

#### II. Project Planning
This phase involves detailed definition and planning across all project management areas [Ref 2].
i.  **Develop Project Management Plan (PMP):** A comprehensive document detailing how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed.
ii. **Define Product and Project Scope:** Clearly define what the research will deliver (product scope) and the work required to deliver it (project scope) [Ref 2]. This includes defining deliverables as unique and verifiable elements required to be produced [Ref 7].
iii. **Create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Decompose the defined scope into progressively lower levels of work elements, including work packages [Ref 5].
iv. **Estimate and Schedule Activities:** Develop detailed estimates for resources, duration, and costs for all work packages [Ref 2]. Create a project schedule.
v.  **Develop Budget:** Consolidate all cost estimates into a comprehensive project budget [Ref 2].
vi. **Plan Resources:** Determine team composition, skills, and availability [Ref 2].
vii. **Develop Communication Plan:** Define how information will be shared, with whom, and how frequently [Ref 2]. Emphasis on effective stakeholder engagement [Ref 10].
viii. **Plan Procurement:** Identify any external services, materials, or equipment required and plan for their acquisition [Ref 2].
ix. **Plan Risk Management:** Identify potential threats and opportunities [Ref 5], analyze their impact, and plan appropriate responses to optimize risk [Ref 10].
x.  **Plan Quality Management:** Determine quality standards and processes to build quality into processes and deliverables [Ref 10].
xi. **Plan Change Management:** Establish processes for managing project changes [Ref 2].
xii. **Define Measurement Criteria:** Outline how project performance will be measured and reported [Ref 2].

#### III. Project Execution
This phase involves carrying out the planned work to achieve project objectives.
i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the activities defined in the PMP to perform the project work [Ref 1].
ii. **Acquire, Develop, and Manage Project Team:** Foster a collaborative team environment [Ref 10].
iii. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Continuously engage with stakeholders as per the communication plan [Ref 1, Ref 10].
iv. **Conduct Research Activities:** Implement the research methodology, including data collection, experimentation, and initial analysis.
v.  **Manage Communications:** Distribute information as planned.
vi. **Implement Risk Responses:** Execute planned strategies for identified risks [Ref 10].
vii. **Conduct Procurements:** Administer contracts with external suppliers.
viii. **Produce Deliverables:** Create the tangible or intangible outcomes as defined [Ref 7].

#### IV. Project Monitoring & Controlling
This phase involves tracking, reviewing, and regulating the progress and performance of the project.
i.  **Monitor Project Work:** Track overall project progress against the PMP [Ref 1].
ii. **Control Scope:** Ensure all changes to project scope are managed through the change management process [Ref 2].
iii. **Control Schedule and Cost:** Monitor actual progress and expenditure against the baseline, taking corrective actions as needed.
iv. **Perform Quality Assurance and Control:** Monitor specific project results to determine if they comply with relevant quality standards and identify ways to improve overall project performance [Ref 10].
v.  **Monitor and Control Risks:** Continuously identify, analyze, and plan for new or evolving risks [Ref 5, Ref 10].
vi. **Manage Issues:** Address any events that arise during the project that require management action [Ref 7].
vii. **Monitor Stakeholder Engagement:** Assess the effectiveness of stakeholder engagement strategies [Ref 1, Ref 10].

#### V. Project Closure
The final phase involves formally completing all project activities and deliverables [Ref 6].
i.  **Close Project or Phase:** Formally finalize all activities across all process groups to complete the project [Ref 6, Ref 9].
ii. **Finalize All Deliverables:** Ensure all unique and verifiable elements required by the project are completed and accepted [Ref 7].
iii. **Obtain Formal Acceptance:** Secure formal acceptance of the project outcomes from the sponsor and relevant stakeholders.
iv. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document project successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
v.  **Archive Project Documents:** Organize and store all project records.
vi. **Release Resources:** Formally release project team members and other resources.
vii. **Close Procurements:** Finalize all contracts and agreements.

### Roles

#### I. Project Sponsor

ii. Provides high-level strategic guidance and resolves significant roadblocks.

#### II. Project Manager

ii. Applies methods, tools, techniques, and competencies to enable the project to meet its objectives [Ref 4, Ref 15].
iii. Leads the project team, manages day-to-day activities, and ensures adherence to the PMP.

#### III. Research Lead (Principal Investigator)

ii. Provide input, feedback, and support throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 10].

#### VI. Quality Assurance/Control Specialist
i.  Ensures that quality is built into processes and deliverables [Ref 10].
ii. Monitors compliance with ISO standards and project quality requirements.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points within the project lifecycle, ensuring that the project proceeds only when defined criteria are met and approved, aligning with effective project governance [Ref 9].

1.  **Project Charter Approval Gate:**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally authorize the project and commit initial resources.
    *   **Criteria:** Approved Project Charter, clear high-level objectives, identified Project Sponsor and Project Manager, initial justification.
    *   **Decision By:** Project Sponsor.
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go decision to proceed with detailed planning.

2.  **Project Management Plan (PMP) Approval Gate:**
    *   **Purpose:** To approve the comprehensive plan for how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed.
    *   **Criteria:** Fully developed PMP covering scope, schedule, budget, resources, risks, quality, and communication plans [Ref 2]. Clear deliverables defined [Ref 7].
    *   **Decision By:** Project Sponsor (and possibly key stakeholders/governance body).
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go decision to proceed with project execution.

3.  **Research Methodology & Design Approval Gate:**
    *   **Purpose:** To ensure the research approach is sound, feasible, and aligns with project objectives and ISO standards.
    *   **Criteria:** Approved detailed research methodology, ethical clearances (if required), and pilot study results (if applicable).
    *   **Decision By:** Project Manager, Research Lead, Project Sponsor, and relevant Subject Matter Experts.
    *   **Outcome:** Go/No-Go decision to commence full-scale data collection/experimentation.

4.  **Major Deliverable Acceptance Gates:**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally accept key research outcomes or project phases.
    *   **Criteria:** Completed and quality-assured deliverables meeting defined acceptance criteria [Ref 7, Ref 10].
    *   **Decision By:** Project Manager, Research Lead, and relevant Key Stakeholders/Sponsor.
    *   **Outcome:** Formal acceptance of research results or phase completion, allowing progression to subsequent phases or final analysis.

5.  **Project Closure Approval Gate:**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally close the project and confirm all objectives have been met.
    *   **Criteria:** All deliverables accepted [Ref 7], final project report completed, lessons learned documented, financial closure, and formal release of resources [Ref 6].
    *   **Decision By:** Project Sponsor.
    *   **Outcome:** Official project closure.
