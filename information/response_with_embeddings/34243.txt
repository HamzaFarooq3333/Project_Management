### Process Recommendation: Finance Marketing Project (ISO Standards)

**Process Summary:**

This document outlines a project management process recommendation for a medium-sized (6-18 months) marketing project within the finance industry, adhering to ISO project management standards (ISO 21500 and ISO 21502) [Ref 1, Ref 7]. This process emphasizes stakeholder engagement, comprehensive planning, adaptable execution, and robust monitoring & control, incorporating principles from PMBOK [Ref 2, Ref 10] to ensure successful project delivery. The approach recognizes the importance of tailoring the process to the specific organizational context [Ref 15] and acknowledges that the project's success is influenced by factors both within and outside the organization. A hybrid approach, as suggested by ISO21502 [Ref 1], is assumed and tailoring for Agile is incorporated, emphasizing iterative development and continuous improvement.

-----

### Phases and Activities:

The project will be managed across the following phases:

I. **Initiation:**
    i.  **Define Project Scope & Objectives:** Clearly define the project scope, objectives, and deliverables, aligning with the organization's strategic goals. Establish measurable success criteria. Relates to planning [Ref 3]
    ii. **Identify Key Stakeholders:** Identify all relevant stakeholders and their interests, establishing communication channels and engagement strategies [Ref 6].
    iii. **Appoint Project Sponsor & Core Team:** Secure a project sponsor to provide executive support and assemble a competent project team.
    iv. **Develop Project Charter:** Create a project charter that formally authorizes the project, defines roles and responsibilities, and outlines high-level timelines and budget.

II. **Planning:**
    i.  **Develop Project Management Plan:** Create a comprehensive project management plan that outlines all aspects of the project, including scope management, schedule management, cost management, risk management, communication management, and quality management [Ref 3].
    ii. **Define Product Scope:** Deliberate on product scope for marketing assets [Ref 3].
    iii. **Develop Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4], defining tasks, deliverables, and dependencies.
    iv. **Estimate Resources & Develop Schedule:** Estimate the resources required for each task and develop a realistic project schedule with clear milestones and deadlines [Ref 3].
    v.  **Develop Budget:** Create a detailed project budget based on resource estimates and schedule, including contingency reserves [Ref 3].
    vi. **Perform Risk Assessment:** Identify potential risks and opportunities, assess their impact and probability, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 4].
    vii. **Create Communication Plan:** Establish a clear communication plan that outlines how information will be disseminated to stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 3].

III. **Execution:**
    i.  **Execute Project Tasks:** Implement the tasks defined in the WBS, following the project schedule and budget.
    ii. **Manage Project Team:** Provide leadership, guidance, and support to the project team, ensuring effective collaboration and communication [Ref 2].
    iii. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Regularly communicate with stakeholders, addressing their concerns and providing updates on project progress. [Ref 10]
    iv. **Implement Risk Mitigation Strategies:** Execute risk mitigation strategies as needed to address identified risks and issues.
    v.  **Manage Changes:** Implement a change management process to handle scope changes, schedule adjustments, and budget revisions [Ref 3].

IV. **Monitoring & Control:**
    i.  **Track Project Progress:** Monitor project progress against the project plan, tracking key performance indicators (KPIs) and identifying any deviations [Ref 2].
    ii. **Manage Issues & Risks:** Proactively identify and resolve issues, and manage risks according to the risk management plan.
    iii. **Control Scope, Schedule & Costs:** Implement change control processes to manage scope creep, schedule delays, and budget overruns [Ref 3].
    iv. **Report Project Status:** Regularly report project status to stakeholders, providing updates on progress, issues, and risks.

V. **Closure:**
    i.  **Obtain Project Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
    ii. **Conduct Project Post-Mortem:** Conduct a project post-mortem to identify lessons learned and areas for improvement.
    iii. **Document Project Outcomes:** Document project outcomes, including final deliverables, project performance data, and lessons learned.
    iv. **Close Project Accounts:** Close all project accounts and finalize project financials.
    v.  **Release Project Resources:** Release project resources back to the organization.

-----

### Roles:

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides executive support, approves project charter and budget, and resolves high-level issues. [Ref 6]
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project.
iii. **Marketing Team Lead:** Leads the marketing team and ensures the successful delivery of marketing deliverables.
iv. **Finance Representative:** Provides financial expertise and ensures compliance with financial regulations.
v.  **Stakeholders:** Provide input, review deliverables, and approve project outcomes.

### Decision Gates:

i. **End of Initiation Phase:** Decision to proceed with the project based on the completed project charter and stakeholder alignment.
ii. **End of Planning Phase:** Decision to proceed with execution based on the finalized project management plan, budget approval, and risk assessment.
iii. **Mid-Project Review:** Assessment of project progress against the plan, identification of potential risks or issues, and decision to adjust the plan if necessary.
iv. **Pre-Closure Review:** Review of completed deliverables against acceptance criteria, confirmation of stakeholder satisfaction, and decision to proceed with project closure.
