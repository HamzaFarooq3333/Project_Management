#### 2. Phases and Activities

The project will be divided into the following phases:

**I. Initiation**

i.  **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's objectives, scope, and key stakeholders. This involves clarifying the marketing goals, target audience, and success metrics. The project sponsor and key stakeholders should be identified [Ref 6].
ii.  **Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify and analyze all stakeholders, their interests, and their influence. This analysis is crucial for managing expectations and communication [Ref 1].
iii. **Scope Definition:** Develop a detailed scope statement, outlining the project deliverables and the work required to produce them [Ref 3].

**II. Planning**

i.  **Scope Planning:** Develop a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 4].
ii.  **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and resource allocation. Use scheduling software to facilitate tracking.
iii. **Budgeting:** Develop a detailed budget, accounting for all project costs, including labor, materials, and marketing expenses [Ref 3].
iv.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and secure all necessary resources, including personnel, equipment, and materials [Ref 5].
v.  **Communication Plan:** Develop a communication plan to outline how project information will be shared with stakeholders, including frequency, methods, and responsibilities [Ref 3].
vi.  **Risk Management:** Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities), assess their likelihood and impact, and develop mitigation plans [Ref 15].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** If applicable, plan for the procurement of external services or resources.

**III. Execution**

i.  **Marketing Campaign Execution:** Implement the marketing strategies and tactics outlined in the plan.
ii.  **Team Management:** Manage the project team, ensuring they have the resources and support needed to perform their tasks.
iii.  **Stakeholder Management:** Regularly engage with stakeholders, providing updates and addressing concerns [Ref 7].
iv. **Change Management:** Implement the change management process to control changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling**

i.  **Performance Tracking:** Track project progress against the plan, using key performance indicators (KPIs) to monitor performance.
ii.  **Issue Management:** Identify, document, and resolve project issues promptly.
iii.  **Risk Monitoring:** Monitor identified risks, triggering contingency plans as needed.
iv.  **Quality Assurance:** Ensure the quality of project deliverables meets established standards.
v. **Reporting:** Provide regular status reports to stakeholders, highlighting progress, issues, and risks.

**V. Closure**

i.  **Project Deliverable Review and Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables from the client or sponsor.
ii.  **Project Documentation:** Finalize and archive all project documentation, including lessons learned.
iii.  **Project Closure:** Formally close out the project, ensuring all contracts are closed and all resources are released.
