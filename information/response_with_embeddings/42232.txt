### Process Recommendation: Medium-Scale Healthcare Construction Project (PRINCE2)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a PRINCE2-based approach for managing a medium-scale healthcare construction project, expected to last between 6-18 months. The process integrates elements from PMBOK, ISO21502, and PRINCE2 standards [Ref 3, Ref 5]. It emphasizes a structured, controlled, and adaptable framework, prioritizing stakeholder engagement, risk management, and value delivery, as per the project management principles outlined in PMBOK [Ref 6, Ref 12]. The process follows the PRINCE2 lifecycle, incorporates robust planning and control mechanisms, and emphasizes tailoring to the specific context of the healthcare industry and project [Ref 7].

-----

### Phases and Activities

I. **Starting Up a Project (SU)**

   i.   Appoint the Project Board and Project Manager.
   ii.  Develop the Project Brief including the project mandate.
   iii. Define the Project Approach
   iv.  Plan the Initiation Stage. The purpose of the SU process is to ensure that prerequisites for initiation are in place by answering the question: "Do we have a viable and worthwhile project?"

II. **Initiating a Project (IP)**

    i. Assemble the Project Initiation Documentation (PID) including the Project Plan and detailed risk management approach.
    ii. Refine the Business Case. This phase focuses on establishing a solid foundation for the project, ensuring a clear understanding of its objectives, benefits, and feasibility [Ref 2].
    iii. Risk assessment and planning (including threats and opportunities)[Ref 11]

III. **Controlling a Stage (CS)**

   i. Authorize Work Packages [Ref 4].
   ii. Monitor Progress. This involves ongoing monitoring of project progress against the plan, taking corrective actions as necessary, and managing issues [Ref 8, Ref 9].
   iii. Review Stage Status.
   iv. Report Highlights
   v. Capture and assess project issues and risks.

IV. **Managing Product Delivery (MP)**

   i. Accept Work Packages.
   ii. Execute Work Packages (focusing on deliverable quality).
   iii. Deliver completed products. This phase focuses on the execution of the project plan, producing the agreed-upon deliverables, and ensuring quality standards are met [Ref 8].

V. **Managing Stage Boundary (SB)**

   i. Plan the next stage.
   ii. Update the Project Plan.
   iii. Update the Business Case. Ensure all documentation is current before proceeding to the next phase.

VI. **Closing a Project (CP)**

   i. Handover project products.
   ii. Evaluate the project.
   iii. Recommend project closure. Focuses on formally closing the project, handing over deliverables, conducting a post-project review, and documenting lessons learned. This also includes stakeholder communication [Ref 1, Ref 6].

-----

### Roles

i. **Executive:** Responsible for the overall success of the project, representing the business interests.
ii. **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's deliverables.
iii. **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those providing the resources and expertise to the project.
iv. **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project.
v. **Project Assurance (Business, User, Technical):** Monitors the project on behalf of the Project Board.
vi. **Team Manager:** Manages a team responsible for delivering one or more Work Packages.

### Decision Gates

i.   **End of Starting Up (SU):** Approval to proceed to Initiation stage, based on the Project Brief and Initiation Stage Plan.
ii.  **End of Initiating (IP):** Approval of the Project Initiation Documentation (PID), confirming the project's feasibility and justification. Decision to proceed to the first delivery stage. [Ref 2, Ref 13]
iii. **End of Each Stage (Managing Stage Boundary - SB):** Review of stage performance, updated Project Plan, and Business Case. Approval to proceed to the next stage.
iv.  **Project Closure (CP):** Formal acceptance of the project deliverables and sign-off from the Project Board.
