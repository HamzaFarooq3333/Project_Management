### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will be divided into four distinct phases, each with specific objectives and activities, ensuring governance and control throughout the endeavor [Ref 8, Ref 9].

#### I. Initiation Phase

This phase establishes the project's foundation, ensuring all critical aspects are understood and agreed upon before significant commitment.
i.  **Define Project Objectives and Scope:** Clearly articulate what the education marketing project aims to achieve and its boundaries. A project is a "temporary endeavour to achieve one or more defined objectives" [Ref 6, Ref 14].
ii. **Identify and Engage Stakeholders:** Identify all individuals, groups, or organizations who have interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by, any aspect of the project [Ref 4, Ref 6]. This includes faculty, admissions, marketing, IT, and target audience representatives. Effective engagement is paramount [Ref 7].
iii. **Appoint Project Sponsor:** Secure a person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success [Ref 6].
iv. **Develop Project Charter:** Document the project's purpose, objectives, high-level scope, key stakeholders, and assigned Project Manager, obtaining formal authorization to start the project. This aligns with setting the context for project management [Ref 11].
v. **High-Level Risk Identification:** Conduct an initial scan for potential threats and opportunities [Ref 8, Ref 15].

#### II. Planning Phase

This phase develops the detailed roadmap for project execution, translating the high-level scope into actionable plans.
i.  **Refine Product and Project Scope:** Clearly define what the marketing materials/campaigns will deliver (product scope) and the work required to deliver it (project scope) [Ref 3].
ii. **Develop Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Decompose the defined scope into progressively lower levels of work elements, leading to manageable work packages [Ref 4].
iii. **Estimate Activities:** Determine the resources, effort, and duration required for each work package [Ref 3].
iv. **Develop Project Schedule:** Sequence activities and assign estimated durations to create a realistic project timeline, considering the < 6 months constraint [Ref 3].
v. **Establish Project Budget:** Allocate financial resources based on estimates, including marketing spend, personnel costs, and tools [Ref 3].
vi. **Formulate Team Composition:** Identify and define roles for the project team, ensuring necessary competencies are available [Ref 3].
vii. **Create Communication Plan:** Outline how and when project information will be exchanged with stakeholders, aligning with effective stakeholder engagement [Ref 3, Ref 7].
viii. **Plan Procurement (if applicable):** If external vendors (e.g., ad agencies, graphic designers) are needed, plan the process for acquiring their services [Ref 3].
ix. **Define Change Management Process:** Establish how changes to the project plan will be requested, evaluated, and approved [Ref 3].
x. **Develop Measurement Strategy:** Define key performance indicators (KPIs) and how project progress and success will be measured against objectives [Ref 1, Ref 3].
xi. **Optimize Risk Responses:** Develop strategies to address identified risks, both positive (opportunities) and negative (threats), ensuring responses are appropriate and cost-effective [Ref 15].

#### III. Execution and Monitoring & Controlling Phase (Project Work and Delivery)

This integrated phase focuses on carrying out the planned work, monitoring progress, managing deviations, and ensuring quality.
i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the activities defined in the project plan to create marketing deliverables [Ref 1].
ii. **Acquire and Develop Project Team:** Onboard team members and foster a collaborative environment [Ref 7].
iii. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Continuously communicate and work with stakeholders to meet their needs and expectations [Ref 1, Ref 7].
iv. **Implement Communication Plan:** Distribute project information according to the defined plan.
v. **Perform Quality Assurance:** Build quality into processes and deliverables to ensure they meet specified requirements [Ref 7].
vi. **Monitor and Control Project Work:** Track progress against the schedule and budget [Ref 1, Ref 3].
vii. **Manage Risks:** Continuously identify new risks, re-evaluate existing ones, and implement risk response plans [Ref 1, Ref 15].
viii. **Manage Changes:** Process change requests according to the defined change management process.
ix. **Report Performance:** Regularly communicate project status to stakeholders, including progress, budget, and risks [Ref 1].

#### IV. Closure Phase

This phase formally concludes the project, ensuring all activities are completed and lessons are captured.
i.  **Obtain Final Acceptance:** Secure formal acceptance of all project deliverables from the client or sponsor.
ii. **Perform Administrative Closure:** Complete all contractual obligations, archive project documentation, and close out any procurement activities. PRINCE2 emphasizes "the final act of project closure" [Ref 9].
iii. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Facilitate a session to capture successes, challenges, and recommendations for future projects.
iv. **Release Team Resources:** Formally release project team members and acknowledge their contributions.
v. **Update Organizational Process Assets:** Incorporate new knowledge and lessons learned into organizational standards and templates.

### Roles

Key roles are essential for clear responsibilities and accountability within the project [Ref 5, Ref 6].

*   **Project Sponsor:** The person responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions [Ref 6]. Provides strategic direction and removes impediments.
*   **Project Manager (PM):** Coordinates activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives [Ref 6]. Responsible for overall planning, execution, monitoring, control, and closure.
*   **Marketing Team Lead:** Manages the day-to-day execution of marketing activities and oversees the marketing team members.
*   **Education Subject Matter Expert (SME):** Provides expertise on educational content, target audience, and institutional branding.
*   **Key Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with an interest in or impact on the project, including representatives from admissions, IT, faculty, and leadership [Ref 4, Ref 6].
*   **Project Team Members:** Individuals assigned to perform specific project tasks (e.g., content creators, graphic designers, digital marketing specialists).

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial control points where the project's viability and progress are formally reviewed and approved, ensuring alignment with organizational objectives and resource allocation [Ref 9].

1.  **Project Charter Approval:**
    *   **Timing:** End of Initiation Phase.
    *   **Purpose:** To gain formal authorization for the project and commit initial resources. The Project Sponsor reviews the charter to ensure project objectives align with strategic goals and initial risks are acceptable [Ref 6, Ref 11].
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go to Planning Phase.
    *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor [Ref 6].

2.  **Project Plan Approval:**
    *   **Timing:** End of Planning Phase.
    *   **Purpose:** To review and approve the detailed project management plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and risk responses, ensuring it is realistic and achievable for the project type and duration [Ref 3, Ref 15].
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go to Execution Phase.
    *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor and Key Stakeholders (e.g., Head of Marketing, Admissions Director).

3.  **Key Deliverable Acceptance:**
    *   **Timing:** Throughout Execution Phase, for major marketing deliverables (e.g., campaign creative, website content launch).
    *   **Purpose:** To formally accept individual key outputs, ensuring they meet quality standards and requirements [Ref 7].
    *   **Decision:** Accept/Revise/Reject Deliverable.
    *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor, Education SME, and relevant Key Stakeholders.

4.  **Project Closure Approval:**
    *   **Timing:** End of Closure Phase.
    *   **Purpose:** To verify that all project work has been completed, deliverables accepted, and administrative closure activities (e.g., lessons learned) performed, formally ending the project [Ref 9].
    *   **Decision:** Project Closed.
    *   **Approver:** Project Sponsor.
