### Phases and Activities

This project will follow a phased approach, aligned with typical project lifecycles [Ref 9].

I. **Initiation Phase:**
  i. Define project objectives and scope in collaboration with stakeholders [Ref 4].
  ii. Identify key stakeholders and their interests [Ref 2, Ref 4].
  iii. Develop a project charter outlining the project’s purpose, objectives, and high-level requirements.
  iv. Secure project sponsorship and resources [Ref 4].
  v. Conduct a preliminary risk assessment [Ref 5, Ref 14].

II. **Planning Phase:**
  i. Develop a detailed project management plan encompassing scope, schedule, cost, resources, communication, risk, and quality [Ref 6].
  ii. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable tasks [Ref 5].
  iii. Define project deliverables and acceptance criteria [Ref 12].
  iv. Estimate project costs and develop a budget [Ref 6].
  v. Develop a project schedule with clear milestones and dependencies [Ref 6].
  vi. Create a communication plan to ensure effective information dissemination to stakeholders [Ref 6].
  vii. Perform a thorough risk assessment and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 8].
  viii. Establish a change management process [Ref 6].

III. **Execution Phase:**
  i. Execute project tasks according to the project management plan.
  ii. Manage project resources effectively.
  iii. Monitor project progress and performance against the plan.
  iv. Implement quality assurance measures to ensure deliverables meet specified standards [Ref 8].
  v. Manage project risks and issues proactively.
  vi. Communicate project status to stakeholders regularly.
  vii. Facilitate team collaboration and knowledge sharing [Ref 8].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling Phase:**
  i. Track project progress against the schedule and budget.
  ii. Monitor project risks and issues, and implement corrective actions.
  iii. Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget using the change management process [Ref 6].
  iv. Report project performance to stakeholders regularly.
  v. Ensure alignment with project objectives [Ref 3].
  vi. Measuring project performance against defined metrics [Ref 2].

V. **Closure Phase:**
  i. Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
  ii. Conduct a post-project review to identify lessons learned.
  iii. Archive project documentation.
  iv. Release project resources.
  v. Communicate project closure to stakeholders.

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction, resources, and support [Ref 4].

*   **Research Team:** Conducts the research activities, collects data, and analyzes findings.

### Decision Gates

i. **End of Initiation:** Approval of Project Charter – Go/No-Go decision based on alignment with strategic objectives and feasibility.
ii. **End of Planning:** Approval of Project Management Plan – Go/No-Go decision based on the completeness, accuracy, and feasibility of the plan. It also makes sure there are realistic expectations [Ref 3].
iii. **End of Execution:** Review of Interim Deliverables - Assessment of whether deliverables meet acceptance criteria and are on track to meet project objectives.
iv. **End of Project:** Acceptance of Final Deliverables – Formal sign-off from stakeholders confirming that all deliverables meet requirements and the project is complete [Ref 12].
