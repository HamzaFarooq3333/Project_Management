#### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation Phase:**

i. **Project Charter Development:** Define the project's objectives, scope, and stakeholders. This includes identifying key stakeholders, their interests, and potential impact on the project [Ref 6].
ii. **Feasibility Study:** Evaluate the project's viability from technical, economic, and operational perspectives.
iii. **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify and analyze stakeholders to understand their needs and expectations [Ref 6].

II. **Planning Phase:**

i. **Project Management Plan Development:** Create a comprehensive plan that covers all aspects of the project, including scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, communication, risk, and procurement.
ii. **Scope Definition:** Define the project scope in detail, including deliverables, acceptance criteria, and exclusions. Develop a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
iii. **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic and detailed project schedule, including tasks, dependencies, and milestones [Ref 3].
iv. **Budget Development:** Estimate project costs and develop a budget that aligns with the project scope and schedule [Ref 3].
v. **Risk Management Planning:** Identify potential risks and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 15].
vi. **Communication Plan Development:** Define how project information will be communicated to stakeholders [Ref 3].

III. **Execution Phase:**

i. **Project Work Execution:** Carry out the activities defined in the project plan. This includes managing resources, coordinating tasks, and ensuring quality [Ref 2].
ii. **Quality Assurance:** Implement quality control measures to ensure that deliverables meet the defined quality standards [Ref 12].
iii. **Communication Management:** Regularly communicate project progress to stakeholders [Ref 3].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling Phase:**

i. **Performance Measurement:** Track project performance against the plan, including schedule, cost, and scope [Ref 2].
ii. **Change Management:** Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and budget in a controlled manner [Ref 3].
iii. **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies [Ref 15].
iv. **Issue Management:** Identify and resolve issues that arise during the project [Ref 8].

V. **Closure Phase:**

i. **Project Deliverable Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from the client.
ii. **Project Documentation:** Archive all project documentation, including plans, reports, and lessons learned.
iii. **Final Project Report:** Prepare a final report summarizing the project's outcomes, achievements, and lessons learned.
iv. **Stakeholder sign-off:** Obtain sign-off that confirms deliverables are met [Ref 9].
