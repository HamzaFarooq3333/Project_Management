#### 2. Phases and Activities

This project will follow a phased approach aligned with the project life cycle, as described by [Ref 12].

**I. Initiation Phase**

i.  **Project Definition & Scope:** Define project objectives, deliverables, and scope [Ref 2, Ref 3].
ii.  **Stakeholder Identification & Analysis:** Identify and analyze stakeholders to understand their interests and expectations [Ref 1, Ref 6].
iii. **Project Charter Development:** Develop the project charter outlining project goals, high-level requirements, and the initial project team.
iv. **Risk Identification:** Identify initial risks, threats, and opportunities.
v. **Governance Establishment:** Establish project governance structure including roles and responsibilities.

**II. Planning Phase**

i.  **Detailed Scope Definition:** Refine project scope including work breakdown structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
ii.  **Schedule Development:** Create a detailed project schedule, including task dependencies, durations, and resource allocation [Ref 3].
iii. **Budgeting & Cost Estimation:** Develop a comprehensive project budget [Ref 3].
iv.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and acquire necessary resources (personnel, equipment, software, etc.).
v.  **Communication Plan:** Develop a communication plan to manage stakeholder communication [Ref 3].
vi. **Procurement Plan (if applicable):** Develop a plan for any necessary procurement activities [Ref 3].
vii. **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a detailed risk management plan to address identified risks [Ref 10, Ref 19].
viii. **Change Management Plan:** Establish a change management process to control project changes [Ref 3].

**III. Execution Phase**

i.  **Project Work Execution:** Execute project tasks according to the project plan.
ii.  **Configuration and Implementation:** Perform the manual configuration activities.
iii. **Stakeholder Communication:** Manage and maintain continuous and effective communication with all stakeholders.
iv.  **Issue and Risk Management:** Monitor, track, and manage issues and risks.
v.  **Change Requests:** Manage and process change requests according to the change management plan.
vi. **Quality Assurance:** Ensure quality standards are met throughout the execution [Ref 8].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling Phase**

i.  **Progress Monitoring:** Track project progress against the plan using appropriate metrics.
ii.  **Performance Reporting:** Generate performance reports (status reports, dashboards).
iii. **Variance Analysis:** Analyze variances (schedule, budget, scope).
iv.  **Issue Management:** Manage and resolve project issues promptly.
v.  **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor and manage risks and implement mitigation strategies.
vi.  **Change Control:** Manage project changes and update the project plan as needed.

**V. Closing Phase**

i.  **Deliverable Verification:** Verify that all deliverables are complete and meet requirements.
ii.  **Project Acceptance:** Obtain formal project acceptance from stakeholders.
iii. **Final Documentation:** Complete all project documentation (lessons learned, final report).
iv.  **Financial Closure:** Close out all financial aspects of the project.
v.  **Resource Release:** Release project resources.
vi.  **Project Review & Evaluation:** Conduct a project review and evaluation.

#### 3. Roles

i.  **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for securing resources and providing executive direction [Ref 6].
ii.  **Project Manager:** Leads and directs the project, responsible for project success.
iii.  **Project Team:** Individuals responsible for executing project tasks.
iv.  **Stakeholders:** Individuals or organizations who can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by the project [Ref 1, Ref 6].
v.  **Business Analyst:** Responsible for gathering and documenting business requirements.
vi.  **Configuration Specialists:** Responsible for the technical configuration and implementation.
vii. **Quality Assurance Specialist:** Responsible for ensuring the quality of the deliverables.

#### 5. Decision Gates

i.  **Project Charter Approval:** Approved by the Project Sponsor (End of Initiation Phase).
ii.  **Project Plan Approval:** Approved by the Project Manager and Project Sponsor, confirming alignment with stakeholders (End of Planning Phase).
iii. **Go/No-Go Decision:** Review progress, risks, budget, and scope. Decision point at major milestones or upon significant change requests (Throughout Project Life Cycle).
iv.  **Deliverable Acceptance:** Formal acceptance of deliverables by stakeholders (End of Execution Phase and/or major milestones).
v.  **Project Closure Approval:** Final project closure approved by the Project Sponsor (End of Closing Phase).

These decision gates ensure that the project is on track and meets the requirements of stakeholders, and are a key aspect of project governance [Ref 12].
