#### 2. Phases and Activities

The project will follow a phased approach, incorporating key activities derived from project management best practices and relevant ISO standards.

I. **Initiation:**

   i.  Define Project Objectives and Scope: Clearly articulate the project goals, deliverables, and scope, referencing ISO 21500 which defines the project as a temporary endeavor [Ref 7].
   ii. Identify Stakeholders: Identify and document all stakeholders and their interests [Ref 3, Ref 4].
   iii. Develop Project Charter: Formalize project authorization, including roles, responsibilities, and high-level constraints.
   iv. Define High-Level Risks: Identify potential risks early on to enable proactive mitigation.
   v. Develop High Level Schedule and Budget: Provide preliminary estimates for the project duration and cost [Ref 2].

II. **Planning:**

   i.  Detailed Scope Definition: Refine and decompose the project scope into manageable work packages, referencing work breakdown structure principles [Ref 4].
   ii. Create Detailed Schedule: Develop a realistic schedule, accounting for dependencies and resource availability [Ref 2].
   iii. Develop Budget: Refine the budget based on detailed scope and schedule, considering resource costs and contingency. [Ref 2]
   iv. Resource Planning: Identify and secure necessary resources (personnel, tools, etc.).
   v. Risk Management Plan: Develop a comprehensive plan to identify, assess, and mitigate risks, including mitigation strategies.
   vi. Communication Plan: Establish a clear communication strategy for stakeholders, including frequency, methods, and responsibilities. [Ref 2]
   vii. Procurement Plan: If necessary, define the process for acquiring external goods or services.
   viii. Quality Management Plan: Define the processes to ensure the quality of project deliverables.

III. **Execution:**

   i.  Project Team Coordination: Manage the project team, facilitating collaboration, and addressing issues.
   ii. Task Execution: Carry out project activities according to the planned schedule and budget.
   iii. Communication and Reporting: Regularly communicate project status, issues, and risks to stakeholders. [Ref 2]
   iv. Change Management: Implement the change management process to control and manage scope changes. [Ref 2]
   v. Risk Response: Execute the planned risk response strategies.
   vi. Quality Assurance: Ensure project deliverables meet quality standards.

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:**

   i.  Progress Monitoring: Track project progress against the plan, using performance metrics.
   ii. Schedule Control: Manage schedule deviations and take corrective actions.
   iii. Cost Control: Monitor and control project costs, implementing cost-saving measures when necessary.
   iv. Quality Control: Verify deliverables and ensure adherence to quality standards.
   v. Risk Monitoring: Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation plans when needed.
   vi. Change Control: Process and manage change requests, assessing their impact on the project.

V. **Closure:**

   i.  Project Deliverables Acceptance: Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
   ii.  Final Documentation: Complete all project documentation.
   iii.  Lessons Learned: Conduct a lessons-learned session to identify best practices and areas for improvement.
   iv.  Project Closure: Formally close the project, ensuring all contracts are closed and resources are released.

#### 3. Roles

*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for overall project success, including planning, execution, monitoring, and closing the project [Ref 12].
*   **Business Analyst (BA):** Gathers and analyzes requirements, and creates requirements documentation.
*   **Software Developers:** Develop the software according to the specifications.
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Tester:** Tests the software to ensure it meets quality standards.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups affected by or having an interest in the project [Ref 3, Ref 4, Ref 7].
*   **Sponsor:** The person obtaining the resources and making executive decisions necessary for success [Ref 7].

#### 5. Decision Gates

Decision gates are crucial checkpoints to assess project progress and ensure alignment with objectives.

*   **Requirements Sign-off:** Before entering the Planning Phase. Verify that all requirements are fully documented and approved by the BA, Stakeholders, and Sponsor.
*   **Planning Phase Approval:** Before Execution. Ensure the scope, schedule, budget, risk, and communication plans are reviewed and approved [Ref 2]. This ensures that all project planning is complete.
*   **Execution Phase Review:** Mid-project check to evaluate progress and make adjustments. Assure all activities are being executed as planned.
*   **Delivery Phase Sign-off:** At the end of Execution and before closing. Verify that all deliverables have been delivered, are of acceptable quality, and have been accepted by the stakeholders [Ref 12].
