### Process Summary

This project recommendation outlines a PMBOK-aligned process for a small-scale healthcare infrastructure project. Given the project's size, short duration (< 6 months), and manual configuration nature, the process will emphasize streamlined planning, effective stakeholder engagement, and proactive risk management to ensure successful delivery. The PMBOK emphasizes several key areas, including planning, stakeholder management, team composition, and continuous measurement [Ref 1, Ref 2]. This process will integrate these principles, while tailoring them to the specific context of a small infrastructure project in a healthcare setting [Ref 4]. This project will be approached in a predictive manner but still have the ability to adapt to changes as needed [Ref 3].

### Phases and Activities

I. **Initiation**

   i. **Project Charter Development:** Define the project scope, objectives, and high-level requirements. Identify key stakeholders and obtain initial approvals. This is critical for getting started [Ref 7].
   ii. **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify all stakeholders (patients, healthcare professionals, IT staff, facility management, etc.) and analyze their interests and influence [Ref 5, Ref 13].
   iii. **High-Level Risk Assessment:** Conduct an initial risk assessment to identify potential threats and opportunities [Ref 10].
   iv. **Appointment of Project Manager and Core Team:** Identify and assign the project manager.

II. **Planning**

   i. **Detailed Scope Definition:** Break down the project into manageable work packages using a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 5].
   ii. **Schedule Development:** Create a realistic project schedule, considering dependencies, resource availability, and the project's short duration.
   iii. **Budget Planning:** Develop a detailed budget, accounting for all costs associated with the project.
   iv. **Resource Planning:** Identify and secure necessary resources, including personnel, equipment, and materials.
   v. **Communication Plan:** Develop a plan to manage all communications related to the project, taking into account stakeholder needs.
   vi. **Procurement Planning:** If applicable, plan any necessary procurement activities.
   vii. **Risk Management Plan:** Develop a detailed risk management plan to identify, analyze, and mitigate potential risks [Ref 10].
   viii. **Quality Planning:** Defining quality standards and how they will be measured.

III. **Execution**

   i. **Manual Configuration:** Carry out the infrastructure setup and configuration according to the detailed plans.
   ii. **Progress Tracking:** Monitor project progress against the schedule and budget.
   iii. **Issue Management:** Implement an issue management process to track and resolve problems.
   iv. **Risk Response:** Execute the risk response strategies outlined in the risk management plan [Ref 10].
   v. **Stakeholder Management:** Manage stakeholder expectations and communication, ensuring timely updates and feedback.

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**

   i. **Performance Measurement:** Track key project metrics to measure performance against the plan [Ref 1].
   ii. **Change Control:** Implement a change control process to manage any requested changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 1].
   iii. **Quality Control:** Conduct quality checks to ensure deliverables meet defined standards.
   iv. **Risk Monitoring:** Continuously monitor identified risks and potential new risks.

V. **Closure**

   i. **Project Deliverables Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables.
   ii. **Final Documentation:** Prepare and archive all project documentation.
   iii. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a lessons-learned session to identify what went well and areas for improvement.
   iv. **Project Closure:** Formally close out the project.

### Roles

*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for overall project planning, execution, and control.
*   **Sponsor:** Provides funding and high-level decision-making. [Ref 13]
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with an interest in the project. [Ref 5, Ref 13]
*   **Configuration Team:** Responsible for the actual configuration work.
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Team/Individual:** Responsible for ensuring the quality of the project deliverables.
*   **IT Support:** This team/individual will provide technical support as needed.
