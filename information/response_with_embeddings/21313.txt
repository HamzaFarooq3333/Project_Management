### Phases and Activities

The project will adopt a phased, predictive lifecycle with potential for iterative or incremental delivery within specific development stages, aligning with ISO 21502's flexibility in delivery approaches [Ref 1]. Each phase concludes with a decision gate to ensure progress and alignment with project objectives and governance [Ref 12].

#### I. Project Initiation
This phase establishes the project's foundation, ensuring alignment with organizational strategy and obtaining formal authorization.
i.  **Define Project Objectives and Scope at a High Level:** Identify the "defined objectives" the project aims to achieve [Ref 7].
ii. **Identify and Analyze Key Stakeholders:** Understand who "has interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of a project" [Ref 7, Ref 3]. Develop a stakeholder engagement plan [Ref 14].
iii. **Establish Project Governance Structure:** Define the "principles, policies and procedures by which a project is authorized and directed to accomplish agreed objectives" [Ref 12]. This includes defining the roles of the Project Sponsor and a Steering Committee.
iv. **Appoint Project Manager and Core Team:** Assign the Project Manager, who will be responsible for "coordinated activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives" [Ref 7].
v.  **Develop Project Charter:** Document the project's purpose, high-level objectives, initial scope, key deliverables (unique and verifiable elements) [Ref 15], success criteria, and assign the Project Sponsor [Ref 7].
vi. **Conduct Initial Risk Identification and Assessment:** Identify potential "threat risk occurrence that would have a negative impact" [Ref 3, Ref 8].

#### II. Project Planning
This phase involves developing a comprehensive plan that guides all subsequent project activities, as emphasized in PMBOK's planning process [Ref 2] and ISO 21502's core practice of "plan" [Ref 11].
i.  **Refine Product and Project Scope:** Clearly define "product scope" and "project scope" to avoid ambiguity and conflict [Ref 2, Ref 4].
ii. **Create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Decompose the defined scope into "progressively lower levels consisting of elements of work" and "work packages" [Ref 3].
iii. **Estimate Effort, Duration, and Resources:** Conduct detailed "estimating" for tasks and activities, including human and material "resources used" [Ref 2, Ref 11].
iv. **Develop Project Schedule:** Create a comprehensive "scheduling" plan, detailing timelines and dependencies [Ref 2].
v.  **Establish Project Budget:** Prepare a detailed "budget" outlining all costs associated with project activities [Ref 2].
vi. **Plan for Team Composition and Organization:** Define roles and responsibilities, aligning with "team composition" [Ref 2].
vii. **Develop Communication Plan:** Outline how information will be shared and "communication plan" will be managed with stakeholders [Ref 2].
viii. **Develop Risk Management Plan:** Detail processes for identifying, analyzing, planning responses to, and monitoring project risks, optimizing "risk responses" [Ref 14].
ix. **Develop Quality Management Plan:** Incorporate "build quality into processes and deliverables" [Ref 14] by defining quality standards, metrics, and assurance activities.
x.  **Develop Change Management Plan:** Define the process for requesting, evaluating, and approving changes to the project baseline, ensuring effective "change management" [Ref 2].
xi. **Plan for Procurement (if applicable):** Outline external acquisitions [Ref 2].
xii. **Baseline Project Plan:** Obtain formal approval for the comprehensive project plan, including scope, schedule, and budget baselines.

#### III. Project Execution & Monitoring
This phase involves carrying out the planned work and closely observing project progress to identify variances, aligning with "project work" and "measurement" [Ref 5] and ISO 21502's practices of "direct" and "monitor" [Ref 11].
i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute the activities defined in the WBS, performing "project work" [Ref 5] and managing the "resources assigned to the project" [Ref 11].
ii. **Manage Project Team:** Lead, motivate, and develop the project team [Ref 11], fostering a "collaborative project team environment" [Ref 14].
iii. **Implement Communication Plan:** Distribute project information to stakeholders as per the plan, ensuring effective "engagement with stakeholders" [Ref 14].
iv. **Conduct Quality Assurance:** Perform planned quality audits and reviews to ensure processes and deliverables meet standards [Ref 14].
v.  **Develop Deliverables:** Produce the "unique and verifiable element that is required to be produced by a project" [Ref 15].
vi. **Monitor Project Progress:** Track actual progress against the baseline schedule and budget. Use "measurement" techniques [Ref 2, Ref 5].

#### IV. Project Control
This phase focuses on managing changes, risks, issues, and ensuring the project stays on track to meet its objectives, incorporating ISO 21502's practice to "control" [Ref 11].
i.  **Perform Integrated Change Control:** Review and manage all change requests, assessing their impact on scope, schedule, budget, and quality [Ref 2].
ii. **Control Scope:** Monitor the status of the project and product scope, managing changes to the scope baseline.
iii. **Control Schedule and Cost:** Monitor and control project schedule and costs, taking corrective actions as needed.
iv. **Control Quality:** Monitor specific project results to determine if they comply with relevant quality standards and identify ways to eliminate causes of unsatisfactory performance.
v.  **Manage Issues:** Address "event that arises during a project or phase" [Ref 15], ensuring timely resolution of roadblocks.
vi. **Control Risks:** Implement risk response plans and monitor residual risks, optimizing "risk responses" [Ref 14].
vii. **Manage Stakeholder Expectations:** Continually "effectively engage with stakeholders" [Ref 14] and manage their expectations regarding "what the project will deliver, costs, and when" [Ref 4].

#### V. Project Closure
This final phase involves formalizing acceptance of the project's product or service and bringing the project to an orderly end. ISO 21502 includes "close the project" as a key practice [Ref 11], and PRINCE2 outlines "the final act of project closure" [Ref 6].
i.  **Obtain Final Deliverable Acceptance:** Secure formal acceptance from the customer or sponsor for the final "deliverable" [Ref 15].
ii. **Perform Contract Closure:** Complete and settle all open contracts.
iii. **Release Project Resources:** Formally release project team members and other resources.
iv. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
v.  **Close Project Administratively:** Archive all project documentation and formally close the project account.
vi. **Measure Project Performance and Value:** Assess the project's overall "delivery" and "measurement" against its objectives and value proposition [Ref 5, Ref 14].

### Roles

Key roles are essential for defining responsibilities and ensuring accountability across the project lifecycle.
*   **Project Sponsor:** The "person responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions to enable success" [Ref 7]. Provides ultimate authorization and governance [Ref 12].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for "project management," which involves "coordinated activities to direct and control the accomplishment of agreed objectives" [Ref 7]. This includes integrating all practices (initiate, plan, monitor, control, close) and motivating the team [Ref 11, Ref 13].
*   **Steering Committee/Governance Board:** Provides overall project governance and strategic direction [Ref 12].
*   **Product Owner/Business Analyst:** Represents stakeholder interests, defines product requirements, and prioritizes backlog.
*   **Technical Lead/Architect:** Provides technical leadership, design oversight, and ensures adherence to architectural standards.
*   **Development Team:** Responsible for performing the "project work" [Ref 5], developing and testing software components, and creating "deliverables" [Ref 15].
*   **Quality Assurance (QA) Lead/Tester:** Ensures quality is "built into processes and deliverables" [Ref 14] through testing and quality control activities.
*   **Stakeholders:** Any "person, group or organization that has interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of a project" [Ref 7, Ref 3]. This includes end-users, customers, and functional managers.

### Decision Gates

Decision gates, also known as phase gates, kill points, or stage gates, are critical points in the project lifecycle where a formal review is conducted to assess the project's performance, validate its continued viability, and make a go/no-go decision for the next phase [Ref 6, Ref 12].

1.  **Project Charter Approval (End of Project Initiation):**
    *   **Purpose:** To formally authorize the project's existence and commit resources.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for detailed planning.
    *   **Reviewers:** Project Sponsor, Steering Committee/Governance Board [Ref 12].
    *   **Criteria:** Clear objectives, high-level scope, identified sponsor, initial business case viability.

2.  **Project Plan Baseline Approval (End of Project Planning):**
    *   **Purpose:** To approve the detailed project plan (scope, schedule, budget, risks, etc.) as the baseline for execution.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go for project execution.
    *   **Reviewers:** Project Sponsor, Steering Committee/Governance Board, key functional managers.
    *   **Criteria:** Approved WBS, realistic estimates and schedule, adequate budget, comprehensive risk management plan, clear "change management" process [Ref 2].

3.  **Phase Completion Reviews (Throughout Execution, e.g., End of Design, End of Development, End of Testing):**
    *   **Purpose:** To review the completion of significant project stages or iterations, ensuring quality and adherence to requirements. PRINCE2 emphasizes "stages of project delivery" [Ref 6].
    *   **Decision:** Proceed to the next development phase or iteration / Rework required.
    *   **Reviewers:** Project Manager, Product Owner, Technical Lead, QA Lead, relevant Stakeholders.
    *   **Criteria:** Deliverables accepted, quality standards met, outstanding issues addressed, updated risk assessment, readiness for next phase.

4.  **Go/No-Go for Deployment (Pre-Production Release):**
    *   **Purpose:** To assess the readiness of the software for production deployment.
    *   **Decision:** Authorize deployment / Delay deployment for further work.
    *   **Reviewers:** Project Sponsor, Steering Committee, Project Manager, Technical Lead, QA Lead, Operations Lead.
    *   **Criteria:** Successful user acceptance testing (UAT), security audits passed, performance benchmarks met, operational readiness, comprehensive deployment plan.

5.  **Final Project Acceptance (End of Project Closure):**
    *   **Purpose:** To obtain formal acceptance that the project has met its objectives and to close the project administratively [Ref 11].
    *   **Decision:** Project closed / Outstanding items require further action.
    *   **Reviewers:** Project Sponsor [Ref 7], Key Stakeholders.
    *   **Criteria:** All deliverables accepted, lessons learned documented, contracts closed, resources released, final financial reconciliation, project performance "measurement" [Ref 5] reviewed.
