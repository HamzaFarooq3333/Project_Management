### Phases and Activities

#### I. Initiation Phase
This phase establishes the project's foundation, ensuring alignment with organizational strategy and defining its initial scope and objectives.
i.  **Define Project Objectives and Business Case**: Clearly articulate the project's purpose, expected benefits, and strategic alignment with the healthcare organization's vision [Ref 14]. This includes establishing the project's value proposition [Ref 12].
ii. **Identify and Analyze Stakeholders**: Thoroughly identify all persons or organizations that could be affected by or perceive themselves to be affected by the project, including regulatory bodies, medical staff, patients, and local community [Ref 2], [Ref 11].
iii. **Develop Project Charter**: Document the project's authorization, high-level scope, objectives, and assignment of the Project Manager. This also includes defining high-level product and project scope [Ref 1].
iv. **Establish Project Governance Structure**: Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project will be authorized and directed to accomplish agreed objectives [Ref 9]. This includes defining roles and responsibilities at a high level.

#### II. Planning Phase
This phase involves developing a comprehensive project management plan that guides all subsequent project activities.
i.  **Develop Detailed Scope Definition and WBS**: Decompose the defined project scope into progressively lower levels of work, culminating in a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and work packages [Ref 4]. This includes both product and project scope [Ref 1].
ii. **Estimate and Schedule Activities**: Develop detailed estimates for activity durations, resources, and costs, leading to a comprehensive project schedule and budget [Ref 1].
iii. **Plan Resources and Team Composition**: Determine the necessary human, equipment, material, and facility resources, and plan for their acquisition and management. Define the project team composition [Ref 1].
iv. **Develop Risk Management Plan**: Identify potential risks (threats and opportunities), analyze their likelihood and impact, and plan appropriate risk responses [Ref 10], [Ref 13]. Risks are addressed continually throughout the project [Ref 10].
v.  **Develop Communication Plan**: Establish how, when, and by whom project information will be communicated to stakeholders [Ref 1].
vi. **Plan Procurement and Contract Management**: Determine which project needs will be met by external parties and plan the procurement processes [Ref 1].
vii. **Establish Change Management Process**: Define the process for requesting, evaluating, approving, or rejecting changes to project baselines [Ref 1].
viii. **Establish Quality Management Plan**: Define the quality standards applicable to the project and how they will be achieved and verified, ensuring quality is built into processes and deliverables [Ref 6].

#### III. Execution Phase
This phase involves carrying out the planned work to achieve project objectives.
i.  **Direct and Manage Project Work**: Perform the activities defined in the project management plan, including managing the construction operations [Ref 2].
ii. **Acquire, Develop, and Manage Project Team**: Build a collaborative project team environment [Ref 6] and ensure team members are effectively utilized.
iii. **Manage Communications**: Implement the communication plan, ensuring effective engagement with stakeholders [Ref 6].
iv. **Conduct Procurements**: Manage contractor performance and ensure compliance with contractual agreements.
v.  **Implement Risk Responses**: Execute planned strategies to address identified risks and exploit opportunities [Ref 10].

#### IV. Monitoring & Controlling Phase
This phase runs concurrently with execution, tracking performance against the plan and taking corrective action as needed.
i.  **Monitor Project Work and Performance**: Track, review, and report project progress, identifying deviations from the plan [Ref 2]. This includes measurement of project performance [Ref 1].
ii. **Control Scope, Schedule, and Costs**: Manage changes to the project baselines using the established change management process [Ref 1].
iii. **Monitor and Control Risks**: Continuously monitor residual risks, identify new risks, and evaluate the effectiveness of risk responses [Ref 10].
iv. **Perform Quality Assurance and Control**: Monitor specific project results to determine if they comply with relevant quality standards and identify ways to eliminate causes of unsatisfactory performance [Ref 6].
v.  **Manage Stakeholder Engagement**: Continually assess stakeholder satisfaction and adjust engagement strategies as needed [Ref 6].

#### V. Closure Phase
This phase formally concludes all project activities, ensures final acceptance, and transfers deliverables.
i.  **Close Project or Phase**: Finalize all activities across all process groups to formally complete the project or a project phase [Ref 7].
ii. **Obtain Final Acceptance**: Secure formal acceptance of the final deliverables from the client or sponsor.
iii. **Complete Contractual Obligations**: Close out all procurement contracts.
iv. **Conduct Lessons Learned**: Document successes, failures, and recommendations for future projects.
v.  **Release Resources**: Formally release project team members and other resources.
vi. **Archive Project Documentation**: Compile and archive all relevant project documentation for future reference and compliance.

### Roles

*   **Project Sponsor**: Provides resources, executive decisions, and overall project governance [Ref 11]. Responsible for obtaining resources and executive decisions to enable success.
*   **Project Manager**: Accountable for project planning, execution, monitoring, control, and closure, ensuring objectives are met [Ref 11]. Leads the collaborative project team environment [Ref 6].
*   **Construction Manager**: Oversees all on-site construction activities, safety, quality, and coordination of subcontractors.
*   **Quality Manager**: Ensures all project deliverables and processes adhere to established quality standards and regulatory requirements specific to healthcare construction [Ref 6].
*   **Risk Manager**: Manages the risk identification, analysis, planning of responses, and monitoring throughout the project lifecycle [Ref 10].
*   **Procurement Manager**: Manages all procurement processes, from vendor selection to contract administration.
*   **Key Stakeholders (e.g., Healthcare Administration, Regulatory Bodies, End-Users)**: Provide requirements, approvals, and insights, and are effectively engaged throughout the project [Ref 6], [Ref 11].
*   **Project Team Members**: Execute project tasks, contributing their expertise in various domains (e.g., engineering, architecture, IT, clinical).

### Decision Gates

These critical decision points ensure formal authorization and review at key transitions, aligning with project governance [Ref 9] and lifecycle management [Ref 7].

i.  **Gate 1: Project Authorization (End of Initiation)**
    *   **Purpose**: To obtain formal approval to proceed with detailed planning.
    *   **Input**: Approved Project Charter, high-level business case, initial stakeholder analysis.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor, Steering Committee.
    *   **Outcome**: Project is formally authorized to enter the planning phase [Ref 9].

ii. **Gate 2: Plan Approval (End of Planning)**
    *   **Purpose**: To secure approval for the comprehensive Project Management Plan and its baselines.
    *   **Input**: Detailed Project Management Plan (including WBS, scope, schedule, budget, risk, quality, communication, and procurement plans) [Ref 1].
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor, Key Stakeholders.
    *   **Outcome**: Baselines are approved, and the project is authorized to commence execution.

iii. **Gate 3: Mid-Project Performance Review (Mid-Execution)**
    *   **Purpose**: To assess overall project health, performance against baselines, and remaining viability.
    *   **Input**: Performance reports, updated risk register, change logs, forecast to complete [Ref 2].
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor, Steering Committee, Project Manager.
    *   **Outcome**: Confirmation to continue as planned, approval for significant adjustments, or, in extreme cases, project re-evaluation.

iv. **Gate 4: Pre-Commissioning & Handover Review (End of Execution)**
    *   **Purpose**: To verify that construction is complete, meets specifications, and is ready for commissioning and operational handover.
    *   **Input**: Quality assurance reports, completed defect lists, safety certifications, commissioning plans, initial operational readiness assessments.
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor, Healthcare Administration, Regulatory Bodies.
    *   **Outcome**: Approval to proceed with commissioning, testing, and formal operational handover.

v.  **Gate 5: Project Closure (End of Closure Phase)**
    *   **Purpose**: To formally confirm project completion, obtain final acceptance, and release all project obligations.
    *   **Input**: Final acceptance documentation, lessons learned report, contract closure reports [Ref 7].
    *   **Decision**: Project Sponsor.
    *   **Outcome**: Formal project closure, release of resources, and archiving of project documentation.
