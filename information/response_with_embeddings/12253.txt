ii. **Planning Phase:**
    a. Detailed Scope Definition: Creating a detailed project scope statement, including a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the scope [Ref 3, 4].
    b. Schedule Development: Creating a detailed project schedule with task durations, dependencies, and critical path analysis. This helps with project timing and deliverables [Ref 3].
    c. Budget Development: Creating a detailed budget, including cost estimates for all activities [Ref 3].
    d. Resource Planning: Identifying and allocating resources (labor, materials, equipment).
    e. Quality Planning: Defining quality standards and processes. This is an important consideration during planning [Ref 8].
    f. Communication Plan Development: Defining communication protocols and frequency.
    g. Risk Management Planning: Developing a detailed risk management plan, including risk identification, assessment, and mitigation strategies [Ref 15].
    h. Procurement Planning: Identifying procurement needs and developing a procurement plan [Ref 3].
    i. Team Composition: Defining team roles, responsibilities, and reporting structures [Ref 3].

iii. **Execution Phase:**
    a. Task Execution: Performing the work defined in the project plan.
    b. Resource Management: Managing and monitoring resource utilization.
    c. Quality Assurance: Implementing quality control processes.
    d. Communication Management: Executing the communication plan.
    e. Change Management: Implementing the change management process to handle changes to scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].
    f. Stakeholder Management: Engaging stakeholders and managing their expectations.

iv. **Monitoring and Controlling Phase:**
    a. Progress Monitoring: Tracking project progress against the plan.
    b. Schedule Control: Managing and controlling the project schedule.
    c. Cost Control: Managing and controlling project costs.
    d. Quality Control: Monitoring and controlling quality.
    e. Risk Monitoring and Control: Monitoring risks and implementing risk mitigation plans.
    f. Issue Management: Addressing and resolving issues.
    g. Performance Measurement: Measuring project performance against key performance indicators (KPIs) [Ref 1].

v. **Closing Phase:**
    a. Project Closure: Formalizing project completion.
    b. Contract Closure: Closing out contracts with vendors and suppliers.
    c. Lessons Learned: Conducting a lessons-learned session to capture project successes and challenges.
    d. Final Documentation: Archiving project documentation.

#### V. Decision Gates

i. **Project Charter Approval:** At the end of the initiation phase; Signifies formal project authorization and commitment [Ref 7].
ii. **Project Plan Approval:** At the end of the planning phase; Signifies approval of the scope, schedule, budget, and other plans [Ref 3].
iii. **Phase Gate Reviews:** Periodic reviews at the end of each major phase (e.g., design, foundation, structure) to ensure deliverables are met.
