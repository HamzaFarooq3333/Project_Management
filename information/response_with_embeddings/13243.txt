#### 2. Phases and Activities

I. **Project Initiation**

    i. Define Project Scope: Clearly articulate the project's objectives, deliverables, and scope [Ref 1, Ref 3].
    ii. Identify Stakeholders: Identify and analyze all stakeholders and their expectations [Ref 5, Ref 7]. Effective stakeholder engagement is key [Ref 9].
    iii. Develop Project Charter: Formalize project authorization, objectives, and high-level requirements [Ref 10].
    iv. Identify high level risks and initial planning: Assess risks and make initial estimations [Ref 11].
    v. Establish Governance: Define project governance structures, roles, and responsibilities [Ref 10].
    vi. Obtain Sponsor Approval: Secure approval from the project sponsor and other key stakeholders. The sponsor obtains the resources [Ref 7].

II. **Planning**

    i. Develop a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): Decompose the project scope into manageable work packages [Ref 5].
    ii. Define Activities: Identify specific tasks required to complete each work package.
    iii. Estimate Resources: Determine the resources (people, equipment, budget) needed for each activity.
    iv. Develop the Project Schedule: Create a detailed schedule with task dependencies, durations, and milestones [Ref 3].
    v. Develop a Budget: Create a budget based on the estimated costs for each activity [Ref 3].
    vi. Develop a Communication Plan: Plan how information will be disseminated to stakeholders [Ref 3].
    vii. Develop a Risk Management Plan: Identify, analyze, and plan responses to potential risks [Ref 11].
    viii. Create a Change Management Plan: Establish a process for managing changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].

III. **Execution**

    i. Execute Project Activities: Perform the work defined in the WBS according to the project plan [Ref 2].
    ii. Manage Resources: Allocate and manage project resources effectively.
    iii. Manage Stakeholder Engagement: Maintain active and effective communication with stakeholders [Ref 9].
    iv. Perform Quality Assurance: Ensure deliverables meet quality standards and are compliant with ISO standards.
    v. Conduct Team Meetings: Hold regular meetings to track progress, address issues, and facilitate collaboration.
    vi. Manage Changes: Implement the change management process.

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling**

    i. Track Project Progress: Monitor project performance against the baseline schedule and budget.
    ii. Manage Schedule: Monitor the schedule and make any corrections.
    iii. Monitor Budget: Track project spending against the budget and manage any variances [Ref 3].
    iv. Monitor Risks and Issues: Track identified risks and issues, implement mitigation plans, and escalate as needed.
    v. Manage Project Changes: Implement the change management process to manage change requests.
    vi. Conduct Performance Reviews: Conduct periodic performance reviews.
    vii. Report Project Status: Prepare regular status reports for stakeholders.

V. **Closure**

    i. Obtain Final Deliverable Acceptance: Ensure that stakeholders formally accept the project deliverables.
    ii. Conduct Project Review: Review the project's performance, document lessons learned, and identify areas for improvement.
    iii. Archive Project Documents: Store all project documentation for future reference.
    iv. Close Contracts: Finalize all vendor contracts and payments.
    v. Release Resources: Reassign project team members and other resources.

#### 3. Roles

*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for overall project planning, execution, monitoring, controlling, and closure. Oversees all aspects of the project.
*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides project funding and high-level guidance, and approves key decisions [Ref 7].
*   **Team Members:** Carry out project tasks and deliverables.
*   **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project. They may be affected by the project, or they may affect it [Ref 5, Ref 7].
*   **Quality Assurance Lead:** Ensures compliance with ISO standards and quality requirements.

*   **R** = Responsible: The person who does the work.

#### 5. Decision Gates

*   **Project Charter Approval:** (End of Phase I) Formal approval by the sponsor and key stakeholders to proceed with the project.
*   **Project Plan Approval:** (End of Phase II) Approval of the detailed project plan (scope, schedule, budget, etc.).
*   **Deliverable Acceptance:** (At completion of key deliverables and end of Phase V) Formal acceptance of the deliverables by stakeholders, confirming that they meet the defined requirements [Ref 13].
*   **Change Requests Approval:** (Throughout the project) Any changes to the scope or deliverables need formal approval.
*   **Project Closure Approval:** (End of Phase V) Formal approval to close the project.
