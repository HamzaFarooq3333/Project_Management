### Process Recommendation: Small-Scale Education Research Project (PRINCE2)

#### Process Summary

This process recommendation outlines a streamlined approach for managing a small-scale (< 6 months) education research project using PRINCE2 methodology.  It emphasizes adaptability and control, essential for a project of this size [Ref 1, Ref 8].  The focus will be on clear objectives, well-defined deliverables, and proactive stakeholder engagement. The project will leverage PRINCE2's principles, practices, and processes, ensuring the project aligns with the project context [Ref 9].  PRINCE2 provides a framework to meet the project's objectives despite potential challenges and ambiguity [Ref 3]. This framework allows for a structured approach that encompasses the entire project lifecycle [Ref 9]. The process aims to provide a temporary endeavor [Ref 6] to achieve a defined objective [Ref 6].

-----

#### Phases and Activities

The project will follow a phased approach, aligned with PRINCE2, adapted for the project's size.

I. **Project Initiation & Definition**

   i. **Define the Project Mandate:** Identify the project's purpose and justification.  Clarify the project objectives and expected outcomes.
   ii. **Project Initiation Document (PID) Development:** Create the PID, which will contain the project scope, objectives, approach, and constraints [Ref 5]. This ensures a shared understanding of project deliverables [Ref 12].
   iii. **Stakeholder Analysis:** Identify and analyze all stakeholders [Ref 2, Ref 6, Ref 18].
   iv. **Risk Assessment:** Perform an initial risk assessment to identify potential threats [Ref 4, Ref 11].
   v. **Planning:** Develop the scope and project plan, including the project scope, estimating, scheduling, budget, team composition, communication plan, procurement, and change management [Ref 5].

II. **Project Execution & Control**

   i. **Team Management:** Manage project team activities to adhere to the project plan.
   ii. **Progress Monitoring:** Track project progress against the plan using measurements and reports [Ref 2, Ref 5].
   iii. **Issue Management:** Identify and resolve project issues [Ref 12].
   iv. **Risk Management:** Monitor and manage identified risks. Implement contingency plans if necessary.
   v. **Quality Control:** Ensure the quality of the research output through regular reviews.
   vi. **Change Control:** Manage any changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 5].
   vii. **Stakeholder Communication:** Maintain regular communication with stakeholders [Ref 8].

III. **Project Closure**

   i. **Final Deliverables Review:** Ensure all deliverables are completed and meet the project's objectives.
   ii. **Final Project Report:** Compile a final project report summarizing the project's achievements, challenges, and lessons learned.
   iii. **Lessons Learned:** Conduct a post-project review to capture lessons learned for future projects.
   iv. **Project Closure Documentation:** Ensure all project documentation is complete and archived.

-----

#### Roles

i. **Project Sponsor:** Responsible for obtaining the resources and executive decisions [Ref 6] necessary for the project's success.
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for day-to-day management of the project, including planning, execution, and control.
iii. **Project Team Members:** Responsible for carrying out the research tasks and producing the project deliverables.
iv. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with an interest in the project.
