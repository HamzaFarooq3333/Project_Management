#### II. Phases and Activities

The project will be executed in the following phases, each comprising specific activities. The lifecycle will be defined by phases from start to end of project [Ref 9]:

**I. Initiation**

i.  **Project Charter Development:** Define project objectives, scope, and high-level requirements.  Identify the project sponsor [Ref 8] and key stakeholders [Ref 1].
ii.  **Stakeholder Identification and Analysis:** Identify and analyze all stakeholders [Ref 1, Ref 8], documenting their interests and expectations.
iii. **High-Level Risk Assessment:** Conduct an initial risk assessment to identify potential threats and opportunities [Ref 13, Ref 14].
iv.  **Develop Project Management Plan:** Create the initial project management plan outlining the project's approach, scope, and cost management. This should include detailed plans for scope, schedule, budget, team composition, communication, procurement, and change management [Ref 2].
v.  **Define Deliverables:** Identify the unique and verifiable deliverables of the project [Ref 6].

**II. Planning**

i.  **Detailed Scope Definition:** Refine the project scope and develop a detailed Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) [Ref 4].
ii.  **Schedule Development:** Create a detailed project schedule, estimating task durations and dependencies.
iii. **Budget Development:** Establish the project budget and cost baseline.
iv.  **Resource Planning:** Identify and secure necessary resources, including personnel, equipment, and materials.
v.  **Risk Management Planning:** Develop a comprehensive risk management plan, including risk identification, analysis, response planning, and monitoring [Ref 13].
vi.  **Communication Planning:** Develop a communication plan to manage stakeholder expectations and facilitate information flow [Ref 2].
vii. **Procurement Planning:** Establish a procurement plan to secure necessary goods and services [Ref 2].
viii. **Quality Planning:** Define quality standards and processes.
ix.  **Change Management Planning:** Define the change management process [Ref 2].

**III. Execution**

i.  **Project Team Management:** Manage and motivate the project team, ensuring they have the necessary resources and support.
ii.  **Work Execution:** Perform the planned work activities, adhering to the project schedule and budget.
iii.  **Stakeholder Management:** Manage stakeholder expectations and address any concerns or issues.
iv.  **Communication Management:** Distribute project information according to the communication plan.
v.  **Risk Response Implementation:** Implement the planned risk responses and monitor the effectiveness of these responses [Ref 13].
vi.  **Quality Assurance:** Conduct quality assurance activities to ensure deliverables meet defined standards.
vii. **Procurement Management:** Manage procurement activities according to the procurement plan.
viii. **Change Management:** Implement the change management process and manage approved changes [Ref 2].

**IV. Monitoring and Controlling**

i.  **Performance Measurement:** Track project performance against the baseline plans, measuring schedule, cost, scope, and quality.
ii.  **Variance Analysis:** Analyze variances and take corrective actions as needed.
iii.  **Risk Monitoring and Control:** Monitor identified risks, identify new risks, and implement risk responses.
iv.  **Issue Management:** Identify and resolve issues that arise during the project [Ref 6].
v.  **Change Control:** Manage change requests, assess their impact, and implement approved changes.
vi.  **Reporting:** Provide regular status reports to stakeholders.

**V. Closure**

i.  **Project Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables from the client.
ii.  **Contract Closure:** Close out all contracts with vendors and suppliers.
iii.  **Final Reporting:** Prepare a final project report summarizing project performance and lessons learned.
iv.  **Project Documentation:** Archive all project documentation.
v.  **Team Dissolution:** Release the project team members.

#### III. Roles

Key project roles include:

i.  **Project Sponsor [Ref 8, Ref 17]:** Responsible for obtaining resources and making executive decisions.
ii.  **Project Manager:** Leads and manages the project, ensuring its successful completion.
iii. **Stakeholders [Ref 1, Ref 8]:** Individuals or groups affected by the project.
iv.  **Project Team:** Performs project work.
v.  **Quality Assurance Manager:** Oversees quality control processes.
vi.  **Risk Manager:** Manages the identification, assessment, and mitigation of risks.
vii. **Change Control Board:** Reviews and approves change requests.

#### V. Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical points in the project where decisions are made to proceed or make adjustments.

i.  **Project Charter Approval:** (Initiation Phase) Approval of the project charter by the sponsor signifies formal project authorization. [Ref 8]
ii.  **Project Plan Approval:** (Planning Phase) Approval of the detailed project plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and risk management plans, by the project sponsor and key stakeholders.
iii. **Phase Gate Reviews:** At the end of each phase (Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring & Controlling, Closure), a review is conducted to ensure the project remains on track, deliverables are met, and any necessary adjustments are made. [Ref 9]
iv.  **Change Request Approval:** (Throughout the Project) All change requests are reviewed and approved by the Change Control Board. [Ref 2]
v.  **Final Acceptance:** (Closure Phase) Formal acceptance of the completed project and deliverables by the client/sponsor.
