### Marketing Project Process Recommendation (PMBOK Aligned)

**Process Summary:**

This process recommendation outlines a structured approach to managing a large-scale (> 18 months) marketing project in the finance industry, aligned with the PMBOK Guide and relevant ISO standards [Ref 1]. The process encompasses initiation, planning, execution, monitoring and controlling, and closure. Given the project's size and industry, a strong emphasis is placed on stakeholder engagement [Ref 3, Ref 6, Ref 11], risk management, and change control [Ref 2]. The process also highlights the importance of tailoring the PMBOK guide to the specific context of the project [Ref 11]. This includes factors such as organisational maturity, technology and culture [Ref 14].

-----

**Phases and Activities:**

I. **Initiation:**
 i. Define project objectives and scope, aligning with business strategy.
 ii. Identify key stakeholders [Ref 3, Ref 6, Ref 11].
 iii. Develop a project charter and secure approval from the project sponsor.
 iv. Conduct a preliminary risk assessment.

II. **Planning:**
 i. Develop a detailed project management plan, encompassing scope, schedule, budget, resources, communication, risk, and quality [Ref 2].
 ii. Create a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to decompose the project scope into manageable tasks [Ref 4].
 iii. Define project performance baselines for scope, schedule, and cost.
 iv. Develop a stakeholder engagement plan.
 v. Develop a comprehensive risk management plan including identification, analysis and response planning [Ref 11].
 vi. Create a procurement plan.

III. **Execution:**
 i. Execute the project plan, managing tasks and resources.
 ii. Manage stakeholder engagement.
 iii. Implement the communication plan.
 iv. Manage project risks and issues.
 v. Perform quality assurance activities.
 vi. Track and report project progress.
 vii. Ensure team collaboration and knowledge sharing [Ref 11].

IV. **Monitoring and Controlling:**
 i. Monitor project performance against baselines.
 ii. Manage changes to the project scope, schedule, and cost.
 iii. Control risks and issues.
 iv. Track and manage project deliverables.
 v. Report project status to stakeholders.
 vi. Conduct regular project reviews and audits.
 vii. Ensure compliance with organizational policies and procedures.

V. **Closure:**
 i. Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from stakeholders.
 ii. Conduct a project closeout meeting.
 iii. Document lessons learned.
 iv. Archive project documentation.
 v. Release project resources.
 vi. Celebrate project success.

-----

**Roles:**

*   **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support, secures resources [Ref 6].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and closing the project.
*   **Marketing Team Lead:** Leads the marketing team and ensures deliverables meet quality standards.
*   **Finance Representative:** Provides financial guidance and ensures budget adherence.
*   **Stakeholder Representative:** Represents the interests of key stakeholders and provides feedback.

**Decision Gates:**

i. **Project Initiation Approval:** Following the creation of the project charter, a formal approval is required from the project sponsor to proceed to the planning phase.
ii. **Project Management Plan Approval:** The completed project management plan requires formal approval from all key stakeholders before execution begins.
iii. **Phase-End Review:** At the end of the execution phase, a review is conducted to assess progress against the project plan and determine whether to proceed to project closure.
