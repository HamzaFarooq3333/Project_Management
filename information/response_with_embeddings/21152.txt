### Phases and Activities

The project lifecycle will follow a streamlined PRINCE2 process, adapted for a small software development project, ensuring that key management activities are performed across all project stages [Ref 9].

#### I. Pre-Project (Starting Up a Project)
This phase focuses on ensuring there is a sound basis for the project before significant resources are committed.
*   i. **Appoint Executive and Project Manager:** Establish clear accountability for the project's success and daily management [Ref 6, Ref 13].
*   ii. **Capture Previous Lessons:** Review lessons from similar projects to inform initial planning and risk identification [Ref 7].
*   iii. **Design and Appoint Project Management Team:** Define the Project Board (Executive, Senior User, Senior Supplier) and allocate key roles.
*   iv. **Prepare Project Brief:** Develop an outline business case, define the project's objectives, scope, and key deliverables (Project Product Description).
*   v. **Plan the Initiation Stage:** Develop a plan for the next phase, outlining activities to produce the Project Initiation Documentation (PID).

#### II. Initiation Stage (Initiating a Project & Directing a Project)
This phase establishes a solid foundation for the project by detailing its justification, scope, plans, and controls.
*   i. **Refine Business Case:** Detailed justification for the project, confirming value and continued viability [Ref 7].
*   ii. **Define Project Scope and Product Scope:** Decompose the project's scope into manageable components, including a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) and Work Packages for software features [Ref 3, Ref 4].
*   iii. **Develop Project Plan:** Create a comprehensive plan encompassing estimating, scheduling, budget, team composition, communication plan, procurement, change management strategy, and measurement approaches [Ref 4].
*   iv. **Establish Project Controls:** Define strategies for managing risks (optimizing risk responses) [Ref 7, Ref 10], quality assurance (building quality into deliverables) [Ref 7], issues, and changes.
*   v. **Create Project Initiation Documentation (PID):** Compile all foundational documents into a single, comprehensive document.
*   vi. **Secure Project Board Approval:** Obtain formal authorization from the Project Board for the PID, allowing the project to proceed to the delivery stages.

#### III. Development Stages (Controlling a Stage, Managing Product Delivery & Managing a Stage Boundary)
This core phase involves the iterative development and delivery of the software product. Given the small scale, this might consist of 1-2 primary stages, potentially employing agile or incremental delivery within each stage [Ref 1].
*   i. **Authorize a Stage:** The Project Board formally approves the Stage Plan for the current development stage.
*   ii. **Develop Products:** Project team performs the defined work packages, including design, coding, testing, and documentation of software features [Ref 2].
*   iii. **Review Product Delivery and Quality:** Conduct regular reviews and quality checks to ensure deliverables meet specifications and quality standards [Ref 7].
*   iv. **Manage Stage Issues and Risks:** Monitor and respond to emerging issues and threats, optimizing risk responses as needed [Ref 7, Ref 10].
*   v. **Report Stage Progress:** Provide regular updates to the Project Board on progress, issues, and forecasts.
*   vi. **Prepare for Next Stage (if applicable):** At the stage boundary, review performance, update the Project Plan, Business Case, and Risk Register, and plan the next stage.
*   vii. **Seek Project Board Approval:** Obtain approval to continue to the next stage or for premature project closure.

#### IV. Project Closure (Closing a Project & Directing a Project)
This final phase ensures that the project is formally closed, products are handed over, and lessons are learned.
*   i. **Hand Over Final Products:** Ensure the completed software and associated documentation are formally transferred to the operational environment.
*   ii. **Evaluate Project Performance:** Assess the project against its original objectives and benefits realization expectations [Ref 2].
*   iii. **Recommend Project Closure:** Project Manager prepares the End Project Report and recommends formal project closure to the Project Board.
*   iv. **Disband Project Team:** Release project resources and personnel.
*   v. **Archive Project Documentation:** Securely store all project records and documentation.
*   vi. **Prepare Lessons Learned Report:** Document insights gained during the project for future reference.

### Roles

The following key roles are essential for governing and managing this PRINCE2 project:

*   **Executive (Sponsor):** The ultimate decision-maker and accountable individual for the project. Responsible for business justification and ensuring the project delivers value [Ref 6, Ref 7]. Secures necessary resources [Ref 6].
*   **Senior User:** Represents the interests of those who will use the project's products and focuses on user requirements and benefits realization.
*   **Senior Supplier:** Represents the interests of those who provide the specialist resources and skills to create the project's products (e.g., software development team lead).
*   **Project Board:** Comprises the Executive, Senior User, and Senior Supplier. Provides overall direction, makes key decisions, and authorizes stages [Ref 6].
*   **Project Manager:** Responsible for the day-to-day management of the project, including planning, monitoring, controlling, and motivating the team to achieve objectives [Ref 6, Ref 13].
*   **Project Team Members:** Individuals performing the specialist work of developing and delivering the software products.
*   **Project Assurance:** Provides independent oversight and checks the project's performance and compliance (often performed by Project Board members in a small project).
*   **Change Authority (optional for small projects):** Manages formal change requests (often integrated into the Project Manager or Project Board role).

### Decision Gates

Decision gates are critical control points for the Project Board to assess viability and authorize progression, ensuring that the project remains aligned with its business justification and objectives.

*   **Gate 1: Project Mandate Review & Start-up Authorization**
    *   **Description:** The Executive, potentially with initial input from Senior User and Senior Supplier, reviews the initial project idea or mandate. The decision is to authorize the "Starting Up a Project" process, approving the allocation of minimal resources to formulate a Project Brief and plan the Initiation Stage. This confirms a justifiable need and the initial strategic alignment for the temporary endeavor [Ref 6, Ref 9].
    *   **Evidence:** Project initiation involves coordination and achieving defined objectives [Ref 6, Ref 13].

*   **Gate 2: Initiation Stage Authorization (Project Initiation Documentation Approval)**
    *   **Description:** The Project Board rigorously reviews the Project Initiation Documentation (PID). This includes a detailed Business Case, the Project Plan (encompassing scope, schedule, budget, and resources), and defined project controls (risk management, quality, communication) [Ref 4]. The decision is to authorize the project to proceed into its first development stage, committing significant resources. This gate ensures the project is well-planned and viable before moving to delivery [Ref 9].
    *   **Evidence:** Planning involves defining product and project scope, estimating, scheduling, and budgeting [Ref 4]. Project management integrates practices to direct, initiate, plan, and control [Ref 13].

*   **Gate 3: Stage Boundary Authorization** (for each subsequent development stage)
    *   **Description:** At the end of each development stage, the Project Board reviews the End Stage Report, the updated Project Plan, and the Business Case. They assess performance against the current Stage Plan, evaluate the overall project's continued viability, and review the plan for the next stage. The decision is to authorize progression to the next stage, request modifications, or, if conditions warrant, prematurely close the project [Ref 7]. This iterative control ensures continuous business justification and adaptation [Ref 9].
    *   **Evidence:** Effective engagement with stakeholders and optimizing risk responses are key [Ref 7]. Project management includes monitoring and controlling stages [Ref 13].

*   **Gate 4: Project Closure Authorization**
    *   **Description:** The Project Board reviews the End Project Report, confirming that all deliverables have been handed over to operations, objectives have been met, and any post-project benefit reviews are planned. They also review the Lessons Learned Report. The decision is to formally approve the closure of the project, releasing resources and concluding all project management activities [Ref 13].
    *   **Evidence:** Project closure is a defined activity in project management [Ref 13]. Focus on value and enabling change to achieve envisioned future state are critical principles [Ref 7].
