### Decision Gates

Key decision gates ensure formal reviews and approvals at critical junctures, enabling effective project governance [Ref 12].

1.  **Project Charter Approval:** At the end of **Phase I (Project Initiation & Definition)**.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go to proceed with detailed planning.
    *   **Authority:** Project Sponsor [Ref 6].
    *   **Evidence:** Approved Project Charter.
    *   **Rationale:** Authorizes the project and the Project Manager to proceed [Ref 12].

2.  **Project Management Plan (PMP) Approval:** At the end of **Phase II (Project Planning)**.
    *   **Decision:** Go/No-Go to proceed with project execution.
    *   **Authority:** Project Sponsor and Key Stakeholders.
    *   **Evidence:** Fully developed and reviewed Project Management Plan, including scope, schedule, budget, and risk plans [Ref 1], [Ref 4].
    *   **Rationale:** Ensures all planning elements are comprehensive and agreed upon before committing resources to execution [Ref 10].

3.  **Research Milestone Review:** Mid-way during **Phase III (Project Execution & Research Work)**.

    *   **Authority:** Project Sponsor, Project Manager, Research Team, potentially key subject matter experts.
    *   **Evidence:** Progress reports, preliminary research findings, updated risk register [Ref 4], [Ref 10].
    *   **Rationale:** Important for research projects to assess progress, confirm viability, and adapt to emerging findings, focusing on value [Ref 15].

4.  **Final Deliverable Acceptance:** At the end of **Phase V (Project Closure)**.
