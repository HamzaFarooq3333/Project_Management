#### Phases and Activities

The project will proceed through distinct phases, each with specific objectives and deliverables, ensuring systematic progress and control over the large-scale research endeavor.

#### I. Project Initiation
This phase establishes the foundational elements of the project, defining its purpose and initial parameters.
*   i. **Define Project Objectives and Vision:** Clearly articulate the research goals and desired outcomes. As a "temporary endeavour to achieve one or more defined objectives" [Ref 9], this sets the direction.
*   ii. **Identify and Analyze Stakeholders:** Proactively identify persons, groups, or organizations that have interests in, or can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by, any aspect of the project [Ref 5, Ref 9]. Develop a stakeholder engagement plan [Ref 1, Ref 11].
*   iii. **Establish Project Governance and Authority:** Define the principles, policies, and procedures by which the project is authorized and directed to accomplish agreed objectives [Ref 8, Ref 7]. This includes defining the project sponsor [Ref 9].
*   iv. **Develop Project Charter:** Document the project's purpose, high-level objectives, scope, and initial resource requirements.
*   v. **Form Initial Project Team:** Identify core roles and begin team formation, fostering a collaborative environment [Ref 1, Ref 11].

#### II. Project Planning
This is a critical phase for a large project, detailing how the research will be conducted and managed to achieve its objectives.
*   i. **Define Product Scope and Project Scope:** Clearly delineate what the research will deliver (product scope) and the work required to deliver it (project scope) [Ref 2]. This includes defining unique and verifiable deliverables [Ref 7].
*   ii. **Develop Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Decompose the defined scope into progressively lower levels of work elements [Ref 5]. This forms the basis for estimating and scheduling [Ref 2].
*   iii. **Estimate Activities, Resources, and Costs:** Develop detailed estimates for project activities, necessary resources (personnel, equipment, materials), and overall budget [Ref 2].
*   iv. **Develop Project Schedule:** Create a comprehensive timeline for all project activities, milestones, and deliverables [Ref 2].
*   v. **Plan Team Composition and Acquisition:** Detail the required team members, their roles, responsibilities, and the strategy for their acquisition [Ref 2].
*   vi. **Develop Comprehensive Management Plans:**
    *   **Communication Plan:** Outline how information will be shared among stakeholders [Ref 2].
    *   **Procurement Plan:** Define the strategy for acquiring necessary goods and services [Ref 2].
    *   **Risk Management Plan:** Identify potential threats (risks with negative impact) [Ref 5] and opportunities, and plan responses to optimize outcomes [Ref 11, Ref 2].
    *   **Quality Management Plan:** Detail how quality will be built into processes and deliverables [Ref 11] and measured [Ref 2].
    *   **Change Management Plan:** Establish the process for managing changes to scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 2].
*   vii. **Baseline Establishment:** Obtain formal approval for the project management plan, establishing the performance measurement baselines.

#### III. Project Execution (Research & Development)
This phase involves performing the actual research work and managing the project team and resources.
*   i. **Direct and Manage Project Work:** Execute planned activities to produce deliverables [Ref 1]. Coordinate resources and integrate activities.
*   ii. **Manage Team Performance:** Guide and support the project team, ensuring a collaborative environment and effective resource utilization [Ref 1, Ref 11].
*   iii. **Conduct Procurements:** Administer contracts with external vendors and suppliers as per the procurement plan [Ref 2].
*   iv. **Implement Stakeholder Engagement:** Continuously engage with stakeholders, managing their expectations and ensuring their involvement [Ref 1, Ref 9, Ref 11].
*   v. **Perform Quality Assurance:** Implement planned quality activities and ensure adherence to quality standards [Ref 11].
*   vi. **Develop and Manage Research Deliverables:** Produce and refine research outputs (e.g., reports, prototypes, methodologies, data sets) ensuring they are unique and verifiable [Ref 7].

#### IV. Project Monitoring & Controlling
This phase runs concurrently with execution, ensuring the project stays on track and deviations are managed.
*   i. **Monitor Project Performance Domains:** Continuously track progress against the baselines across all performance domains, including scope, schedule, budget, and resources [Ref 1, Ref 2].
*   ii. **Control Scope:** Monitor the status of the project and product scope, and manage changes to the scope baseline [Ref 2]. Utilize the WBS for control [Ref 5].
*   iii. **Control Schedule and Costs:** Track actual progress against the schedule and actual expenditures against the budget [Ref 2].
*   iv. **Manage Risks and Issues:** Monitor identified risks, implement risk responses, and manage new risks and issues as they arise [Ref 5, Ref 7, Ref 11].
*   v. **Perform Integrated Change Control:** Review all change requests, approve changes, and manage changes to deliverables, organizational process assets, project documents, and the project management plan [Ref 2].
*   vi. **Monitor Stakeholder Engagement:** Assess the effectiveness of stakeholder engagement strategies [Ref 1].
*   vii. **Perform Quality Control:** Monitor and record results of quality activities to assess performance and recommend necessary changes [Ref 11].

#### V. Project Closure
This final phase formally concludes the project or a project phase.
*   i. **Finalize All Activities:** Complete all outstanding activities across all process groups to formally close the project [Ref 6].
*   ii. **Obtain Final Acceptance of Deliverables:** Secure formal acceptance from the sponsor and relevant stakeholders for all final research deliverables [Ref 7].
*   iii. **Close Procurements:** Complete and close out all project contracts and agreements [Ref 2].
*   iv. **Release Project Team:** Formally release team members and other resources.
*   v. **Conduct Lessons Learned:** Document project successes, failures, and lessons learned to contribute to organizational knowledge [Ref 6].
*   vi. **Archive Project Documents:** Compile and archive all relevant project documentation.
