### Process Summary

This process recommendation outlines a project management framework for a small-scale education marketing project (less than 6 months) guided by ISO standards. The framework emphasizes clear stakeholder engagement, thorough planning, and adaptability throughout the project lifecycle, in alignment with both the PMBOK Guide and ISO 21502 [Ref 1, Ref 2]. The process is tailored for the education industry, focusing on delivering value within a defined timeframe and budget. The key principles that guide this process are stakeholder engagement [Ref 7], adaptability [Ref 7], and a focus on value [Ref 7]. This framework will also identify potential challenges such as ambiguity regarding roles or expectations [Ref 5].

### Phases and Activities

This project will be organized into the following phases.

#### I. Initiation

i. **Define Project Objectives:** Clearly define the project's objectives, success criteria, and alignment with the educational institution's strategic goals [Ref 6].
ii. **Identify Stakeholders:** Identify all stakeholders involved in the project, including students, faculty, administrators, and external partners [Ref 6].
iii. **Appoint Project Manager:** Assign a qualified Project Manager with experience in marketing projects and knowledge of the education sector.
iv. **Develop Project Charter:** Create a project charter that outlines the project's scope, objectives, stakeholders, and initial budget [Ref 3].
v. **Obtain Project Approval:** Secure formal approval of the project charter from key stakeholders.

#### II. Planning

i. **Define Project Scope:** Develop a detailed project scope statement, outlining deliverables, acceptance criteria, and exclusions [Ref 3].
ii. **Create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS):** Decompose the project scope into smaller, manageable work packages [Ref 4].
iii. **Develop Project Schedule:** Estimate activity durations, sequence activities, and create a project schedule using appropriate scheduling tools.
iv. **Develop Budget:** Estimate the costs associated with each work package and create a project budget [Ref 3].
v. **Develop Communication Plan:** Define communication channels, frequency, and responsibilities for all stakeholders [Ref 3].
vi. **Develop Risk Management Plan:** Identify potential risks (both threats and opportunities), assess their likelihood and impact, and develop mitigation strategies [Ref 15].
vii. **Develop Resource Management Plan:** Identify resource requirements and allocate resources to project activities.
viii. **Define Quality Standards:** Establish quality standards and metrics to ensure deliverables meet the required standards.

#### III. Execution

i. **Execute Project Activities:** Carry out the tasks defined in the project schedule, following established processes and procedures.
ii. **Manage Project Team:** Provide leadership and guidance to the project team, ensuring effective collaboration and communication.
iii. **Manage Stakeholder Engagement:** Maintain regular communication with stakeholders, providing updates on project progress and addressing any concerns [Ref 7].
iv. **Manage Risks:** Monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as needed. Identify and address any new risks that arise [Ref 15].
v. **Manage Changes:** Implement a change management process to handle any changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].
vi. **Ensure Quality:** Monitor and control the quality of deliverables to ensure they meet the defined standards.

#### IV. Monitoring and Controlling

i. **Track Project Progress:** Monitor project progress against the project schedule and budget.
ii. **Performance Reporting:** Generate regular performance reports to communicate project status to stakeholders.
iii. **Variance Analysis:** Analyze any deviations from the project plan and take corrective actions as needed.
iv. **Risk Monitoring:** Continuously monitor identified risks and implement mitigation strategies as needed [Ref 15].
v. **Change Control:** Manage and control any changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget [Ref 3].

#### V. Closure

i. **Obtain Final Acceptance:** Obtain formal acceptance of all project deliverables from the stakeholders.
ii. **Conduct Project Post-mortem:** Conduct a post-mortem analysis to identify lessons learned and areas for improvement.
iii. **Document Project Outcomes:** Document all project outcomes, including deliverables, reports, and lessons learned.
iv. **Release Resources:** Release project resources and close out contracts.
v. **Celebrate Success:** Recognize and celebrate the project team's accomplishments.

-----

### Roles

i. **Project Sponsor:** Provides overall direction and support for the project, secures resources, and makes executive decisions [Ref 6].
ii. **Project Manager:** Responsible for planning, executing, monitoring, and controlling the project.
iii. **Marketing Team Members:** Responsible for executing marketing activities, creating content, and managing campaigns.
iv. **Stakeholders:** Individuals or groups with an interest in the project's outcome [Ref 6]. This includes representatives from the educational institution such as faculty, administrators and students.

### Decision Gates

i. **Project Charter Approval:** After the Initiation phase, a decision gate is required to approve the project charter. Go/No-Go decision based on alignment with strategic goals and available resources.
ii. **Project Plan Approval:** At the end of the Planning phase, a decision gate is required to approve the project plan. Go/No-Go decision based on the feasibility of the schedule, budget, and risk management plan [Ref 3].
iii. **Final Acceptance:** Upon completion of the Execution phase, a decision gate is required to obtain final acceptance of the project deliverables. Go/No-Go decision based on whether the deliverables meet the defined acceptance criteria.
