 Phases And Activities

I. Initiation — Confirm project need, feasibility, and high-level scope; obtain project charter approval.
II.   Activities: Business case development, stakeholder identification, preliminary scope definition, project charter creation, sponsor sign-off.
III. Planning — Develop detailed project plans including design, schedule, budget, resources, permits, and quality standards.
IV.   Activities: Detailed design review & finalization, permitting & regulatory approvals, work breakdown structure (WBS) creation, detailed scheduling & sequencing, budget allocation, resource planning (labor, materials, equipment), procurement strategy, risk identification & mitigation planning, quality management plan (inspections, standards), communication plan.
V. Execution — Mobilize resources, procure materials, and carry out the physical construction work according to plan.
VI.   Activities: Site mobilization & preparation, foundation work, structural framing, utility rough-ins (electrical, plumbing, HVAC), exterior finishes, interior finishes, landscaping, material procurement & delivery, managing subcontractors.
VII. Monitoring & Control — Track project progress, manage changes, control costs, and ensure quality and safety throughout execution.
VIII.   Activities: Daily/weekly progress tracking, cost control & expenditure monitoring, quality inspections & testing, schedule adherence monitoring, risk response implementation, change request management, safety compliance & incident reporting, stakeholder communication.
IX. Closure — Finalize all project activities, obtain necessary approvals, complete handover, and formally close the project.
X.   Activities: Final inspections & punch list completion, obtaining occupancy permits/certifications, client handover & sign-off, final financial closeout, contract closure, archiving project documentation, lessons learned session.

 Roles

XI.   Project Manager: Overall accountability for project success, planning, execution oversight, stakeholder management.
XII.   Site Manager/Superintendent: Day-to-day on-site management, crew supervision, quality control, safety enforcement.
XIII.   Architect/Designer: Responsible for design integrity, specifications, and design-related issues.
XIV.   Key Trade Leads (e.g., Foreman): Oversee specific construction trades (e.g., carpentry, electrical, plumbing).
XV.   Client/Owner Representative: Primary contact for project requirements, decisions, and approvals.
XVI.   Sponsor: Provides high-level support, approves major decisions, and champions the project.
XVII.   Quality Inspector (Optional/External): Conducts independent quality checks and ensures compliance with standards.
