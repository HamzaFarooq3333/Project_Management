 Recommended Improved Process for an Education Marketing Project

A medium-scale education marketing project focused on [achieving specific marketing goals, e.g., increasing enrollment, promoting a new program, building brand awareness], aligned with PMBOK, expected to complete in 6-18 months.

Revised Phases and Activities

I. Initiation
II.   Confirm business needs & marketing objectives (e.g., lead generation targets, brand uplift).
III.   Define high-level target audience(s).
IV.   Identify key stakeholders and their expectations.
V.   Conduct high-level feasibility assessment (resources, budget, timeline).
VI.   Obtain Project Charter approval.

VII. Planning
VIII.   Develop detailed Marketing Strategy & Campaign Plan (channels, messaging, content themes).
IX.   Define Scope (what's in/out of campaigns, content types).
X.   Create detailed Content Strategy & Calendar.
XI.   Establish Budget and Resource plan (human, tools).
XII.   Develop comprehensive Schedule/Timeline with key milestones.
XIII.   Conduct detailed Risk Management (campaign failure, compliance breaches, budget overruns).
XIV.   Define Communication Plan (internal/external reporting).
XV.   Define Quality Assurance Plan for marketing assets and data.
XVI.   Review Compliance Scope (e.g., FERPA, GDPR, anti-spam laws, brand guidelines).
XVII.   Establish Performance Metrics (KPIs) and Measurement Plan.

XVIII. Execution
XIX.   Content Creation: Develop all marketing assets (copy, visuals, videos, landing pages, ad creatives).
XX.   Campaign Development: Configure marketing automation, CRM, ad platforms.
XXI.   Technical Implementation: (If needed) Website updates, tracking pixel implementation, data integration.
XXII.   Campaign Launch: Deploy campaigns across chosen channels.
XXIII.   Lead Management: Implement lead capture and nurturing processes.
XXIV.   A/B Testing & Optimization: Run tests to improve campaign performance.
XXV.   Manage Stakeholder Engagement & Communications.

XXVI. Monitoring & Control
XXVII.   Track Campaign Performance: Monitor KPIs against targets using analytics tools.
XXVIII.   Budget & Schedule Control: Track actual spend and progress against plan.
XXIX.   Risk Monitoring & Response: Identify new risks, implement mitigation strategies.
XXX.   Quality Assurance: Review marketing assets for brand consistency, accuracy, and compliance.
XXXI.   Performance Analysis & Optimization: Analyze data, make real-time adjustments to campaigns.
XXXII.   Report Progress: Regular updates to stakeholders on performance and issues.

XXXIII. Closure
XXXIV.   Campaign Performance Report: Final comprehensive analysis of results against objectives.
XXXV.   Financial Closure: Reconcile all project expenditures.
XXXVI.   Lessons Learned: Conduct a post-mortem to identify successes, failures, and improvements.
XXXVII.   Asset Archiving: Store all campaign assets and documentation.
XXXVIII.   Knowledge Transfer: Handover ongoing campaign elements, data, and insights to operational teams.
XXXIX.   Obtain Final Project Acceptance.

Revised Roles
XL.   Project Manager: Overall project planning, execution, monitoring, and closure.
XLI.   Marketing Lead: Defines marketing strategy, oversees campaign design and content.
XLII.   Content Specialist/Copywriter: Develops messaging, content, and creative assets.
XLIII.   Digital Marketing Specialist: Manages campaign deployment, SEO/SEM, social media, ad platforms.
XLIV.   Data Analyst: Sets up tracking, analyzes campaign performance, provides insights.
XLV.   Domain Stakeholder (Education): Provides subject matter expertise, ensures educational alignment and compliance.
XLVI.   Sponsor: Provides funding, high-level guidance, removes roadblocks.
XLVII.   (Optional: Technical Support Specialist - for platform integration, if needed)

Revised Decision Gates
