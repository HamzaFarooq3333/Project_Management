 Phases And Activities

III.   Conduct feasibility study and high-level risk assessment.
IV.   Identify and analyze key stakeholders.
V.   Define preliminary scope, objectives, and success criteria.
VI.   Develop and obtain approval for the Project Charter.

VII. Planning (Detailed Design & Strategy)
VIII.   Scope Management: Develop detailed requirements (functional/non-functional), create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS).
IX.   Schedule Management: Develop comprehensive project schedule, identify milestones, dependencies.
X.   Cost Management: Estimate costs, establish budget baseline.
XI.   Quality Management: Define quality standards, metrics, and create a Quality Management Plan (including test strategy).
XII.   Resource Management: Plan for human, equipment, and material resources.
XIII.   Communications Management: Develop a Communication Plan for stakeholders.
XIV.   Risk Management: Identify, analyze, plan responses for risks, create a Risk Register.
XV.   Procurement Management: Plan for external acquisitions (if any), define vendor selection criteria.
XVI.   Stakeholder Management: Develop a plan for effective stakeholder engagement.
XVII.   Integration Management: Develop the Project Management Plan, integrating all subsidiary plans.
XVIII.   Architecture & Design: Define system architecture, technical specifications, and detailed design.

XIX. Execution (Build, Test & Deploy)
XX.   Acquire and manage project team and resources.
XXI.   Construct/Configure infrastructure (e.g., servers, network) and software modules.
XXII.   Integrate system components and data.
XXIII.   Perform Unit Testing, System Testing, and Integration Testing.
XXIV.   Conduct User Acceptance Testing (UAT) with domain stakeholders.
XXV.   Develop comprehensive user and system documentation.
XXVI.   Deliver user training programs.
XXVII.   Manage approved changes, risks, and issues as they arise.

XXVIII. Monitoring & Controlling (Oversight & Adaptation)
XXIX.   Track project progress against baselines (scope, schedule, cost, quality).
XXX.   Measure performance and report status to stakeholders.
XXXI.   Validate scope, control changes, and manage scope creep.
XXXII.   Monitor and control risks and issues, implement risk responses.
XXXIII.   Ensure quality standards are met through ongoing assurance and control activities.
XXXIV.   Manage communications and stakeholder engagement.
XXXV.   Conduct regular team meetings and performance reviews.
XXXVI.   Take corrective and preventive actions as needed.

XXXVII. Closure (Formal Handover & Review)
XXXVIII.   Obtain formal acceptance of project deliverables from the Sponsor and Domain Stakeholders.
XXXIX.   Perform administrative closure (final accounting, contract closure).
XL.   Conduct lessons learned analysis and document best practices.
XLI.   Archive project documentation and assets.
XLII.   Release project team resources.
XLIII.   Transition deliverables to operations/support teams.
XLIV.   Measure project success against original objectives.

 Roles

XLV.   Project Manager: Overall accountability for planning, executing, and closing the project. Integrates all project activities.
XLVI.   Technical Lead: Accountable for technical solution design, development, integration, and adherence to technical standards.
XLVII.   QA Engineer: Responsible for developing test plans, executing quality assurance activities, and reporting on quality metrics.
XLVIII.   Domain Stakeholder (Information Technology / Business Owner): Represents the business and IT operational needs, defines requirements, participates in UAT, and provides final acceptance.
XLIX.   Sponsor: Provides strategic guidance, secures resources, resolves high-level roadblocks, and holds ultimate accountability for project success.
L.   Business Analyst (Optional but Recommended): Elicits, analyzes, and documents detailed business and functional requirements.
LI.   Solution Architect (Optional but Recommended): Designs the overall system architecture and ensures technical feasibility and scalability.

 Decision Gates

LVI.   Gate 1: Project Charter Approval
LVII.   Purpose: Confirm business need, strategic alignment, high-level scope, and secure initial funding/resources.
LVIII.   Exit Criteria: Approved Project Charter.
LIX.   Gate 2: Requirements Baseline & Architecture Approval
LX.   Purpose: Ensure detailed requirements are stable and the technical architecture is approved.
LXI.   Exit Criteria: Approved Project Management Plan (including detailed scope, schedule, budget), signed-off architecture design documents.
LXII.   Gate 3: System Integration Testing (SIT) Completion & UAT Readiness
LXIII.   Purpose: Validate that the system components integrate successfully and are ready for user acceptance testing.
LXIV.   Exit Criteria: SIT completion report, resolved critical defects, approved UAT plan.
LXV.   Gate 4: Final Validation & Go-Live Approval
LXVI.   Purpose: Confirm solution meets business needs, all testing is complete, users are trained, and system is ready for production deployment.
LXVII.   Exit Criteria: Successful UAT, Go-Live readiness checklist complete, final production cutover plan, formal sign-off.
