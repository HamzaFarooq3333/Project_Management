I.   Critique: This is a major weakness due to its incompleteness (only 3 activities) and some potentially questionable assignments for a PMBOK-aligned project.
II.   Planning: PM should generally be Accountable (A) for the overall project plan, not just responsible. TL is Responsible (R) for technical planning.
III.   Execution: PM is Accountable (A) for project success, which includes execution, even if the team performs the work. TL is Responsible (R) for technical execution.
IV.   Monitoring: PM is typically Responsible (R) for tracking overall project metrics and Accountable (A) for decisions based on monitoring. QA Engineer is Responsible (R) for conducting QA activities.
V.   Recommendations:
VI.   Expand Significantly: Include key activities from all five phases. A RACI matrix should cover all critical decisions and deliverables.
VII.   Re-evaluate Assignments:
VIII.   Project Manager: Usually Accountable (A) for the overall project success and key phase deliverables/decisions (e.g., Project Plan, Go-Live). Responsible (R) for managing the project team, communications, risks, etc.
IX.   Technical Lead: Responsible (R) for all technical delivery, design, and implementation. Accountable (A) for the technical solution's integrity.
X.   QA Engineer: Responsible (R) for all testing activities (test planning, execution, defect management). Consulted (C) on requirements and design.
XI.   Domain Stakeholder: Consulted (C) on requirements, design, testing. Informed (I) on progress. Can be Responsible (R) or Accountable (A) for specific business acceptance criteria or UAT sign-off.
XII.   Sponsor: Predominantly Accountable (A) for the project's success and benefits realization, and for removing organizational roadblocks. Consulted (C) on major changes or risks. Informed (I) on overall project status.
