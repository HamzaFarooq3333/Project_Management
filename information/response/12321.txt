III. Roles

I.   Critique: Good core roles, but could benefit from one more.
II.   Recommendations:

V.   Consider: Trainers - If user training is extensive and specialized.

IV. RACI Matrix

The current RACI matrix has several issues regarding best practices, particularly the "A" (Accountable) and "R" (Responsible) assignments. There should generally be only one "A" per activity. "R" indicates who does the work, "A" indicates who owns the outcome. Often, the Project Manager is "A" for overall project activities, delegating "R" to leads.

I.   Critique:
II.   Planning: PM as 'A' and TL as 'R' for all planning is too simplistic. PM is 'A' for the overall plan, 'R' for integration and approval. TL is 'R' for technical plan. DS should be 'C' for requirements. Sponsor should be 'A' for final plan approval.
III.   Execution: TL as 'A' and PM as 'R' reverses the typical relationship for overall execution. PM is 'A' for overall project execution, TL is 'R' for technical execution. QA needs more than 'C'.
IV.   Monitoring: Similar to planning, PM as 'A' and TL as 'R' simplifies. QA needs to be 'R' for quality validation.

VI.   Key RACI Principles Applied:
VII.   Only one "A" (Accountable) per activity.
VIII.   "R" (Responsible) is who does the work; "A" is who owns the outcome and is ultimately answerable.
IX.   "C" (Consulted) means two-way communication; "I" (Informed) means one-way.

V.   Purpose: Ensures all detailed planning (scope, schedule, budget, risk, quality, etc.) is complete, reviewed, and formally approved by the Sponsor and key stakeholders. This establishes the project baselines against which performance will be measured.
VI.   Refine Existing Gates:
VII.   Gate 1: Project Charter Approval (as is).
VIII.   Gate 2: Configuration/Build Completion & System Integration Testing (SIT) Review (clarify what "review" entails, e.g., includes SIT sign-off).
IX.   Gate 3: Final Validation (UAT Sign-off) & Go-Live Approval (explicitly state UAT sign-off).

 Conclusion

The AI-generated recommendation provides a solid high-level framework that is consistent with the general phases of project management.
