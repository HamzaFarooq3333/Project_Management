I. Refine Project Definition & Context:
II.   Explicitly state that this is a physical building construction project involving physical structures, materials, and trades, distinct from software development.
III.   Emphasize the healthcare aspect: considerations for sterile environments, patient safety, specific regulations (e.g., HIPAA for data, but also specific facility requirements).

IV. Revise Phases and Activities (Construction-Specific):
V.   1. Initiation: Keep as is. (Confirm business needs, feasibility study, project charter development & approval).
VI.   2. Planning:
VII.   Define detailed architectural and engineering designs.
VIII.   Obtain permits and regulatory approvals.
IX.   Develop comprehensive procurement strategy (for materials, equipment, subcontractors).
X.   Create detailed construction schedule and budget.
XI.   Perform thorough risk management (safety, environmental, design flaws).
XII.   Develop a communication plan.
XIII.   3. Execution:

XV.   Foundation and structural build.
XVI.   MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing) system installation.

XIX.   Commissioning of systems (HVAC, fire suppression, security).
XX.   4. Monitoring & Controlling:

XXIII.   Manage changes to scope, schedule, budget.
XXIV.   Ensure safety compliance (crucial for construction, especially healthcare).
XXV.   Manage stakeholder communications.
XXVI.   Risk monitoring and mitigation.
XXVII.   5. Closure:
XXVIII.   Punch list creation and completion.
XXIX.   Final inspections and sign-offs by authorities (e.g., building inspectors, fire marshal).
XXX.   Obtain occupancy permits.
XXXI.   Facility commissioning and handover to operations.
XXXII.   Deliver as-built documentation, O&M manuals, warranties.
XXXIII.   Conduct post-project review and lessons learned.

XXXV.   Project Manager: (Keep) Overall project leadership, integration, stakeholder management.
XXXVI.   Sponsor: (Keep) Provide resources, strategic direction, ultimate authority.
XXXVII.   Domain Stakeholder (Healthcare): (Keep) Represents end-users, defines operational requirements.
XXXVIII.   Add: Architect/Lead Engineer: Responsible for design, specifications, ensuring compliance.
XXXIX.   Add: Construction Manager/General Contractor: Manages day-to-day site operations, subcontractors, safety.
XL.   Add: Quality Control Inspector: Conducts material and workmanship inspections. (Could be internal or third-party).
XLI.   Add: Health & Safety Officer: Ensures strict adherence to safety protocols and regulations.
XLII.   Add: Procurement Specialist: Manages acquisition of materials and subcontractor services.
