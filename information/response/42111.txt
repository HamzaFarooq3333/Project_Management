 Phases And Activities

I. Initiation — Confirm Project Viability & Obtain Authorization
II.   Confirm Business Needs & Objectives: Refine initial problem statement, validate desired business outcomes, and identify success criteria.
III.   Identify Key Stakeholders: Document primary stakeholders (users, sponsors, technical teams, domain experts) and their initial expectations/interests.
IV.   Define High-Level Scope & Deliverables: Outline the primary functions and components to be built/configured, and key outputs.
V.   Identify High-Level Risks & Assumptions: Brainstorm initial potential blockers and underlying assumptions.
VI.   Develop Project Charter: Document the project's purpose, objectives, high-level requirements, major deliverables, overall budget/timeline estimates, and assign the Project Manager.
VII.   Obtain Project Charter Approval (Decision Gate 1): Secure formal authorization to proceed from the Sponsor.

VIII. Planning — Detail the How, What, When, and Who
IX.   Define Detailed Scope & Requirements:
X.   Gather & Elaborate Requirements: Work with Domain Stakeholders to capture functional and non-functional requirements (e.g., performance, security, usability).
XI.   Create Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): Decompose the project into manageable tasks and work packages.
XII.   Define Architecture & Technical Design:
XIII.   System Architecture & Integration Design: Technical Lead defines the overall system structure, module interactions, and integration points.
XIV.   Configuration & Build Specifications: Detail how software modules will be configured, parameters, and necessary customisations.
XV.   Develop Project Schedule:
XVI.   Estimate Activities: Estimate effort and duration for each WBS task.
XVII.   Sequence Activities: Define task dependencies.
XVIII.   Develop Schedule Baseline: Create a realistic timeline, including milestones.
XIX.   Plan Resources: Identify required human resources (roles, skills), tools, and infrastructure.
XX.   Plan Quality Assurance (QA) & Testing: Define testing strategy, test cases (integration, UAT), and quality metrics.
XXI.   Identify & Plan for Risks: Conduct a more detailed risk analysis, identify mitigation strategies, and plan contingencies.
XXII.   Develop Communication Plan: Define how, when, and to whom project information will be disseminated.
XXIII.   Define Compliance Scope & Strategy: Identify relevant regulatory or internal compliance requirements and how they will be met.
XXIV.   Develop Project Management Plan: Consolidate all planning elements into a concise project plan document.

XXV. Execution — Build, Integrate, and Manage
XXVI.   Develop/Configure Software Modules: Technical Lead and team perform the actual configuration, customisation, and coding as per design.
XXVII.   Integrate Components: Connect different modules and ensure they function together as designed.
XXVIII.   Manage Project Team: Guide and support the project team, resolve issues, and foster collaboration.
XXIX.   Conduct Quality Assurance Activities:
XXX.   Technical Reviews & Inspections: Peer reviews of configurations, code, and designs.
XXXI.   Execute Unit & Integration Testing: Technical Lead and QA Engineer perform initial testing to verify functionality and interfaces.
XXXII.   Manage Stakeholder Engagement: Keep stakeholders informed and address their concerns.
XXXIII.   Manage Procurements (if applicable): Acquire any necessary external software, hardware, or services.
XXXIV.   Obtain Configuration/Build Completion Review (Decision Gate 2): Technical Lead and Project Manager confirm that all required configuration/build tasks are complete and ready for formal testing.

XXXV. Monitoring & Control — Track, Review, and Adjust
XXXVI.   Monitor & Control Project Work: Track overall project progress against the plan (scope, schedule, cost, quality).
XXXVII.   Report Performance: Regularly communicate project status, progress, and forecasts to stakeholders.
XXXVIII.   Manage Scope Changes: Implement a change control process to evaluate and approve/reject any proposed changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget.
XXXIX.   Conduct Quality Control & System Testing:
XL.   Execute System Testing: QA Engineer performs comprehensive testing of the integrated system.
XLI.   Execute User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Domain Stakeholders and end-users validate the solution against business requirements.
XLII.   Manage Risks: Continuously monitor identified risks and new risks, execute mitigation plans as needed.
XLIII.   Manage Issues: Log, track, and resolve project issues proactively.
XLIV.   Conduct Quality Audits: Periodically review project processes and deliverables for adherence to quality standards.

XLV. Closure — Finalize, Transfer, and Learn
XLVI.   Obtain Final Validation & Go-Live Approval (Decision Gate 3): Sponsor and Domain Stakeholders formally accept the project deliverables and authorize deployment.
XLVII.   Deploy/Go-Live: Implement the configured system into the production environment.
XLVIII.   Deliver Documentation: Provide comprehensive system documentation (architecture, configuration guides, operational manuals) and user manuals.
XLIX.   Conduct User Training: Train end-users on the new system functionality.
L.   Verify Operational Readiness: Ensure support teams are ready to operate and maintain the system.
LI.   Obtain Formal Final Acceptance: Secure formal sign-off from all relevant stakeholders.
LII.   Conduct Lessons Learned: Facilitate a session to identify successes, failures, and improvements for future projects.
LIII.   Release Project Resources: Formally release team members and other resources.
LIV.   Archive Project Documents: Store all project records in a designated repository.
LV.   Perform Administrative & Contractual Closure: Finalize administrative tasks and close out any contracts.

 Roles

LVI.   Project Manager (PM): Overall accountability for project success, planning, execution, monitoring, stakeholder communication, risk management, and team leadership.
LVII.   Technical Lead (TL): Accountable for technical design, architecture, configuration/development quality, technical problem-solving, and guiding the technical team.
LVIII.   QA Engineer (QA): Responsible for defining testing strategy, creating test cases, executing tests (system, integration, UAT support), reporting defects, and validating quality.
LIX.   Domain Stakeholder (Information Technology): Provides business requirements, validates solutions, participates in UAT, and ensures the solution meets business needs.
LX.   Sponsor: Provides project funding, high-level direction, removes organizational impediments, and grants key approvals.
LXI.   End-Users (Implicit under Domain Stakeholder/Testing): Provide feedback, participate in UAT, and receive training.

 Key Takeaways for Improvement:

I. Detail is King (even for small projects): While keeping it lean, PMBOK alignment requires specific processes from its knowledge areas (Scope, Schedule, Quality, Risk, etc.) to be explicitly mentioned.
II. Clarity in Activity Names: Use more descriptive and PM-standard activity names (e.g., "Develop Project Charter" instead of just "obtain approval").
III. Explicit Deliverables: Each phase, and many activities, implicitly produce deliverables. Making these explicit (e.g., "Approved Project Charter") helps clarify expectations.
IV. Continuous Monitoring & Control: Emphasize that Monitoring isn't just a phase but an ongoing activity throughout Execution, particularly for change, risk, and issue management.
V. Formal Closure: Highlight the importance of administrative and contractual closure, not just documentation and training.

By incorporating these enhancements, the recommendation becomes much more robust, actionable, and genuinely aligned with PMBOK best practices, even for a small, short-duration IT project.
