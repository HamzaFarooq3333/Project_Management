Domain Stakeholder (Finance/Marketing): Represents the business needs, provides domain expertise, approves marketing content and strategy, ensures alignment with business goals and financial regulations. IV. Decision Gates

The existing decision gates are good. Broadening their description makes them more applicable to the marketing context beyond just technical builds.

LXX.   Gate 1: Project Charter Approval
LXXI.   Criteria: Project objectives, high-level scope, key stakeholders, and initial risks are clearly defined and formally approved by the Sponsor. This marks the transition from concept to planning.
LXXII.   Gate 2: Project Plan & Readiness for Execution Approval
LXXIII.   Criteria: Detailed project plan (including scope, schedule, budget, resources, marketing strategy, compliance plan, risk management) is approved by key stakeholders and the Sponsor. All pre-execution checks, including initial compliance reviews, are complete, confirming the project is ready to commence execution. This replaces "Configuration/Build Completion Review" to be more encompassing.
LXXIV.   Gate 3: Final Validation, Go-Live & Closure Approval
LXXV.   Criteria: All marketing deliverables (content, campaigns, technical configurations) have undergone final testing, quality assurance, and a comprehensive legal/compliance review. Campaign launch readiness is confirmed, and the Sponsor provides approval to go live/deploy and begin the formal closure process.
