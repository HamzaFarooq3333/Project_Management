IV. RACI Matrix (Revisions Needed)

The current RACI has some inconsistencies regarding accountability (A) and responsibility (R).

| Activity | Project Manager | Technical Lead | QA Engineer | Domain Stakeholder | Sponsor |
| :-------- | :-------------- | :------------- | :---------- | :----------------- | :------- |
| Planning | A (R for leading plan creation) | R (for technical planning, architecture design, estimates) | C (for quality planning) | C (for business requirements, compliance) | I (approves plan baseline) |
| Execution | A (for overall execution, resource mgmt, progress) | R (for technical delivery, configuration, development) | R (for testing activities, defect management) | C (for UAT, business feedback) | I (informed of progress) |
| Monitoring | A (for overall monitoring & control) | R (for technical status reporting, issue resolution) | R (for quality metrics, test progress) | C (for business impact, compliance, UAT results) | I (informed of overall progress/risks) |
| Closure | A (for formal closure & documentation) | R (for technical documentation, handover) | C (for final quality review) | C (for UAT sign-off, training validation) | A (final project acceptance) |

Explanation of Changes:
I.   Planning: PM is Accountable for the overall plan, but Responsible for leading its creation. Technical Lead is Responsible for the technical parts. Sponsor typically Approves the final plan, making them Accountable for its acceptance.
II.   Execution: PM is Accountable for overall project execution. Technical Lead is Responsible for technical delivery. QA is Responsible for testing.
III.   Monitoring: PM is Accountable for overall monitoring and control. QA and Domain Stakeholder are Responsible for their specific monitoring aspects (e.g., defect tracking, UAT sign-off).
IV.   Closure: Sponsor is Accountable for final project acceptance, while PM is Accountable for managing the closure process.
